New Hampshire - Life Coach Legal Requirements

Key Finding: New Hampshire does NOT require licensing for life coaches.

Critical Boundary: Life coaches cannot diagnose, treat, or present themselves as mental health professionals.

Legislative Note: Attempted licensing legislation (HB 1058) failed in 2019-2020.

Historical Context: In 2019-2020, New Hampshire considered House Bill 1058, which would have required life coaches to obtain state licensure. The bill was ultimately defeated, and life coaching remains an unregulated profession in the state.

Legal Definition

New Hampshire law does not provide a specific definition for "life coach." Life coaching is considered an unregulated profession in the state, distinct from licensed mental health professions such as psychology, clinical mental health counseling, marriage and family therapy, and pastoral psychotherapy.

Licensing Requirements

No license is required to practice as a life coach in New Hampshire. Following the defeat of HB 1058, the state does not have a regulatory framework, certification process, or registration system for life coaches.

Life coaches may operate as independent practitioners or business owners without state oversight, provided they stay within the boundaries of coaching and do not engage in activities reserved for licensed mental health professionals.

Practice Regulations

While life coaching itself is unregulated, New Hampshire has strict regulations governing mental health professions that create clear boundaries for life coaches:

  • Psychology: Licensed under RSA Chapter 329-B by the Board of Psychologists
  • Clinical Mental Health Counseling: Licensed under RSA Chapter 330-A by the Board of Mental Health Practice
  • Marriage and Family Therapy: Licensed under RSA Chapter 330-A by the Board of Mental Health Practice
  • Pastoral Psychotherapy: Licensed under RSA Chapter 330-A by the Board of Mental Health Practice

Boundaries with Clinical Professionals

Life coaches in New Hampshire must understand and respect the boundaries between coaching and licensed mental health practice:

What Life Coaches CANNOT Do:

  • Diagnose mental health conditions or psychological disorders
  • Provide psychotherapy or clinical treatment
  • Use titles such as "therapist," "counselor," "psychologist," or "clinical mental health counselor"
  • Claim to treat, cure, or heal mental illness
  • Practice within the defined scope of licensed mental health professions
  • Present credentials in a way that implies clinical training or licensure
  • Hold themselves out as licensed mental health practitioners

What Life Coaches CAN Do:

  • Provide goal-setting and accountability services
  • Offer motivational support and encouragement
  • Help clients develop action plans for personal or professional growth
  • Facilitate self-discovery and exploration of values
  • Use the title "life coach" or "coach"
  • Work with clients who are mentally healthy and seeking personal development

Restrictions & Prohibitions

Unauthorized Practice of Psychology

Under RSA 329-B:22, it is unlawful to engage in the practice of psychology without a license. The statute defines the practice of psychology to include:

  • Psychological testing and assessment
  • Diagnosis and treatment of mental and emotional disorders
  • Psychotherapy and counseling services

Penalty: Violation is a misdemeanor, with potential criminal penalties and civil liability.

Unauthorized Practice of Mental Health Counseling

Under RSA 330-A:27, practicing clinical mental health counseling, marriage and family therapy, or pastoral psychotherapy without proper licensure is prohibited.

The Board of Mental Health Practice has authority to investigate complaints and take enforcement action against individuals practicing without appropriate licensure.

Relevant Statutes

RSA 329-B:22 - Unauthorized Practice of Psychology

Prohibits the practice of psychology without proper state licensure. Violations may result in criminal penalties and civil enforcement actions.

RSA 330-A:27 - Penalties for Unauthorized Practice

Makes it illegal to practice clinical mental health counseling, marriage and family therapy, or pastoral psychotherapy without licensure from the Board of Mental Health Practice.

RSA 329-B:2 - Definition of Practice of Psychology

Defines the practice of psychology to include assessment, diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of mental and emotional disorders or disabilities.

RSA 330-A:2 - Definitions for Mental Health Practice

Provides definitions for clinical mental health counseling, marriage and family therapy, and pastoral psychotherapy, establishing scope of practice for licensed professionals.

Best Practices

Clear Communication

  • Use clear language distinguishing coaching from therapy in all marketing materials
  • Include disclaimers stating you are not a licensed mental health professional
  • Explain the difference between coaching and therapy to prospective clients
  • Given the history of HB 1058, be especially clear about your role and limitations

Scope of Practice

  • Focus on future-oriented goals and action plans
  • Avoid language suggesting diagnosis or treatment of mental health conditions
  • Refer clients to licensed professionals when mental health concerns arise
  • Maintain a referral network of licensed therapists, psychologists, and psychiatrists

Professional Development

  • Obtain training from reputable coaching organizations (ICF, CCE, IAC)
  • Consider voluntary certification through recognized coaching bodies
  • Stay informed about New Hampshire laws governing mental health practice
  • Monitor legislative developments regarding potential coaching regulation
  • Engage in continuing education on ethics and boundaries

Documentation

  • Use client agreements that clearly define the coaching relationship
  • Document that coaching is not therapy or mental health treatment
  • Keep clear records of coaching sessions and client progress
  • Maintain appropriate confidentiality while understanding legal limits

Business Operations

  • Obtain general liability insurance
  • Consider professional liability insurance specific to coaching
  • Register your business with the New Hampshire Secretary of State if operating as an entity
  • Comply with local business licensing requirements

Legislative Awareness

  • Stay informed about potential future licensing legislation
  • Engage with professional coaching associations that monitor regulatory developments
  • Understand the concerns that led to HB 1058 and address them proactively in your practice
