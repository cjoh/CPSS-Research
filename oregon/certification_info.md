# Oregon Peer Support Specialist Certification Information

## Official Certification Name
**Peer Support Specialist (PSS)** - Adult Mental Health
**Peer Wellness Specialist (PWS)** - Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Certifying Body
**Oregon Health Authority (OHA)** - Traditional Health Worker (THW) Program
- Website: https://www.oregon.gov/oha/hsd/amh-pd/pages/training-certification.aspx
- THW Program: https://www.oregon.gov/oha/ei/pages/thw-pss.aspx
- Email: thw.program@odhsoha.oregon.gov
- Phone: 971-673-3353

## Certification Overview
A Peer Support Specialist is a person who has lived experience with substance abuse or behavioral health challenges and in Oregon, they are trained and certified by the Oregon Health Authority to provide peer recovery services. Oregon offers separate but related certifications for mental health (PSS) and addiction (PWS).

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements
**For Peer Wellness Specialist (Addiction):**
- Must be a self-identified individual currently or formerly receiving addictions treatment or in recovery from an addiction disorder
- Must meet abstinence requirements for recovering staff in alcohol or other drug treatment programs

**For Peer Support Specialist (Mental Health):**
- Personal lived experience with mental health challenges
- Active engagement in recovery/wellness

### Education Requirements
- No specific education requirement (high school diploma not explicitly required)

### Training Requirements

#### Standard Training Path
- Complete **40-hour training program** designed to meet OHA standards
- Training must be from an OHA-approved provider
- Covers peer support competencies, recovery principles, ethics, and professional conduct

#### Legacy Clause (Valid through June 30, 2025)
If you have worked or volunteered in the capacity of a Peer Support Specialist or similar role for at least **2,000 hours between January 1, 2008 - June 30, 2025**:
- Submit letter of recommendation from previous employer or organization
- Provide verifiable evidence that you met the 2,000-hour requirement
- Can apply for certification without completing 40-hour training

### Background Check
- Must pass criminal background check
- Background check requirements determined by OHA

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Training
Complete 40-hour OHA-approved training program (unless qualifying under legacy clause)

### Step 2: Prepare Application Materials
- Gather training certificate
- Obtain any required documentation
- Prepare background check information

### Step 3: Submit Application
Submit certification application to Oregon Health Authority THW Program

### Step 4: Background Check
Complete and pass criminal background check

### Step 5: Receive Certification
Receive THW certification from OHA

## Application Fees
- **Standard Fee:** $100
- **FEE WAIVED from July 1, 2021 - December 31, 2025**
- Current applicants can get certified at no cost through the end of 2025

## Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
THW certification is valid for **three years (36 months)** from certification date

### Continuing Education Requirements
- **20 hours** of continuing education training before renewal date

### Renewal Process
Submit renewal application with documentation of continuing education hours before expiration

### Renewal Fee
Contact OHA for current renewal fee information (standard fee may be waived during promotional period)

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
To become an OHA-approved training provider, organizations must meet Oregon Health Authority standards for Traditional Health Worker peer support training.

### Requirements
- Training program must be **40 hours minimum**
- Must cover all required peer support competencies
- Curriculum must align with OHA standards
- Trainers must be qualified

### Application Process
Contact Oregon Health Authority THW Program:
- **Email:** thw.program@odhsoha.oregon.gov
- **Phone:** 971-673-3353
- **Website:** https://www.oregon.gov/oha/ei/pages/thw-pss.aspx

### Training Standards
- Must prepare participants for OHA certification
- Include recovery principles and practices
- Cover ethics and professional boundaries
- Include peer support competencies
- Meet OHA curriculum guidelines
