Certification Overview

A Peer Support Specialist is a person who has lived experience with substance abuse or behavioral health challenges and in Oregon, they are trained and certified by the Oregon Health Authority to provide peer recovery services. Oregon offers separate but related certifications for mental health (PSS) and addiction (PWS).

Requirements

Personal Recovery Requirements

For Peer Wellness Specialist (Addiction):

  • Self-identified individual currently or formerly receiving addictions treatment or in recovery from an addiction disorder
  • Must meet abstinence requirements for recovering staff in alcohol or other drug treatment programs

For Peer Support Specialist (Mental Health):

  • Personal lived experience with mental health challenges
  • Active engagement in recovery/wellness

Education Requirements

  • No specific education requirement (high school diploma not explicitly required)

Training Requirements

Standard Training Path

  • 40-hour training program designed to meet OHA standards
  • Training must be from an OHA-approved provider
  • Covers peer support competencies, recovery principles, ethics, and professional conduct

Legacy Clause (Valid through June 30, 2025)

If you have worked or volunteered in the capacity of a Peer Support Specialist or similar role for at least 2,000 hours between January 1, 2008 - June 30, 2025:

  • Submit letter of recommendation from previous employer or organization
  • Provide verifiable evidence that you met the 2,000-hour requirement
  • Can apply for certification without completing 40-hour training

Background Check

  • Must pass criminal background check
  • Background check requirements determined by OHA

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete 40-hour OHA-approved training program (unless qualifying under legacy clause)
  2. Prepare Application Materials: Gather training certificate, obtain required documentation, prepare background check information
  3. Submit Application: Submit certification application to Oregon Health Authority THW Program
  4. Background Check: Complete and pass criminal background check
  5. Receive Certification: Receive THW certification from OHA

Fees and Costs

Renewal Requirements

  • Certification Period: Three years (36 months) from certification date
  • Continuing Education: 20 hours of continuing education training before renewal date
  • Renewal Process: Submit renewal application with documentation of continuing education hours
  • Renewal Fee: Contact OHA for current renewal fee information

How to Become a Training Provider

To become an OHA-approved training provider, organizations must meet Oregon Health Authority standards for Traditional Health Worker peer support training.

Requirements

  • Training program must be 40 hours minimum
  • Must cover all required peer support competencies
  • Curriculum must align with OHA standards
  • Trainers must be qualified
