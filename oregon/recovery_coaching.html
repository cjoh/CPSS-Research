Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Licensed Professions (Require Licensure)

Oregon extensively regulates licensed mental health professionals including:

  • Licensed Professional Counselors
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Licensed Clinical Social Workers

Governed by: ORS Chapter 18.225 and Oregon Administrative Rules

Recovery Coach Status

  • NO SPECIFIC REGULATION FOUND distinguishing recovery coaching from licensed therapy
  • Recovery coaching appears to operate in unregulated or lightly regulated space
  • No statutory scope of practice for recovery coaches identified

Key Risk

Without clear statutory boundaries, recovery coaches must be careful not to engage in activities that constitute practice of licensed professions (counseling, therapy, social work).

Scope of Practice

What Recovery Coaches Should DO

  • Provide peer support and recovery navigation
  • Share lived experience
  • Connect individuals to resources
  • Offer mentoring and advocacy
  • Refer to licensed professionals for clinical needs

What Recovery Coaches Must NOT Do

  • Provide psychotherapy or clinical counseling
  • Diagnose mental health or substance use conditions
  • Develop clinical treatment plans
  • Hold yourself out as providing clinical services

Important Considerations

Advantages of Unregulated Status

  • No state certification costs or requirements
  • No specific training mandates (though voluntary training available)
  • Flexibility in practice structure
  • No supervision requirements

Risks of Unregulated Status

  • No legal protection of title "recovery coach"
  • No clear scope of practice definition
  • Potential liability exposure without regulatory guidance
  • Must be extremely careful not to cross into licensed practice
  • No clear legal framework if disputes arise

Recommendations

Even though unregulated, recovery coaches should:

  • Complete voluntary training programs in trauma-informed care and ethics
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Develop clear service agreements defining non-clinical nature of services
  • Establish referral relationships with licensed professionals
  • Avoid any activities that could constitute counseling, therapy, diagnosis, or treatment
  • Consider national certification (CARC, NCPRSS, etc.) for professional credibility
