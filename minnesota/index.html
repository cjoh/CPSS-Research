Official Certification: Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

Important 2025 Employment Requirement

Effective January 1, 2025: A recovery peer must NOT be classified or treated as an independent contractor. All peers must be W-2 employees.

Certification Overview

A Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) in Minnesota is a person with personal lived experience related to substance use who has been trained to use their lived experience to support others in recovery. Minnesota Certification Board is identified as the largest certification organization for peers in Minnesota.

Certifying Body

Requirements

Personal Requirements

  • Lived Experience: Must have personal lived experience related to substance use (having a friend or family member with this experience does NOT meet requirement)
  • Recovery Duration: Minimum of one (1) year in recovery from substance use disorder
  • Age: Must be 18 years or older

Training Requirements

Duration: Minimum of 46 hours of training

Provider: Must complete training through an accredited trainer or organization approved by Minnesota Certification Board

Domains:

  • Ethics and boundaries
  • Advocacy
  • Mentoring and education
  • Recovery and wellness support

Examination Requirements

  • Type: 75-question state examination
  • Administrator: Minnesota Certification Board
  • Format: Computer-based examination

Supervision Requirements

Recovery peers must work under the supervision of:

  • Licensed alcohol and drug counselor, OR
  • Mental health professional who meets qualifications under Minnesota Statutes, 245I.04, subdivision 2

Application Process

1

Verify Eligibility

Ensure personal lived experience, 1 year recovery, and age 18+

2

Complete Training

Complete 46 hours MCB-approved training covering all domains

3

Pass Examination

Pass 75-question MCB state examination

4

Apply & Maintain

Apply to MCB ($250), receive credential, work under supervision

Visit MCB Training Providers

Fees and Certification Period

First-Time Certification: $250 (includes processing fee, exam, and two years of certification)

Certification Duration: Two (2) years from date of issuance

Continuing Education

Peer specialists must maintain ongoing professional development. Specific CE hours should be confirmed with MCB for biennial renewal requirements.

Related Minnesota Credentials

Minnesota offers multiple peer support credentials:

  • Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) - Substance use disorder (focus of this page)
  • Certified Peer Specialist - Mental health
  • Certified Family Peer Specialist - Family members supporting loved ones with behavioral health challenges
