# Minnesota Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)**

## Certifying Body
**Minnesota Certification Board (MCB)**

**Contact Information:**
- Organization: Minnesota Certification Board
- Website: https://www.mcboard.org
- Email: mcb@mcboard.org
- Peer Recovery Page: https://www.mcboard.org/peer-recovery/
- Approved Training Providers: https://www.mcboard.org/approved-peer-training/

## Personal Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Lived Experience:** Must have personal lived experience related to substance use (having own lived experience; having a friend or family member with this experience does NOT meet requirement)
- **Recovery Duration:** Minimum of one (1) year in recovery from substance use disorder
- **Age:** Must be 18 years or older
- **Personal Experience:** Definition requires personal lived experience with substance use, not vicarious experience

## Employment Status Requirements

### NEW for 2025
**Effective January 1, 2025:** A recovery peer must NOT be classified or treated as an independent contractor.

This is a significant change affecting how peers can be employed in Minnesota.

## Training Requirements

### Core Training
- **Duration:** Minimum of 46 hours of training
- **Provider:** Must complete training through an accredited trainer or organization approved by Minnesota Certification Board
- **Format:** Various formats available (in-person, virtual, hybrid) depending on provider
- **Domains:** Training must cover:
  - Ethics and boundaries
  - Advocacy
  - Mentoring and education
  - Recovery and wellness support

## Examination Requirements

### State Examination
- **Type:** 75-question state examination
- **Administrator:** Minnesota Certification Board
- **Passing Score:** Must pass the exam (specific passing score set by MCB)
- **Format:** Computer-based examination

## Supervision Requirements

### Working Requirement
Recovery peers must work under the supervision of:
- Licensed alcohol and drug counselor, OR
- Mental health professional who meets qualifications under Minnesota Statutes, 245I.04, subdivision 2

## Application and Certification Process

1. Verify personal eligibility (lived experience, 1 year recovery)
2. Complete 46 hours of MCB-approved training
3. Pass 75-question MCB state examination
4. Apply to Minnesota Certification Board for CPRS credential
5. Maintain current credential through renewals and CE
6. Work under qualified supervision

## Fees

**First-Time Certification:**
- **Cost:** $250
- **Includes:** Processing fee, exam, and two years of certification

## Certification Period

**Duration:** Two (2) years
- Certification is valid for two years from date of issuance
- Must renew before expiration

## Continuing Education Requirements

### Biennial CE Requirements
Details on specific CE hours should be confirmed with MCB, but peer specialists must maintain ongoing professional development.

## Credentials and Organizations

### Minnesota Certification Board (MCB)
- Identified as the **largest certification organization for peers in Minnesota**
- Offers the Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) credential
- Sets standards for training and certification

### Substance Use vs. Mental Health
**Important Clarification:**
- Recovery peers for **substance use disorder** must hold credential from Minnesota Certification Board, Upper Midwest Indian Council on Addictive Disorders, or National Association for Alcoholism and Drug Abuse Counselors
- Credentials must demonstrate skills and training in: ethics and boundaries, advocacy, mentoring and education, and recovery and wellness support

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Minnesota's Program
- **One Year Recovery:** Relatively shorter recovery requirement than some states
- **46-Hour Training:** Standard training duration aligned with many states
- **Two-Year Certification:** Longer validity period reduces renewal burden
- **Multiple Providers:** Various training options across the state
- **Academic Options:** Some colleges/universities offer credit-bearing programs
- **Financial Aid Eligible:** College programs may qualify for financial aid
- **Largest Certifying Board:** MCB is recognized as largest peer certification organization in Minnesota
- **2025 Employment Change:** No more independent contractor status

### Important 2025 Changes

**Effective January 1, 2025:**
- **Employment Status:** Recovery peers can no longer be classified or treated as independent contractors
- **Impact:** All peers must be W-2 employees
- **Planning:** Employers and peers must adjust employment arrangements
- **Compliance:** This is a legal requirement, not optional

### Best Practices for Applicants

**Before Starting:**
1. Ensure you have minimum 1 year recovery from substance use
2. Verify you have personal lived experience (not vicarious through family/friends)
3. Research MCB-approved training providers
4. Compare costs and formats (in-person vs. virtual)
5. Consider academic programs if seeking college credit
6. Check if financial aid available through college programs

**During Training:**
1. Complete full 46 hours with approved provider
2. Engage fully with all training domains
3. Network with other trainees for peer support
4. Prepare for 75-question state exam
5. Arrange employment with understanding of 2025 W-2 requirement

**Certification Process:**
1. Complete 46-hour training
2. Schedule and pass 75-question MCB exam
3. Submit application with $250 fee
4. Receive certification valid for 2 years
5. Ensure employment arrangement complies with 2025 requirements
6. Work under qualified supervision
7. Plan for CE and renewal in 2 years

**Employment Considerations (2025):**
1. Cannot work as independent contractor
2. Must be W-2 employee
3. Requires supervision by licensed professional
4. Verify employer understands new requirements

### Timeline Considerations
- **Training:** 46 hours (intensive format or spread over weeks)
- **Exam:** Schedule after training completion
- **Certification Processing:** After passing exam and application
- **Total Timeline:** Can complete training and certification in 1-3 months
- **Validity:** 2 years before first renewal

### Comparison to Other States
- Minnesota's 1-year recovery requirement is shorter than many states (2-3 years)
- 46-hour training is standard across many states
- Two-year certification period is longer than some annual renewal states
- $250 initial certification fee is mid-range
- 2025 independent contractor prohibition is somewhat unique
