# Minnesota Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** LEGALLY DEFINED in Minnesota Rules

**Definition (Minnesota Rules 9533.0020):**
> "Recovery coach" means an individual who has a mental health disorder, substance use disorder, or co-occurring disorder, or has experience with addiction or mental illness in their family or close friendships, who provides nonclinical, peer-based activities that engage, educate, and support individuals with co-occurring disorders, using the coach's own personal, lived experiences of recovery.

**Certification:** Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

**Citation:** Minnesota Rules 9533.0020

## Coaching vs. Therapy
**Clear Statement:** "Not a sponsor or a therapist but rather a role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator."

Provide nonclinical, peer-based activities. Not addiction counselors.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Certification required

**Requirements:**
- Lived experience (not just family/friends - must be personal)
- Complete necessary training
- Apply for CPRS Credential
- Pass IC&RC examination
- Pay fee

**Administered by:** Minnesota Certification Board

**Citation:** Minnesota Certification Board standards

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** YES - in Minnesota Rules
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Nonclinical peer-based activities
- **Independent practice?** Certification required
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- One of few states with regulatory definition
- Personal lived experience required (family/friends insufficient)
- IC&RC examination
- Clear distinction from clinical practice
