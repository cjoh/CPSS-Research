# Nevada Recovery Coaching Legal Research

**Research Date:** November 5, 2025

## Legal Definition of Recovery Coaching

**Status:** EXPLICITLY DEFINED IN STATE LAW

**Legal Definition:** NRS 433.627 defines "Peer recovery support specialist" as "an adult who is authorized under the provisions of NRS 433.631 to provide peer recovery support services for compensation as a regular part of his or her job duties."

**Effective Date:** Certification requirement became effective August 1, 2023, following passage of Senate Bill 69 (2021).

**Citation:** Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) ยง 433.627

## Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary

**Scope of Practice:**
- Peer recovery support specialists must adhere to a code of ethics established by the Nevada Certification Board
- Must maintain professional boundaries
- Required to refer clients to licensed providers when necessary
- Scope of practice aligned with SAMHSA (Substance Abuse Mental Health Services Administration) guidelines

**Key Limitation:** The statute defines PRSS as providing services "as a regular part of his or her job duties," suggesting employment-based practice framework.

**Citation:** Nevada Certification Board scope of practice guidelines; NRS 433.631

## Independent Practice Requirements

**Certification Requirements (Mandatory as of July 31, 2023):**
- High school diploma or jurisdictionally certified high school equivalency (HiSET or GED)
- Must be in recovery for at least two years prior to certification
- Minimum 475 hours of volunteering or paid work experience
- Complete one of three NCB-approved, State-sanctioned PRSS 101 foundational training courses
- Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery Examination
- Must live or work in Nevada at least 51% of the time

**Independent Practice Status:**
- Statute does not explicitly prohibit independent practice
- Statute references providing services "as a regular part of his or her job duties" which suggests employment context
- **No explicit prohibition on independent practice found in search results**
- For definitive answer, recommend contacting Nevada Certification Board directly

**Mandatory Certification:** As of July 31, 2023, ALL recovery support specialists in Nevada need to be certified by the Nevada Certification Board to offer recovery support services. Certification is NOT required for volunteer peer recovery support specialists or youth providing peer support to other youth.

**Renewal:** Recertification every two years requiring:
- $100 renewal fee
- 20 hours of continuing education in IC&RC Peer Recovery Domains
- Including 6 hours in ethics

**Citation:** Nevada Revised Statutes ยง 433.631; Nevada Certification Board requirements

## Summary

**Regulatory Status:** STATUTORY CERTIFICATION REQUIRED (as of August 1, 2023)

**Independent Practice:** Not explicitly addressed in statute; language suggests employment-based practice but does not prohibit independent practice. Further clarification needed from Nevada Certification Board.

**Coaching vs. Therapy:** Scope of practice defined by Nevada Certification Board aligned with SAMHSA guidelines; must refer to licensed providers when appropriate

**Key Finding:** Nevada is one of the few states with EXPLICIT STATUTORY DEFINITION and MANDATORY CERTIFICATION for peer recovery support specialists. This creates a clear legal framework but leaves independent practice question somewhat ambiguous.

**Recommendation:** Contact Nevada Certification Board directly for clarification on independent practice permissions and limitations.
