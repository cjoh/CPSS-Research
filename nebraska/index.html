Certification Overview

Nebraska's Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS) program provides certification for individuals with lived experience in mental health and/or substance use recovery. Nebraska does not differentiate between Mental Health Peer Support Specialists and Substance Use Peer Support Specialists.

Nebraska Division of Behavioral Health - Office of Consumer Affairs

Website: dhhs.ne.gov - Peer Support Training & Certification

Requirements

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be 19 years of age
  • Completion of high school or GED or higher degree
  • Must be lawfully present in the United States and provide documentation
  • Self-identify as a current or previous consumer

Training Requirements

  • 60 hours covering 13 domains and associated competencies
  • 10 hours of Ethics required
  • 40 hours must be live interactive hours
  • Program includes 40 hours of in-person instruction and 20 hours of online learning

13 Training Domains

  • Engagement
  • Support
  • Lived Experience
  • Personalize Peer Support
  • Recovery Planning
  • Resources, Services & Supports
  • Health, Wellness & Recovery Skills
  • Crisis Management
  • Communication
  • Collaboration & Teamwork
  • Leadership and Advocacy
  • Professional Growth & Development
  • Ethics

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete 60-hour DBH OCA approved Peer Support Core Curriculum training
  2. Review Code of Ethics: Read and agree to abide by Nebraska CPSS Code of Ethics
  3. Pass Certification Test: Take official certification test at State Office of Consumer Affairs and DHHS
  4. Receive Certification: Upon passing, eligible for employment as certified peer specialist in Nebraska
