Nebraska Recovery Coaching Legal Information

Regulatory Status: Voluntary Certification Program

Nebraska operates a voluntary certification program for Certified Peer Support Specialists (CPSS). This is NOT a statutory licensure - it's administered by the Department of Health and Human Services, separate from licensed mental health professionals.

Legal Definition

Status: No specific statutory definition for "recovery coach" found in Nebraska law.

Terminology: Nebraska uses "Certified Peer Support Specialist" (CPSS) rather than "recovery coach."

Key Finding: Nebraska operates an integrated certification program for individuals with lived experience of mental health and/or substance use disorders, rather than separate credentials.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

What CPSS Cannot Do

  • CPSS are explicitly NOT licensed mental health practitioners
  • Cannot provide clinical diagnosis or treatment
  • Cannot practice psychotherapy or counseling
  • Cannot develop clinical treatment plans

What CPSS Can Do

  • Provide services within the boundaries of their expertise
  • Collaborate with other professionals to meet client needs
  • Share lived experience of recovery
  • Provide peer support based on personal recovery journey

Independent Practice Requirements

Certification Requirements

  • Must be 19 years of age
  • High school diploma, GED, or higher degree required
  • Must be lawfully present in the United States
  • Self-identify as current or previous consumer of behavioral health services
  • Attestation of minimum of one year of recovery required
  • Complete 60 hours of Nebraska DHHS-approved Peer Support Core Curriculum training (including 10 hours ethics, minimum 40 hours live interactive)
  • Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery Examination within six months of approval
  • Read and agree to abide by the Nebraska CPSS Code of Ethics

Independent Practice Status

CPSS may open their own practice in Nebraska.

However: For Medicaid billing purposes, national certification in peer support and enrollment under a supervising practitioner, provider group, or provider organization is required.

No explicit prohibition on independent practice found, but Medicaid reimbursement structure suggests supervision requirements for billing.

Renewal

Certificate must be renewed every 2 years with 20 hours of continuing education units.

Relevant Statutes and Regulations

  • Nebraska DHHS Division of Behavioral Health CPSS Certification Guidelines - Non-statutory administrative program

Key Finding: Recovery coaching/peer support is NOT a licensed profession in Nebraska. It operates as a voluntary certification program administered by DHHS, separate from the statutory licensing framework for mental health professionals.

Practical Guidance

Key Distinctions

  • Recovery coaching/peer support is completely separate from licensed mental health practice in Nebraska
  • CPSS operate under ethical guidelines requiring practice within scope of training
  • Collaboration with licensed professionals is required when appropriate
  • No specific statutory boundary defined between coaching and therapy

Important Considerations

  • While independent practice is permitted, Medicaid billing requires affiliation with supervising practitioner or organization
  • CPSS must maintain clear boundaries regarding clinical services
  • Adherence to CPSS Code of Ethics is mandatory
  • Consider professional liability insurance
  • Establish clear referral relationships with licensed professionals
