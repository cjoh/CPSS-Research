# Colorado Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Peer Support Professional (PSP)** - for substance use disorder recovery

## Certifying Body
**Colorado Providers Association (COPA)**
- Administers certification through International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC)
- Website: Contact for current information

**Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Financing**
- Oversees peer services as part of Medicaid behavioral health services
- Website: https://hcpf.colorado.gov/peerservices

## Important 2025-2026 Update
**Effective January 1, 2026**: All Peer Support Professionals delivering Medicaid behavioral health services must either be credentialed or will be credentialed by a nationally recognized organization by July 1, 2026 to be eligible for reimbursement.

## Certification Overview
Peer Support Professionals in Colorado provide support based on shared lived experience of substance use disorder recovery. The certification follows IC&RC standards and domains.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
- **Recovery Requirement**: Minimum of 1 year established in own recovery
- Must have history of substance use disorder
- Currently engaged in recovery
- Willing to self-identify and use lived experience to help others

### Residency Requirements
- **Colorado Residency**: Must be Colorado resident 51% of the time to qualify for certification

### Educational Requirements
- No specific degree requirement listed
- Must complete 60 hours of required training

## Training Requirements

### Total Required Hours: 60 Hours

**Distribution:**
- **Minimum Live Training**: At least 30 of the 60 hours must be earned in live, interactive, non-self-paced training (online or in-person)
- **Remaining 30 hours**: Can be self-paced or asynchronous

### IC&RC Domain Training (specific hours required):
- **Advocacy**: 10 hours
- **Recovery/Wellness Support**: 10 hours
- **Mentoring**: 10 hours
- **Additional Required Topics**:
  - Trauma-informed care
  - Cultural sensitivity
  - Whole health
  - Substance use
  - Mental health
  - Family support

## Work Experience Requirements
- **Total Hours**: 500 hours of work experience (volunteer or paid)
- **Timeline**: Over a minimum of 6 months
- **Focus**: Specific to IC&RC domains (advocacy, recovery support, mentoring)

## Application and Certification Process

### Step 1: Meet Eligibility Requirements
- Verify 1+ year of established recovery
- Confirm Colorado residency (51% of time)
- Prepare documentation of lived experience

### Step 2: Complete Required Training
- Select approved training provider
- Complete 60 hours of training:
  - 30+ hours live, interactive training
  - Specific hours in IC&RC domains
  - Required topic areas
- Obtain training completion certificates

### Step 3: Gain Work Experience
- Complete 500 hours work/volunteer experience
- Must span at least 6 months
- Focus on IC&RC domains
- Document all hours appropriately

### Step 4: Apply for Certification
- Submit application to COPA
- Provide all training documentation
- Submit proof of 500 work experience hours
- Include verification of Colorado residency
- Pay application fee

### Step 5: Pass Examination (if required)
- IC&RC may require competency examination
- Contact COPA for current exam requirements

### Step 6: Receive Certification
- COPA issues Peer Support Professional certificate
- Begin working as certified PSP
- Eligible for Medicaid reimbursement

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become an approved training provider in Colorado:

### Step 1: Contact COPA and State Agencies
- **COPA**: Colorado Providers Association (IC&RC certification body)
- **HCPF**: Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Financing
- **Website**: https://hcpf.colorado.gov/peerservices

### Step 2: Develop IC&RC-Compliant Curriculum
Must include:
- 60 hours total training
- Specific hours in IC&RC domains:
  - Advocacy (10 hours)
  - Recovery/Wellness Support (10 hours)
  - Mentoring (10 hours)
- Required topic areas (trauma-informed care, cultural sensitivity, etc.)
- Minimum 30 hours live, interactive training

### Step 3: Submit for Approval
- Provide curriculum for review
- Include instructor qualifications
- Demonstrate IC&RC standard compliance
- Show assessment methodology

### Step 4: Meet Quality Standards
- Qualified instructors with lived experience
- Evidence-based curriculum
- Appropriate training format (in-person or virtual)
- Valid assessment tools

### Step 5: Receive Approval
- Listed as approved training provider
- Issue certificates counting toward certification
- Report completions to COPA
