Official Certification: Peer Support Professional (PSP)

Important 2025-2026 Update

Effective January 1, 2026: All Peer Support Professionals delivering Medicaid behavioral health services must either be credentialed or will be credentialed by a nationally recognized organization by July 1, 2026 to be eligible for reimbursement.

Certification Overview

Peer Support Professionals in Colorado provide support based on shared lived experience of substance use disorder recovery. The certification follows IC&RC (International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium) standards and domains.

Certifying Bodies

Colorado Providers Association (COPA)
Administers certification through IC&RC

Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Financing
Website: hcpf.colorado.gov/peerservices

Requirements

Personal Experience

  • Minimum Recovery: 1 year established in own recovery from substance use disorder
  • Currently engaged in recovery
  • Willing to self-identify and use lived experience

Residency

  • Must be Colorado resident 51% of the time

Training Requirements

Total: 60 Hours

Live Training: At least 30 hours must be live, interactive, non-self-paced (online or in-person)

Remaining 30 hours: Can be self-paced or asynchronous

IC&RC Domain Training (specific hours required):

  • Advocacy: 10 hours
  • Recovery/Wellness Support: 10 hours
  • Mentoring: 10 hours
  • Additional Topics: Trauma-informed care, cultural sensitivity, whole health, substance use, mental health, family support

Work Experience Requirements

  • Total Hours: 500 hours of work experience (volunteer or paid)
  • Timeline: Over a minimum of 6 months
  • Focus: Specific to IC&RC domains

Application Process

  1. Verify 1+ year recovery and Colorado residency (51% of time)
  2. Complete 60 hours training (30+ hours live, IC&RC domain requirements)
  3. Complete 500 hours work/volunteer experience over 6+ months
  4. Submit application to COPA with all documentation
  5. Pass IC&RC examination (if required)
  6. Receive PSP certification

Employment & Reimbursement

Important: Effective January 1, 2026, must be credentialed to bill Medicaid

Employment opportunities in community mental health centers, substance use disorder treatment programs, integrated behavioral health clinics, hospital-based programs, community-based organizations, and recovery community centers.

Key Considerations

  • Plan certification timeline to meet 2026 credentialing deadline
  • Colorado follows IC&RC standards
  • Minimum 1 year recovery required
  • 500 hours work experience over 6 months
  • Mix of live and self-paced training allowed
