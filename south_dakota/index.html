South Dakota Peer Support Specialist Certification

No State Certification Available

Certification Overview

South Dakota is currently the only state that does not offer any peer support specialist certification. The state does not have a state-endorsed certification process.

Important Note

South Dakota does not offer peer certification and is one of only two states that do not reimburse for either mental health or substance use disorder peer support through Medicaid.

Available Training Options

Despite the lack of state certification, there are training programs available:

Straight Up Care Program

  • Authorized 100 $500 grants to cover certification
  • Offers 46-hour self-paced online certification
  • Must be a resident of South Dakota
  • Must identify as in-recovery from mental health or addiction or have a loved one who they have helped navigate these spaces
  • Must be over the age of 18

Drug Court Pilot Program

Dr. Melissa Dittberner, a professor at the University of South Dakota, developed a curriculum for peer support specialist training in collaboration with the South Dakota Foundation for Medical Care.

Important Limitations

Completing training programs will not make you a certified recovery coach by the state, but it will still be possible for you to practice. Since South Dakota lacks state certification, individuals interested in peer support work should consider private training programs or potentially seek certification from neighboring states if working with their residents.
