South Dakota Recovery Coaching Legal Information

Legal Status Overview

Completely Unregulated - Independent Practice Fully Permitted

Regulatory Status: NO state certification, licensing, or registration

Independent Practice: Fully permitted - no restrictions whatsoever

Legal Definition

Status: NO STATE-MANDATED DEFINITION OR CERTIFICATION

South Dakota does NOT have a state-endorsed certification process for recovery coaches.

Key Finding: Completing training programs will NOT make you a "certified recovery coach" in any official South Dakota state capacity, but it is still possible to practice.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Licensed Professional Framework

South Dakota regulates licensed health professionals under Title 36 of South Dakota Codified Laws, but does NOT include recovery coaches.

  • Licensed Mental Health Counselors
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Licensed Clinical Social Workers

Key Distinction

Licensed professionals governed by Title 36; recovery coaches operate outside this framework. Recovery coaching is NOT a licensed profession in South Dakota.

Independent Practice Requirements

State Certification: NONE

  • South Dakota does not have a state-endorsed certification process
  • No state examination required
  • No state registration required
  • No mandatory training hours
  • Can start practicing immediately
  • Can set own fees (up to $150/hour reported)

Available Training Options (Voluntary)

  • Face It Together - both in-person and online classes
  • Project Recovery - directs individuals to complete online program through CASAT

Basic Training Requirements (Voluntary Programs)

  • Must be at least 18 years old
  • Have GED, high school diploma, or college transcript
  • Have experience with addiction (yourself or close family member, partner, or friend)

Scope of Practice

What Recovery Coaches CAN Do

  • Establish private practice immediately
  • Work in collaborative roles in healthcare teams
  • Set own fees
  • Provide peer support based on lived experience
  • Offer recovery navigation and coaching

What Recovery Coaches Must Avoid

  • Diagnosing mental health or substance use disorders
  • Providing clinical treatment or psychotherapy
  • Developing clinical treatment plans
  • Presenting yourself as a licensed professional

Note: Even without recovery coach regulations, crossing into licensed practice territory is prohibited.

Important Considerations

Advantages

  • No state certification fees or requirements
  • No mandatory training, examination, or renewal
  • Complete flexibility in practice structure
  • No supervision requirements or continuing education mandates

Risks

  • NO legal protection of "recovery coach" title (anyone can use it)
  • NO regulatory guidance on scope of practice
  • NO state oversight or accountability framework
  • Potential liability exposure without regulatory structure
  • Must self-determine ethical boundaries

Strongly Recommended (Despite Lack of Requirements)

  • Complete voluntary training programs (Face It Together or CASAT)
  • Obtain national certification (CARC, NCPRSS, etc.) for credibility
  • Professional liability insurance (ESSENTIAL in unregulated environment)
  • Comprehensive service agreements clearly defining non-clinical nature
  • Business entity formation (LLC) for liability protection
  • Establish supervision or consultation relationships

Legal Citations

Statutory Framework

  • SD Codified Laws Title 36 - Professions and Occupations (does NOT include recovery coaches)
  • No specific statutes found governing recovery coaches

Regulatory Authority

  • South Dakota Department of Health - Licensing Boards (regulate licensed professions, NOT recovery coaches)
  • No state agency has jurisdiction over recovery coaches
