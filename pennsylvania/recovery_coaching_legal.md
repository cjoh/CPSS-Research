# Pennsylvania Recovery Coaching Legal Research

**Research Date:** November 5, 2025

## Legal Definition of Recovery Coaching

**Status:** Defined by certification board (not statute)

**Terminology:** Pennsylvania uses three related certifications:
1. **Certified Peer Specialist (CPS)** - for individuals with lived experience in mental health recovery
2. **Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS)** - for individuals with lived experience in substance use disorder recovery
3. **Certified Family Recovery Specialist (CFRS)** - for family members supporting recovery

**Definition (CRS):** An individual with personal, lived experience of their own recovery from substance use disorder. By offering insight into the recovery process, Recovery Specialists share a unique perspective with those with similar life experiences. The Recovery Specialist is a role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator.

**Key Characteristic:** Based on lived experience, NOT clinical training.

**Citation:** Pennsylvania Certification Board (PCB) definitions and standards

## Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary

**Legal Status:**
- Recovery Specialists/Peer Specialists are NOT licensed professionals
- Standards developed and administered by third-party, non-profit certification provider (Pennsylvania Certification Board)
- Distinct from licensed professions regulated by Pennsylvania Department of State, Bureau of Professional and Occupational Affairs

**Scope of Practice:**
- Role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator
- Share lived experience and insight into recovery process
- Provide peer support based on personal recovery journey
- **Must NOT engage in clinical diagnosis, treatment, or therapy**

**Code of Ethics:**
- Main purpose of certification is public protection
- Ensures individuals are not harmed while receiving recovery support services
- Once certified, must adhere to Code of Ethics at all times (even if no longer providing services)
- Violation of Code of Ethics can result in loss of certification

**Citation:** PCB Code of Ethics; PCB certification standards

## Independent Practice Requirements

**Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) Requirements:**

**Education:**
- Minimum high school diploma/GED

**Recovery Experience:**
- At least 18 months of continuous personal, lived recovery experience to enroll in CRS training

**Work/Education Requirement:**
- Within the last three years, must have either:
  - Maintained at least 12 months of successful work, OR
  - Earned at least 24 credit hours at college or post-secondary education institution

**Training:**
- 78 hours of mandatory, standardized recovery specialist training
- Includes 66 in-class hours and 12 hours of homework

**Examination:**
- Must pass PCB Examination for Certified Recovery Specialist after application approval

**Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) Requirements:**

**Training:**
- 75 hours of mandatory peer specialist training
- Only three authorized training vendors provide acceptable training

**Continuing Education:**
- 36 hours of continuing education every two years
- 24 hours must be specific to peer support and/or recovery practices
- 3 hours in ethics
- Remaining 9 hours chosen by CPS

**Independent Practice Status:**
- **PERMITTED** - Recovery coaches/specialists may open their own practice
- CARCs (Certified Addiction Recovery Coaches) in most states can establish a private practice as a Recovery Coach
- **However, encouraged to seek Supervision support**

**Medicaid Billing:**
- Formal certification through PCB is necessary to provide Medicaid billable peer support services
- May require organizational affiliation for Medicaid reimbursement

**Citation:** PCB CRS certification requirements; PCB CPS certification requirements; Penn State Beaver Certified Recovery Specialist Program

## Legal Citations

**Statutory Framework:**
- **No specific statute found** governing recovery coaches or peer specialists
- Recovery coaching NOT regulated under Pennsylvania professional licensure laws
- Operates as voluntary certification system

**Certification Authority:**
- **Pennsylvania Certification Board (PCB)** - third-party, non-profit certification provider
- Develops and administers standards for certification

**Related Regulations:**
- Pennsylvania Department of Drug and Alcohol Programs (DDAP) licensing applies to facilities, not individual recovery coaches
- Licensed professionals (LPC, LCSW, etc.) governed by separate statutory frameworks

**Professional Practice Context:**
- Pennsylvania Act 76 - addresses independent practice for licensed counselors (does NOT apply to recovery specialists)
- Title 49 Pa. Code - Professional licensing (does NOT include recovery coaches)

## Summary

**Regulatory Status:** VOLUNTARY CERTIFICATION (not statutory licensure)

**Independent Practice:** PERMITTED - Recovery coaches/specialists may open their own practice

**Coaching vs. Therapy:** Clearly distinguished through lived experience basis vs. clinical training; must adhere to Code of Ethics and avoid clinical services

**Key Finding:** Pennsylvania allows recovery coaches/specialists to practice independently through voluntary certification system administered by Pennsylvania Certification Board. This is separate from statutory licensure of clinical professionals.

**Practical Guidance for Independent Practice:**

**Allowed:**
- Open own private practice
- Provide peer support based on lived experience
- Role model, mentor, advocate functions
- Share recovery journey and insights
- Help with engagement in treatment or recovery systems

**Must Avoid:**
- Clinical diagnosis
- Treatment planning (clinical)
- Psychotherapy or counseling
- Presenting as licensed professional
- Crossing boundaries into licensed practice

**Best Practices:**
1. Maintain certification through PCB
2. Complete continuing education requirements (36 hours/2 years)
3. Adhere to Code of Ethics at all times
4. Consider voluntary supervision for support
5. Obtain professional liability insurance
6. Clear service agreements defining non-clinical nature
7. Establish referral relationships with licensed professionals
8. Document services appropriately
9. Understand Medicaid billing requirements if applicable

**Recommendation:** While independent practice is permitted, recovery coaches should maintain clear boundaries, seek supervision support, and ensure they understand the distinction between peer support and clinical services.
