Certification Overview

Pennsylvania offers multiple peer support certifications. The Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) is for individuals with personal lived experience in substance use disorder recovery who provide recovery support services. The Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) serves those in mental health recovery. The program was recently updated in 2024 to remove barriers and enhance workforce opportunities.

Important 2024 Updates

High school diploma requirement REMOVED

Mandatory recent employment/volunteer experience requirement (within last 3 years) REMOVED

Changes aimed at enhancing workforce opportunities and breaking down barriers to certification.

Certification Types

  • Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) - For Substance Use Disorder
  • Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) - For Mental Health
  • Certified Family Recovery Specialist (CFRS) - For Family Support

Requirements (CRS)

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • Minimum 18 months of continuous personal, lived recovery experience from substance use disorder
  • Must be able to articulate recovery journey through narrative statements

Education Requirements

  • NO high school diploma required (barrier removed in 2024)
  • Applicants provide narratives through PCB application describing how they achieved recovery and wellness

Training Requirements

  • 78 hours of mandatory, standardized recovery specialist training
  • Must be completed through Pennsylvania Certification Board-approved trainer
  • Training covers recovery principles, ethics, peer support competencies, communication skills, cultural competence, documentation, and professionalism

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Enroll in and complete 78-hour CRS training through PCB-approved provider
  2. Prepare Application Materials: Write personal recovery narrative, gather training completion certificate, prepare additional documentation
  3. Submit Application: Submit complete application through Pennsylvania Certification Board
  4. Application Review: PCB reviews application and issues certification if approved

Fees and Costs

Contact Pennsylvania Certification Board for current fee schedule including initial certification fees, examination fees (if applicable), and renewal fees.

Typical Training Cost: $1,000-$1,500 (varies by provider)

Renewal Requirements

  • Continuing Education: 30 hours per renewal period (typical for Pennsylvania certifications)
  • Must include ethics training
  • From PCB-approved providers
  • Submit renewal application with continuing education documentation

How to Become a Training Provider

To become an approved training provider for Pennsylvania Certification Board CRS programs, organizations must meet PCB standards.

Requirements

  • Curriculum must cover all 78 required training hours
  • Must align with PCB Recovery Specialist Curriculum standards
  • Trainers must be qualified and approved by PCB
  • Organization must apply for and receive PCB approval

Recovery Specialist Curriculum

Website: pacertboard.org/recoveryspecialist

Standardized curriculum developed by PCB covering all required competency areas. Must be followed by all approved trainers.
