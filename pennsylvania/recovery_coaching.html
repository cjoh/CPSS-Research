Legal Status Overview

Independent Practice Permitted

State Certifications: Certified Peer Specialist (CPS), Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS), Certified Family Recovery Specialist (CFRS)

Regulatory Status: Voluntary certification (not statutory licensure)

Independent Practice: Permitted - Recovery coaches may open their own practice

Legal Definition

Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS)

"An individual with personal, lived experience of their own recovery from substance use disorder. By offering insight into the recovery process, Recovery Specialists share a unique perspective with those with similar life experiences. The Recovery Specialist is a role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator."

Key Characteristic: Based on lived experience, NOT clinical training.

Pennsylvania Uses Three Related Certifications:

  • Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) - for individuals with lived experience in mental health recovery
  • Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) - for individuals with lived experience in substance use disorder recovery
  • Certified Family Recovery Specialist (CFRS) - for family members supporting recovery

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Recovery Specialists/Peer Specialists (Permitted)

  • Role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator
  • Share lived experience and insight into recovery process
  • Provide peer support based on personal recovery journey
  • NOT licensed professionals
  • Must adhere to Code of Ethics

Licensed Professionals (Different Requirements)

  • Licensed Professional Counselors (LPC)
  • Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSW)
  • Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Governed by Pennsylvania Department of State, Bureau of Professional and Occupational Affairs

Recovery Specialists Must NOT: Engage in clinical diagnosis, treatment, or therapy

Code of Ethics

  • Main purpose of certification is public protection
  • Ensures individuals are not harmed while receiving recovery support services
  • Once certified, must adhere to Code of Ethics at all times
  • Violation of Code of Ethics can result in loss of certification

Independent Practice Requirements

Independent Practice: PERMITTED

Recovery coaches/specialists may open their own practice. CARCs (Certified Addiction Recovery Coaches) in most states can establish a private practice as a Recovery Coach.

However: Encouraged to seek Supervision support

Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) Requirements

  • Education: Minimum high school diploma/GED
  • Recovery Experience: At least 18 months of continuous personal, lived recovery experience
  • Work/Education: Within last three years, must have either:
    • Maintained at least 12 months of successful work, OR
    • Earned at least 24 credit hours at college or post-secondary education institution
  • Training: 78 hours of mandatory, standardized recovery specialist training (66 in-class hours, 12 hours homework)
  • Examination: Must pass PCB Examination for Certified Recovery Specialist

Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) Requirements

  • Training: 75 hours of mandatory peer specialist training from authorized vendors
  • Continuing Education: 36 hours every two years
    • 24 hours specific to peer support/recovery practices
    • 3 hours in ethics
    • Remaining 9 hours chosen by CPS

Medicaid Billing

Formal certification through PCB is necessary to provide Medicaid billable peer support services. May require organizational affiliation for Medicaid reimbursement.

Scope of Practice

What Recovery Coaches/Specialists CAN Do

  • Open own private practice
  • Provide peer support based on lived experience
  • Role model, mentor, advocate functions
  • Share recovery journey and insights
  • Help with engagement in treatment or recovery systems

What Recovery Coaches/Specialists Must NOT Do

  • Clinical diagnosis
  • Treatment planning (clinical)
  • Psychotherapy or counseling
  • Presenting as licensed professional
  • Crossing boundaries into licensed practice

Important Considerations

Best Practices for Independent Practice

  • Maintain certification through PCB
  • Complete continuing education requirements (36 hours/2 years)
  • Adhere to Code of Ethics at all times
  • Consider voluntary supervision for support
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Clear service agreements defining non-clinical nature
  • Establish referral relationships with licensed professionals
  • Document services appropriately
  • Understand Medicaid billing requirements if applicable

Legal Citations

Statutory Framework

  • No specific statute found governing recovery coaches or peer specialists
  • Recovery coaching NOT regulated under Pennsylvania professional licensure laws
  • Operates as voluntary certification system

Certification Authority

  • Pennsylvania Certification Board (PCB) - third-party, non-profit certification provider
  • Develops and administers standards for certification

Related Regulations

  • Pennsylvania Department of Drug and Alcohol Programs (DDAP) licensing applies to facilities, not individual recovery coaches
  • Pennsylvania Act 76 - addresses independent practice for licensed counselors (does NOT apply to recovery specialists)
  • Title 49 Pa. Code - Professional licensing (does NOT include recovery coaches)
