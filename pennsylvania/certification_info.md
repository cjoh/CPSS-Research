# Pennsylvania Peer Support Specialist Certification Information

## Official Certification Names
**Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS)** - For Substance Use Disorder
**Certified Peer Specialist (CPS)** - For Mental Health
**Certified Family Recovery Specialist (CFRS)** - For Family Support

## Certifying Body
**Pennsylvania Certification Board (PCB)**
- Website: https://www.pacertboard.org/
- CRS Page: https://www.pacertboard.org/crs
- CPS Page: https://www.pacertboard.org/cps
- Email: Contact through website
- Oversight: PA Department of Human Services, Office of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services (OMHSAS)

## Certification Overview
Pennsylvania offers multiple peer support certifications. The Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) is for individuals with personal lived experience in substance use disorder recovery who provide recovery support services. The program was recently updated in 2024 to remove barriers and enhance workforce opportunities.

## Important 2024 Updates from OMHSAS/RCPA

### Eligibility Changes (Effective 2024)
- **High school diploma requirement REMOVED**
- **Mandatory recent employment/volunteer experience requirement (within last 3 years) REMOVED**
- Applicants now provide narratives through PCB application describing how they achieved recovery and wellness
- Changes aimed at enhancing workforce opportunities and breaking down barriers
- OMHSAS successfully collaborated with CMS to revise Pennsylvania's State Medicaid Plan for Certified Peer Specialists

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements (CRS)
- **Minimum 18 months** of continuous personal, lived recovery experience from substance use disorder
- Must be able to articulate recovery journey through narrative statements

### Training Requirements

#### Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS)
- **78 hours** of mandatory, standardized recovery specialist training
- Must be completed through Pennsylvania Certification Board-approved trainer
- Training covers:
  - Recovery principles and practices
  - Ethics and boundaries
  - Peer support competencies
  - Communication skills
  - Cultural competence
  - Documentation and professionalism

### Application Requirements
- Completion of 78-hour approved training
- Narrative statements about personal recovery journey
- Application fee payment
- Background check (may be required by employers)

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Training
Enroll in and complete 78-hour CRS training through PCB-approved provider

### Step 2: Prepare Application Materials
- Write personal recovery narrative
- Gather training completion certificate
- Prepare any additional required documentation

### Step 3: Submit Application
Submit complete application through Pennsylvania Certification Board

### Step 4: Application Review
PCB reviews application and issues certification if approved

## Application Fees
Contact Pennsylvania Certification Board for current fee schedule:
- Initial certification fees
- Examination fees (if applicable)
- Renewal fees

## Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
Contact PCB for specific renewal cycle requirements

### Continuing Education Requirements
**30 hours** of continuing education per renewal period (typical for Pennsylvania certifications)
- Must include ethics training
- From PCB-approved providers

### Renewal Process
Submit renewal application with continuing education documentation

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
To become an approved training provider for Pennsylvania Certification Board CRS programs, organizations must meet PCB standards.

### Requirements
- Curriculum must cover all 78 required training hours
- Must align with PCB Recovery Specialist Curriculum standards
- Trainers must be qualified and approved by PCB
- Organization must apply for and receive PCB approval

### Recovery Specialist Curriculum
**Website:** https://www.pacertboard.org/recoveryspecialist
- Standardized curriculum developed by PCB
- Covers all required competency areas
- Must be followed by all approved trainers

### Application Process
Contact Pennsylvania Certification Board:
- **Website:** https://www.pacertboard.org/
- Review instructor/trainer requirements
- Submit application for training provider approval
- Meet all PCB standards and requirements

### Approved Instructor Requirements
Visit: https://www.pacertboard.org/node/141627/instructors for current instructor requirements and approved instructor list
