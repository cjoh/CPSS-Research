Legal Status Overview

Status: UNCLEAR - Recovery coaches defined in administrative code, but independent practice status uncertain

State-Defined Term: Recovery coach (Wis. Admin. Code DHS 75.03(76))

Work Setting: Defined within DHS 75-certified substance use programs

Clinical Services: NOT clinical staff, CANNOT provide clinical services

State Certification: No state certification requirement found

Legal Definition

Wis. Admin. Code DHS 75.03(76)

"Recovery coach" means an individual who:

  • Uses lived experience of recovery to support individuals
  • Promotes recovery-oriented services
  • Works within DHS 75-certified substance use programs
  • Is NOT clinical staff
  • CANNOT provide clinical services

DHS 75 Programs

What is DHS 75? Wisconsin Administrative Code Chapter DHS 75 establishes standards for certification of substance abuse treatment programs.

DHS 75-Certified Programs Include:

  • Residential substance abuse treatment facilities
  • Outpatient substance abuse treatment programs
  • Opioid treatment programs
  • Other certified SUD treatment services

Independent Practice Status

Independent Practice: UNCLEAR

What We Know:

  • Recovery coaches are defined in administrative code governing DHS 75-certified programs
  • Definition appears to contemplate work within certified substance use programs
  • Recovery coaches are explicitly NOT clinical staff
  • Supervision is required for "recovery support staff" in DHS 75 programs

What Is Unclear:

  • Whether recovery coaches can operate independent practices outside DHS 75 programs
  • Whether the DHS 75 definition restricts all recovery coaching, or only those within certified programs
  • Whether non-clinical peer support services can be offered independently
  • Whether supervision requirement applies only within DHS 75 programs or more broadly

Interpretation Challenge: The administrative code defines recovery coaches within the context of DHS 75-certified programs, but does not explicitly prohibit independent practice outside those programs. This creates legal ambiguity.

DHS 75 Program Requirements

Recovery Support Staff Supervision

Within DHS 75-certified programs, recovery support staff (including recovery coaches) must work under supervision.

Supervision Requirements:

  • Supervisor must be qualified clinical staff member
  • Regular oversight of recovery support services
  • Quality assurance and guidance
  • Ensure services remain non-clinical and within scope

Clinical vs. Non-Clinical Boundaries

Critical Distinction: Recovery Coaches Are NOT Clinical Staff

Wisconsin law explicitly states: Recovery coaches are NOT clinical staff and CANNOT provide clinical services.

This Means:

  • Recovery coaches operate in non-clinical capacity
  • Services must be peer-based, not clinically-based
  • Clear boundaries must be maintained
  • Clinical activities are prohibited

Prohibited Clinical Activities

Recovery coaches CANNOT provide:

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Diagnosing substance use disorders or mental health conditions
  • Clinical Treatment: Providing clinical treatment or therapy
  • Treatment Planning: Creating clinical treatment plans
  • Clinical Assessment: Conducting clinical assessments
  • Psychotherapy: Providing counseling or therapy services
  • Medical Advice: Giving medical or psychiatric advice

Non-Clinical Recovery Support Activities

Recovery coaches typically provide non-clinical services such as:

  • Sharing lived experience of recovery
  • Providing peer support and encouragement
  • Recovery coaching and mentoring
  • Advocacy and empowerment
  • Connection to recovery resources
  • Facilitation of peer support groups
  • Recovery education and skill-building
  • Support for non-clinical recovery planning
  • Assistance navigating systems and services

State Certification

No State Certification Requirement Found

Research Finding: Wisconsin does not appear to have a state-mandated certification or licensing requirement for recovery coaches.

What This Means:

  • No state examination required
  • No state registration process identified
  • No mandatory state training standards found

Voluntary Certification:

While not required by state law, recovery coaches may choose to obtain:

  • National certifications (IC&RC, NAADAC)
  • Other professional credentials
  • Employer-specific training

Practical Guidance

Working Within DHS 75-Certified Programs

  1. Seek Employment: Apply to DHS 75-certified substance use programs
  2. Complete Training: Obtain employer-required or voluntary certifications
  3. Work Under Supervision: Accept supervision by clinical staff
  4. Maintain Non-Clinical Boundaries: Provide only peer support services
  5. Follow Program Policies: Adhere to DHS 75 program requirements
