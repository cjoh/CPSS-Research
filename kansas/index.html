No Cost Certification Program

There is NO FEE to obtain the Kansas Certified Peer Mentor credential - making this one of the most accessible programs in the nation!

Overview

The Kansas Certified Peer Mentor (KCPM) program offers a two-level training system that allows for progressive skill development. The credential is issued by the Kansas Department for Aging and Disability Services (KDADS) Division of Behavioral Health Services.

Basic Requirements

  • Age: Must be at least 18 years old
  • Recovery Status: Must self-identify as a person in recovery from a substance use disorder
  • Recovery Time: Minimum of one (1) year in stable, full recovery
  • Lived Experience: Personal lived experience with behavioral health condition required
  • Education: No specific educational requirements beyond high school age

Two-Level Training System

Level 1: Kansas Certified Peer Specialist in Training

Format: BHS approved online (self-paced)

Platform: LearnWorlds training platform (kansascpstraining.getlearnworlds.com)

Developer: Wichita State University: Community Engagement Institute

Exam Requirement: Must achieve score of 80% or greater

Timeline: If working and billing for peer support services prior to certification, must complete within 4 months of starting position

Level 2: Kansas Certified Peer Specialist

Format: 2.5 day in-person or virtual training

Exam Requirement: Must pass exam with 80% proficiency

Timeline: If working and billing for peer support services prior to certification, must complete within one year of completing Level One

Frequency: Offered at least 4 times per year in varying locations around the state

Continuing Education

Annual Requirement: 6 hours of continuing education units (CEUs)

Topics may include:

  • Ethics
  • Boundaries
  • Skill development
  • Cultural competency

2025 Training Schedule

Training is offered up to 4 times per year in varying locations around Kansas, with both in-person and virtual options available. Level 2 trainings are scheduled at minimum 4 times annually.

Related Certifications in Kansas

Kansas offers three distinct peer support certifications:

  • Kansas Certified Peer Mentor (KCPM) - For substance use disorder (SUD)
  • Kansas Certified Peer Specialist (KCPS) - For mental health (MH)
  • Kansas Certified Parent Peer Support Specialist (KCPPSS) - For parents with children experiencing behavioral health challenges
