# Louisiana Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Defined operationally (not in statute)

**Definition:** Recovery coaches are "integral part of clinical team, but not therapists; they don't diagnose clients or prescribe medication."

**Administered by:** Louisiana Department of Health

## Coaching vs. Therapy
**Clear Distinction:**
> "A recovery coach is not a licensed therapist or counselor. They do not provide a diagnosis, and they cannot prescribe medication or a treatment plan."

**What coaches do:** Advocate, help develop goals, boost confidence, give referrals, conduct recovery groups.

**Citation:** Louisiana Dept of Health regulations

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Certification required for employment

**Requirements:**
- Age 18+
- Personal lived experience
- Minimum 12 months continuous sobriety
- Complete core peer support specialist training (Louisiana Dept of Health or state-approved entity)
- 10 continuing education units annually

**Recent Update:** Emergency Rule for Behavioral Health Service Providers (effective November 13, 2024)

**Citation:** Louisiana Department of Health Emergency Rule (Nov 2024)

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** Operationally defined; not in statute
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Clearly distinguished; not clinical practice
- **Independent practice?** Certification required for employment
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- Part of clinical team but not clinicians
- Cannot diagnose or prescribe
- Recent emergency rule (Nov 2024) affects behavioral health providers
- Annual CE requirement
