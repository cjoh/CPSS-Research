# Louisiana Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Peer Support Specialist (PSS)**

Office of Behavioral Health (OBH)

- Agency: Louisiana Department of Health, Office of Behavioral Health
- Phone: Contact The Extra Mile for training inquiries
- Website: https://ldh.la.gov/page/peer-support-specialist
- Training Manager: Amanda L. Holmes, PhD, MHM, RFPSS, RPSS (LA State Program Manager for Recovery Peer Program)

## Personal Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Age:** Must be at least 18 years of age or older
- **Education:** Minimum of high school diploma or GED
- **Lived Experience:** Must have lived experience with a behavioral health diagnosis (mental health, addiction, or co-occurring disorders)
- **Recovery Status:** At least 12 months of continuous demonstrated recovery as indicated by SAMHSA's working definition of recovery
- **Definition:** Peer support specialists use knowledge, skills, and experience to help others work toward meaningful recovery

## Training Requirements

### Intensive Training Program
- **Duration:** 2 weeks, 76 hours total
- **Daily Schedule:** 8 hours of class work daily plus several hours of homework
- **Format:** Virtual or in-person at various locations around the state
- **Developer:** Recovery Opportunity Center of Arizona
- **Frequency:** Office of Behavioral Health makes every effort to ensure a minimum of 10 trainings are provided each year

### Training Capacity
- Capacity for trainings is limited
- Application process is competitive
- Must meet eligibility criteria to be considered

## Certification Requirements

After completing the 76-hour training, candidates must:
1. Successfully complete all training modules
2. Pass certification examination
3. Demonstrate competency in peer support principles
4. Agree to Code of Ethics

## Application Process

1. Verify eligibility requirements (age, education, recovery time)
2. Submit competitive application to The Extra Mile or approved provider
3. Be accepted into training cohort
4. Complete 76-hour training (2 weeks)
5. Pass certification examination
6. Receive Peer Support Specialist certification
7. Become eligible to provide Medicaid-reimbursable services

## Fees

**Training Fees:** Contact training provider for current costs
- Training is provided through competitive application process
- Some funding may be available through OBH

## Continuing Education Requirements

### Annual CE Requirements
- **Minimum:** 10 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) per calendar year
- **Ethics:** 3 CEUs must be in the area of Ethics
- **Competencies:** 7 CEUs in competencies related to tenets of peer support
- **Total:** 10 CEUs annually required for renewal

## Renewal Requirements
- Complete 10 CEUs per calendar year
- Maintain recovery status
- Continue to work or volunteer in peer support capacity
- Adhere to ethical standards

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Louisiana's Program
- **Intensive Training:** 76-hour training is more comprehensive than many states
- **Standardized Curriculum:** Use of Recovery Opportunity Center of Arizona curriculum ensures consistency
- **Medicaid Recognition:** Certified peers can provide reimbursable services
- **Competitive Process:** Limited capacity ensures quality of peer workforce
- **Dual Population:** Serves both mental health and substance use peer support
- **Strong Recovery Foundation:** 12-month recovery requirement ensures stability
- **Ongoing Support:** 10 trainings per year provides multiple entry points

### Important Considerations
- Two-week intensive format requires significant time commitment
- Competitive application process - not all applicants may be accepted
- 12-month continuous recovery requirement is strictly enforced
- Strong emphasis on ethics (3 of 10 annual CEUs)
- Medicaid reimbursement opportunity increases employment prospects
- Limited training providers ensures quality but may limit geographic access
- Homework component requires commitment beyond classroom hours

### Best Practices for Applicants
1. Apply early when registration opens
2. Ensure 12 months continuous recovery before applying
3. Have all documentation ready (diploma/GED, recovery verification)
4. Be prepared for 2-week intensive commitment
5. Plan for daily homework in addition to class time
6. Contact George Mills early with questions
7. Connect with LaPAAC for peer support and mentorship
