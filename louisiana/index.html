Intensive 76-Hour Training Program

Louisiana offers one of the most comprehensive training programs in the nation - a 2-week, 76-hour intensive that includes Medicaid reimbursement opportunities.

Overview

The Louisiana Peer Support Specialist (PSS) credential is issued by the Louisiana Department of Health, Office of Behavioral Health. Certified peers are qualified providers for specific Medicaid services.

Basic Requirements

  • Age: Must be at least 18 years old
  • Education: Minimum of high school diploma or GED
  • Lived Experience: Must have lived experience with a behavioral health diagnosis (mental health, addiction, or co-occurring disorders)
  • Recovery Status: At least 12 months of continuous demonstrated recovery as indicated by SAMHSA's working definition of recovery

Training Requirements

Duration: 2 weeks, 76 hours total

Daily Schedule: 8 hours of class work daily plus several hours of homework

Format: Virtual or in-person at various locations around the state

Developer: Recovery Opportunity Center of Arizona

Frequency: Office of Behavioral Health provides minimum of 10 trainings per year

Application: Competitive process - capacity is limited

Continuing Education

Annual Requirement: 10 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) per calendar year

Ethics: 3 CEUs must be in the area of Ethics

Competencies: 7 CEUs in competencies related to tenets of peer support

  • Must maintain active certification to bill Medicaid

Key Advantages

  • Comprehensive Training: 76 hours is more thorough than many states
  • Medicaid Recognition: Certified peers can provide reimbursable services
  • Multiple Trainings: 10 trainings per year provides multiple entry points
  • Strong Foundation: 12-month continuous recovery requirement ensures stability
  • Ethics Emphasis: 3 of 10 annual CEUs must be in ethics
  • Dual Population: Serves both mental health and substance use peer support

Important Considerations

  • 2-Week Intensive: Requires significant time commitment
  • Competitive: Not all applicants may be accepted due to capacity limits
  • 12-Month Recovery: Strictly enforced continuous recovery requirement
  • Homework: Daily classroom hours plus evening homework
  • Apply Early: Limited capacity - first-come, first-served after meeting eligibility
