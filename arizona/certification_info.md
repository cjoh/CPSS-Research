# Arizona Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Names
- **Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS)** - for substance use disorder/addiction recovery
- **Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)** - national certification through IC&RC/ABCAC

## Certifying Bodies

### Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System (AHCCCS)
- **Role**: Recognizes and approves training programs
- **Website**: https://www.azahcccs.gov/AHCCCS/HealthcareAdvocacy/OIFA/credentialing.html
- **Peer Support Page**: https://www.azahcccs.gov/AHCCCS/HealthcareAdvocacy/OIFA/peersupport.html

### Arizona Board for Certification of Addiction Counselors (ABCAC)
- **Role**: Administers IC&RC Certified Peer Recovery Specialist exam
- **Application Fee**: $50
- **Exam Fee**: $150
- **Renewal Fee**: $100 (every 2 years)

## Certification Overview
A Peer and Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS) in Arizona is someone with lived experience of behavioral health and/or substance use recovery who has received specialized training. To be employed as a PRSS, individuals must complete an AHCCCS-approved training program and pass a competency exam.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
- Lived experience of behavioral health and/or substance use recovery
- Willing to self-identify as a person in recovery
- Willing to use personal recovery story to help others
- Specific recovery time requirements vary by training program

### Educational Requirements
- No specific degree requirement
- Ability to complete approved training program
- Strong communication and interpersonal skills

### Background Check
- May be required by employer or training program
- Check with specific training provider

## Training Requirements

### AHCCCS-Approved Training
- Training programs must be AHCCCS-recognized
- Many programs available across the state
- Training covers behavioral health AND substance abuse
- Some programs also focus on:
  - Criminal justice involvement
  - Opioid Use Disorder (OUD)
  - Co-occurring disorders
  - Specific populations (youth, veterans, families)

### Core Competencies
Training must meet competency standards established by SAMHSA and approved by AHCCCS, covering:
- Advocacy
- Peer support principles
- Recovery and wellness
- Mentoring and education
- Professional ethics and boundaries
- Cultural competency
- Trauma-informed care
- Self-care and wellness

## Application and Certification Process

### Step 1: Complete AHCCCS-Approved Training
1. Select an AHCCCS-approved training provider
2. Register and complete required training hours
3. Receive certificate of completion

### Step 2: Pass Competency Examination
**IC&RC Certified Peer Recovery Specialist Exam**
- Administered by Arizona Board for Certification of Addiction Counselors (ABCAC)
- Application fee: $50
- Exam fee: $150
- Apply through ABCAC website

### Step 3: Submit Application to ABCAC
1. Complete CPRS application
2. Submit training certificate
3. Pay application fee ($50)
4. Await approval

### Step 4: Take Certification Exam
1. Schedule exam after application approval
2. Pay exam fee ($150)
3. Pass IC&RC Certified Peer Recovery Specialist exam

### Step 5: Receive Certification
- Certificate issued by ABCAC
- Begin working as certified peer recovery specialist

## Application Fees

### Initial Certification Costs
- **Application Fee**: $50 (ABCAC)
- **Exam Fee**: $150 (ABCAC)
- **Training Costs**: Vary by provider (some offer free or subsidized training)

**Total Initial Cost**: $200 minimum (plus training fees if applicable)

## Renewal Requirements

### Recertification Period
**Every 2 years**

### Renewal Requirements
- **Renewal Fee**: $100
- **Continuing Education**: 20 hours, including:
  - 6 hours in ethics
  - 3 hours in cultural diversity
  - 11 hours in other approved topics

### Renewal Process
1. Complete required continuing education hours
2. Document all CE hours through ABCAC-approved providers
3. Submit renewal application
4. Pay renewal fee ($100)
5. Maintain certification in good standing

## Employment and Reimbursement
- Peer support services are Medicaid billable in Arizona
- AHCCCS covers peer support services as part of behavioral health benefits
- Employment opportunities in:
  - Behavioral health clinics
  - Substance use treatment facilities
  - Community health centers
  - Tribal health programs
  - Criminal justice diversion programs
  - Hospital-based programs

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become an AHCCCS-approved training provider:

### Step 1: Review AHCCCS Standards
- Visit: https://www.azahcccs.gov/AHCCCS/HealthcareAdvocacy/OIFA/credentialing.html
- Review peer support training requirements
- Ensure curriculum meets SAMHSA competency standards

### Step 2: Develop Curriculum
Required components:
- Core competency areas (advocacy, mentoring, recovery support, ethics)
- Evidence-based practices
- Trauma-informed approaches
- Cultural competency training
- Assessment and evaluation tools

### Step 3: Submit Application to AHCCCS
- Contact AHCCCS Office of Individual and Family Affairs (OIFA)
- Submit training curriculum for review
- Provide instructor qualifications
- Include evaluation methodology

### Step 4: Meet Quality Standards
- Qualified instructors with peer support expertise
- Appropriate training facilities (in-person or virtual)
- Valid assessment tools
- Certificate completion process

### Step 5: Receive Approval
- AHCCCS reviews and approves curriculum
- Organization added to approved provider list
- Begin offering training
- Report completions to AHCCCS

### Contact for Training Provider Application
**AHCCCS Office of Individual and Family Affairs (OIFA)**
- Website: https://www.azahcccs.gov/AHCCCS/HealthcareAdvocacy/OIFA/
- Check website for training provider application process

## Specialized Training Focus Areas

Many Arizona training programs offer specialized tracks:

### Criminal Justice Focus
- Supporting individuals with criminal justice involvement
- Reentry support and community integration
- Working with justice-involved populations

### Opioid Use Disorder (OUD) Specialization
- Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) support
- Overdose prevention and naloxone training
- Supporting long-term opioid recovery

### Co-Occurring Disorders
- Supporting individuals with mental health and substance use disorders
- Integrated treatment approaches
- Complex care coordination

### Tribal and Cultural Specialization
- Native American recovery approaches
- Cultural traditions in recovery
- Tribal community support
