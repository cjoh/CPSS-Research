Certification Overview

Arizona offers peer support specialist certification for substance use disorder recovery through AHCCCS-approved training programs. To be employed as a PRSS, individuals must complete an AHCCCS-approved training program and pass a competency exam.

Certifying Bodies

Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System (AHCCCS)
Recognizes and approves training programs
Website: AHCCCS Peer Support

Arizona Board for Certification of Addiction Counselors (ABCAC)
Administers IC&RC Certified Peer Recovery Specialist exam
Application Fee: $50 | Exam Fee: $150 | Renewal: $100 (every 2 years)

Requirements

  • Lived experience of behavioral health and/or substance use recovery
  • Complete AHCCCS-approved training program
  • Pass IC&RC Certified Peer Recovery Specialist exam

Training Standards

Training must meet SAMHSA competency standards covering:

  • Advocacy
  • Peer support principles
  • Recovery and wellness
  • Mentoring and education
  • Professional ethics and boundaries
  • Cultural competency
  • Trauma-informed care

Application Process

  1. Complete AHCCCS-approved training
  2. Apply to ABCAC with $50 application fee
  3. Schedule and pass IC&RC exam ($150)
  4. Receive CPRS certification and begin work

Renewal Requirements

Every 2 years - $100 renewal fee

  • 20 hours continuing education, including:
  • 6 hours in ethics
  • 3 hours in cultural diversity
  • 11 hours in other approved topics

Employment & Reimbursement

Peer support services are Medicaid billable in Arizona. AHCCCS covers peer support services as part of behavioral health benefits. Employment opportunities in behavioral health clinics, substance use treatment facilities, community health centers, tribal health programs, criminal justice diversion programs, and hospital-based programs.

Specialized Training Focus Areas

  • Criminal Justice Focus: Supporting justice-involved populations
  • Opioid Use Disorder (OUD): MAT support and overdose prevention
  • Co-Occurring Disorders: Integrated treatment approaches
  • Tribal & Cultural: Native American recovery approaches
