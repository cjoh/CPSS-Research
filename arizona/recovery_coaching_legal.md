# Arizona Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition of Coaching
**Status:** No legal definition found

"Recovery coach" is not defined in Arizona statute or regulations. Coaching generally is not a regulated industry in Arizona.

**Citation:** No specific statute

## Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary
**Unlicensed persons cannot:**
- Diagnose or treat mental health conditions
- Provide therapy or counseling requiring licensure
- Present themselves as licensed professionals

**Key Distinction:**
- Therapy is clinical recovery support (requires license)
- Coaching is peer recovery support (generally unregulated)

**Primary difference:** Licensed therapists need counseling degree and specialize in addiction therapy; recovery coaches don't need degree or certification.

**Citation:** Arizona Board of Behavioral Health Examiners rules (ARS Title 32, Chapter 32.1)

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** UNREGULATED - No prohibition on independent practice

**Key Points:**
- No certification required to practice as recovery coach
- No licensing requirement
- Can operate independent practice/LLC
- Must avoid activities requiring professional licensure

**Interventionists:** No separate regulatory framework found

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** NO
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Must avoid activities requiring licensure (diagnosis, therapy, clinical treatment)
- **Independent practice?** YES - unregulated
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Practical Implications
Arizona does not regulate recovery coaching. Coaches can practice independently but must avoid clinical practice requiring licensure.

**Best practices:**
- Maintain clear boundaries (coaching vs. therapy)
- Consider voluntary national certification (CCAR, IC&RC)
- Obtain professional liability insurance
- Provide clear disclosures about non-clinical nature of services
