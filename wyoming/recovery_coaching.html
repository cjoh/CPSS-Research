Regulatory Status Summary

Regulatory Status: COMPLETELY UNREGULATED - No state licensing, certification, registration, or oversight

Independent Practice: FULLY PERMITTED - No restrictions whatsoever

Coaching vs. Therapy: No statutory boundary defined; recovery coaches must self-regulate to avoid unauthorized practice

Legal Definition of Recovery Coaching

Status: NO SPECIFIC STATUTORY DEFINITION FOUND

Wyoming does not appear to have specific state licensing laws or regulatory boards that govern recovery coaches as a distinct professional category.

Related Regulatory Bodies:

  • Wyoming Mental Health Professions Licensing Board (for licensed mental health professionals)
  • Wyoming Department of Health, Behavioral Health Division
  • No specific recovery coach licensing board identified

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary

Key Risk

Without statutory framework, recovery coaches must be extremely careful not to engage in activities constituting unauthorized practice of licensed professions.

Licensed Professional Framework: Wyoming regulates licensed mental health professionals through Wyoming Mental Health Professions Licensing Board and various licensing boards for specific professions.

Recovery Coach Status: No specific statutory scope of practice found for recovery coaches. Not included in Wyoming mental health professional licensing framework.

Independent Practice Requirements

Advantages of Completely Unregulated Status

  • NO state certification fees or requirements
  • NO mandatory training or education requirements
  • NO examination requirements
  • NO supervision mandates
  • Complete flexibility in practice structure
  • Can start practicing immediately
  • Can set own qualifications and standards
  • NO renewal processes
  • NO continuing education requirements
  • NO state bureaucracy or oversight

Risks of Completely Unregulated Status

  • NO legal protection of "recovery coach" title (anyone can use it)
  • NO regulatory guidance on scope of practice
  • NO state oversight or accountability framework
  • Significant liability exposure without regulatory structure
  • NO recourse to state board if disputes arise
  • Must completely self-determine ethical boundaries
  • Must avoid unauthorized practice of licensed professions on your own
  • NO standardized qualifications for consumers to evaluate
  • NO state-backed credentialing for professional credibility
  • Consumers have no regulatory protection

Basic Training Requirements (Voluntary)

Training and certification requirements vary depending on organization offering certification. No college degree required, but specialized training and experience are required. Requirements for enrollment typically include:

  • High school diploma
  • Lived experience of mental health or substance use disorders
  • Over age of 18

Scope of Practice

DO NOT (Unauthorized Practice)

  • Diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Provide psychotherapy or clinical counseling
  • Develop clinical treatment plans
  • Conduct clinical assessments
  • Prescribe or recommend medications
  • Present yourself as licensed professional
  • Engage in practice requiring professional license

DO (Recovery Coaching Activities)

  • Provide peer support based on lived experience
  • Offer recovery navigation and coaching
  • Share your recovery journey
  • Connect clients to appropriate resources
  • Refer to licensed professionals for clinical needs
  • Maintain clear non-clinical boundaries

Critical Recommendations

Ethical Imperative

The ABSENCE of regulation increases (not decreases) practitioners' ethical responsibility. You must impose on yourself the standards that the state does not require.

Despite Lack of State Requirements, You Should:

  • Obtain Voluntary Training and Certification: Complete comprehensive training and consider national certifications (CARC, NCPRSS, etc.)
  • Form LLC or Business Entity: CRITICAL for liability protection
  • Get Professional Liability Insurance: ABSOLUTELY ESSENTIAL in unregulated environment
  • Develop Comprehensive Contracts: Clear service agreements and policies
  • Create Code of Ethics: Establish your own professional standards
  • Provide Informed Consent: Explain you are NOT a licensed professional
  • Establish Referral Network: Connections with licensed professionals
  • Engage in Continuing Education: Even though not required

Employment Outlook

Employment opportunities exist across various settings. Over next ten years, expect growth of roughly 23% for rehabilitation (recovery) coaches in Wyoming. Can earn $40,000 or more annually.

Important Warnings

  1. "Anyone Can Call Themselves a Recovery Coach": With no title protection, consumers cannot distinguish qualified coaches from unqualified ones.
  2. No Safety Net: If disputes arise, there is no regulatory board to provide guidance, mediate complaints, or protect consumers.
  3. Liability is Personal: Without regulatory framework, you bear full personal liability. LLC and insurance are CRITICAL.
  4. Unauthorized Practice Risk Remains: Even though recovery coaching is unregulated, crossing into licensed professional territory is still illegal.
  5. Consumer Vulnerability: Without state oversight, consumers have minimal protection from unqualified or unethical practitioners.

Bottom Line for Wyoming

Maximum freedom requires maximum responsibility. Without state regulation to guide and protect, recovery coaches in Wyoming must create their own professional standards, protect themselves and their clients through voluntary measures, and bear full personal responsibility for the quality and safety of their services.
