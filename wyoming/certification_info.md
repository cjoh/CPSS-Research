# Wyoming - Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) Certification

## Overview
Wyoming's peer specialist certification is managed by Recover Wyoming, which serves as Wyoming's member board for the International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC) for the Peer Recovery credential. Effective February 1, 2021, all Wyoming Peer Specialists are required to become certified through the IC&RC system.

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Specialist (CPS)** - Wyoming Peer Recovery Certification through IC&RC

## IC&RC Affiliation
**International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC)**
- Sets international standards for peer recovery certification
- Provides exam and baseline credentialing standards
- Enables reciprocity across member boards
- Website: https://internationalcredentialing.org

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements
1. **Personal Recovery**: Must have lived experience of substance use disorder and be in recovery
2. **Education**: High school diploma or GED (standard requirement for IC&RC credentials)
3. **Age**: Must be 18 years or older (standard requirement)
4. **Recovery Status**: Must demonstrate sustained recovery
5. **Training**: Complete IC&RC-approved peer specialist training (46 hours required)
6. **Examination**: Pass the IC&RC Peer Recovery exam

### Wyoming-Specific Requirements
- Wyoming residents preferred but may not be strictly required
- Contact Recover Wyoming for specific Wyoming requirements
- Must meet IC&RC minimum standards plus any Wyoming additions

## Scope of Practice

### What Wyoming Certified Peer Specialists Do
- Provide recovery support based on lived experience
- Share personal recovery journey appropriately
- Model recovery principles and practices
- Support individuals in their recovery journey
- Connect individuals to recovery resources and community
- Facilitate peer support groups and activities
- Advocate for individuals in recovery
- Promote hope, empowerment, and self-determination

### Practice Settings
Peer specialists work in various settings including:
- Substance use disorder treatment programs
- Recovery community organizations
- Behavioral health centers
- Peer-run organizations
- Recovery housing programs
- Hospital settings
- Correctional facilities (with forensic training)
- Community outreach programs

### Professional Responsibilities
- Maintain ethical standards per IC&RC Code of Ethics
- Practice within defined scope of peer support
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Participate in supervision (if required)
- Complete continuing education
- Model recovery and wellness
- Respect confidentiality

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Who do I contact first about certification?
**A:** Contact Recover Wyoming at (307) 421-7261 or visit their website at recoverwyoming.org.

### Q: How long does the certification process take?
**A:** Varies depending on training schedule and exam availability. Contact Recover Wyoming for current timelines.

### Q: Can I take training outside Wyoming?
**A:** Possibly, but verify with Recover Wyoming that the training is IC&RC-approved and will be accepted for Wyoming certification.

### Q: What if I'm certified in another IC&RC state?
**A:** Contact Recover Wyoming about reciprocity and endorsement options. You may not need to retake training or exam.

### Q: Is there financial assistance for training?
**A:** Check with Recover Wyoming about scholarship opportunities or payment plans. Some employers may cover training costs.

### Q: Can I work as a peer specialist before certification?
**A:** Requirements vary by employer. Some may allow work under supervision while pursuing certification. Check with potential employers and Recover Wyoming.

### Q: What's the difference between peer specialist and recovery coach?
**A:** Certified Peer Specialist is a formal credential through IC&RC. Recovery coach may be a less formal role. Contact Recover Wyoming for clarification.

### Q: Do I need to live in Wyoming to get certified?
**A:** Contact Recover Wyoming for residency requirements. As Wyoming's member board, they certify for Wyoming practice.
