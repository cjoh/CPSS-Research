Official Certification: CPSS (Mental Health) & Peer Recovery Coach (SUD)

Certification Overview

Idaho offers two main certification pathways: CPSS for mental health (or co-occurring disorders) and Certified Peer Recovery Coach for substance use disorders.

Certifying Bodies

For Mental Health (CPSS):
Idaho Department of Health and Welfare - Partnership with BPA Health
Website: Idaho DHW

For Substance Use (Peer Recovery Coach):
Idaho Board of Alcohol/Drug Counselor Certification, INC. (IBADCC)
Provides Certified Peer Recovery Coach certification

Requirements for CPSS (Mental Health Focus)

  • Mental health diagnosis (or mental health + substance use disorder)
  • At least one year of ongoing recovery
  • High school diploma or GED
  • 40 hours of training
  • 20 hours of supervision and 100-200 hours of work/volunteer experience
  • 10 hours of continuing education each year with at least 1 hour in Ethics

Training & Certification Process

BPA Health

Through partnership with Idaho Department of Health and Welfare, BPA Health helps those with lived experience apply for and renew Peer Support Specialist certification

Recovery Coach Certification (Substance Use Focus)

Provider: Idaho Board of Alcohol/Drug Counselor Certification (IBADCC)

Recertification: Every two years - 20 hours of continuing education in IBADCC-approved program

Application Process

  1. Determine appropriate pathway (CPSS for mental health/co-occurring or Recovery Coach for SUD)
  2. Meet lived experience and recovery time requirements
  3. Complete required training (40 hours for CPSS)
  4. Gain supervised work/volunteer experience
  5. Apply through BPA Health (for CPSS) or IBADCC (for Recovery Coach)
  6. Receive certification and begin practice
BPA Health Certification Peer Support Training

Employment Opportunities

Employment opportunities in community mental health centers, substance use treatment programs, integrated behavioral health services, hospital-based programs, and community recovery organizations.
