Tennessee Recovery Coaching Legal Information

Research Date: November 5, 2025

Back to State Index Peer Support Certification Guide

CRITICAL: Tennessee is one of the MOST RESTRICTIVE states for recovery coaching

INDEPENDENT PRACTICE EXPLICITLY PROHIBITED

It is a VIOLATION OF THE CPRS CODE OF ETHICS to provide peer recovery services as a private practice in Tennessee. CPRS are NEVER authorized to provide peer recovery services on their own for pay or for reimbursement from a health plan.

Legal Definition

Status: Explicitly defined by Tennessee Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services (TDMHSAS)

Definition: Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) is "a person who has lived experience of a mental illness, substance use disorder or co-occurring disorder, who has made the journey from illness to wellness, and who now wishes to help others."

Official Program: State certification program provides State certification for individuals who provide direct peer-to-peer support services.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

STRICTLY PROHIBITED Activities

  • Clinical assessment or diagnosis
  • Treatment planning (clinical)
  • Psychotherapy of any kind
  • Any clinical interventions requiring professional license
  • Providing services from home or personal office
  • Offering services independently for pay
  • Individual billing to TennCare (Medicaid)

Permitted Activities

  • Peer support services (through licensed agency only)
  • Recovery navigation under supervision
  • Sharing lived experience within agency settings
  • Supporting others in recovery journey as agency employee

MANDATORY Requirements

Certification Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years of age
  • High school diploma or GED
  • Self-identify as person in recovery from mental illness, substance abuse, or co-occurring disorder
  • Demonstrated self-directed recovery for MINIMUM OF 24 CONSECUTIVE MONTHS (2 years)
  • Complete 40 hours of training (offered in-person and online)
  • Complete minimum of 75 hours of supervised paid or volunteer work within past year providing peer recovery services
  • No less than 3 hours of supervision (one hour for every 25 hours of service provided) from approved supervisor

EXPLICIT PROHIBITIONS

1. Cannot Provide Services from Home or Office

It is a VIOLATION OF THE CPRS CODE OF ETHICS to provide peer recovery services as a private practice.

2. Cannot Offer Services Independently for Pay

CPRS are NEVER authorized to provide peer recovery services on their own for pay or for reimbursement from a health plan. Doing so constitutes UNETHICAL BEHAVIOR and a VIOLATION OF THE SCOPE OF ACTIVITIES.

3. Must Work for Approved Agencies

  • CPRS CANNOT offer services on their own for pay or reimbursement
  • MUST be employed by an agency that is licensed by TDMHSAS
  • MUST work under direct clinical supervision of a licensed behavioral health professional

4. Cannot Bill Individually

  • CPRS can provide Medicaid-reimbursable services in outpatient and inpatient programs UNDER THE EMPLOYER'S CONTRACT with health plans
  • Individual CPRS CANNOT bill TennCare (Medicaid) directly

Path to Practice in Tennessee

Required Steps

  1. Meet 2-year recovery requirement
  2. Complete 40 hours of training
  3. Obtain certification from TDMHSAS
  4. Seek employment with TDMHSAS-licensed agency
  5. Work under supervision of licensed professional
  6. Provide services through agency only

CANNOT Create:

  • Independent private practice
  • LLC for recovery coaching services
  • Home-based coaching business
  • Independent contracting arrangements

Continuing Education

Must complete at least 10 hours of continuing education per certification year. All continuing education must be approved by TDMHSAS.

Important Note

Tennessee's regulations are unusually explicit and restrictive. Anyone attempting to provide recovery coaching services independently in Tennessee would be in direct violation of state regulations and the CPRS Code of Ethics. The only legal path is employment through a TDMHSAS-licensed agency with proper supervision.
