Certification Overview

Tennessee offers three peer support certifications managed through the CADRE portal (Career Development for Peer Recovery Specialists): CPRS (Certified Peer Recovery Specialist), CYAPSS (Certified Young Adult Peer Support Specialists), and CFSS (Certified Family Support Specialist).

  • CPRS - Certified Peer Recovery Specialist
  • CYAPSS - Certified Young Adult Peer Support Specialists
  • CFSS - Certified Family Support Specialist

Requirements for CPRS Certification

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years old
  • Hold a high school diploma or GED
  • Self-identify as a person in recovery from mental illness, substance abuse, or co-occurring disorder
  • Have demonstrated self-directed recovery for a minimum of 24 consecutive months
  • Cannot have a conservator (this contraindicates certification)

Training Requirements

  • Must attend and successfully complete 40-hour CPRS training
  • Includes role playing, feedback, group work, self-examination, and tests
  • Topics covered: communication, problem-solving, values, motivation, wellness, opioid use disorder, and ethics

Work Experience Requirements

  • Must complete a minimum of 75 hours of supervised paid or volunteer work within the past year providing peer recovery services
  • No less than 3 hours of supervision (one hour for every 25 hours of service)

Application Process

  1. Complete 40-Hour Training: Attend and successfully complete CPRS training program
  2. Gain Work Experience: Complete 75 hours of supervised paid or volunteer work
  3. Register in CADRE Portal: Create account at cadre.tn.gov
  4. Submit Application: Complete certification application with required documentation
  5. Receive Certification: Obtain CPRS certification from Tennessee Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services

Fees and Costs

Contact Tennessee Office of Consumer Affairs for current fee schedule and training costs.

Renewal Requirements

  • Renewal Period: Every two years
  • Continuing Education: 20 hours per two-year certification period
  • Ethics Training: Minimum of 2 hours in ethics per two-year period
  • Approval: All continuing education must be approved by TDMHSAS
