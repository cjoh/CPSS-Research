Official Certification for Substance Use: Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC)

Note: Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) is a separate credential for mental health peer support

Important: Two Separate Peer Support Pathways

Massachusetts has distinct certification pathways:

  • Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC) - For substance use/addiction (focus of this page)
  • Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) - For mental health peer support

Certification Overview

A Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC) in Massachusetts is a person with personal lived experience of substance use disorder and recovery who has been trained to use their experience to support others in their recovery journey. Both mental health and substance use peer services are Medicaid-reimbursable in Massachusetts.

Certifying Body for Substance Use Peer Support

Massachusetts Board of Substance Abuse Counselor Certification (MBSACC)
Issues the Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC) credential
Website: www.mbsacc.com
Bureau of Substance Addiction Services (BSAS)
Primary regulatory body for substance use disorder services
Oversees recovery coach training programs
Provides Medicaid reimbursement for certified recovery coaches

Requirements

Personal Requirements

  • Lived Experience: Personal experience with substance use disorder and recovery
  • Recovery Status: Must be in recovery (specific duration varies by program)
  • Education: High school diploma or equivalent typically required

Training Requirements

  • Evidence-based recovery coach curriculum
  • Peer support principles and practices
  • Ethics and boundaries
  • Recovery-oriented systems of care
  • Trauma-informed approaches
  • Cultural competency

Primary Training Provider

Recovery Education Collaborative (REC)

Status: Commonwealth's official provider of Recovery Coach trainings
Role: Provides BSAS-approved recovery coach training
Format: In-person and online options available
Contact: Check with BSAS for current REC training schedule

Academic Pathway Option

Northern Essex Community College (NECC)

Peer Recovery Specialist Certificate Program
Type: 16-credit certificate program
Website: NECC Program
Content: Addiction-recovery cycle, 12-step and self-help organizations, community resources, relapse prevention, coping skills
Benefit: Academic credentials plus certification preparation

Application Process

1

Complete Prerequisites

Verify lived experience and recovery status requirements

2

Enroll in Training

Complete BSAS-approved recovery coach training through REC or approved provider

3

Complete Requirements

Finish all training hours and pass certification examination (if required)

4

Apply & Receive Credential

Submit application to MBSACC and receive CARC credential

Visit MBSACC NECC Program

National Certification Option

National Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (NCPRSS)

Certifying Body: NAADAC, the Association of Addiction Professionals

Status: Gold standard in peer support professional certifications

Recognition: Nationally recognized credential with portability across states

Medicaid Reimbursement

Massachusetts recognizes both mental health and substance use disorder peer services as Medicaid-reimbursable. Certified Addictions Recovery Coaches can provide Medicaid-reimbursable services in qualified settings.

Mental Health Peer Support (Reference)

Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) - Mental Health

Training Provider: Transformation Center/Kiva Centers

Duration: 60-hour, ten-day course

Oversight: Department of Mental Health (DMH)

Focus: Mental health peer support (separate from substance use certification)

Note: This is a different pathway from CARC. Some individuals may pursue both certifications.
