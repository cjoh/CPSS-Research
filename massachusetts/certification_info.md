# Massachusetts Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Names

Massachusetts has **two separate peer support pathways:**

1. **Certified Peer Specialist (CPS)** - For Mental Health
2. **Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC)** - For Substance Use/Addiction

This document focuses on substance use peer support.

## Certifying Bodies

### For Substance Use Disorder Peer Support
**Massachusetts Board of Substance Abuse Counselor Certification (MBSACC)**
- Issues the **Certified Addictions Recovery Coach (CARC)** credential
- Website: https://www.mbsacc.com

**Bureau of Substance Addiction Services (BSAS)**
- Primary regulatory body for substance use disorder services in Massachusetts
- Oversees recovery coach training programs
- Provides Medicaid reimbursement for certified recovery coaches

### For Mental Health Peer Support (Reference)
**Massachusetts Department of Mental Health (DMH)**
- Oversees mental health peer specialist certification
- Separate pathway from substance use certification

## Personal Requirements

### For CARC (Substance Use)
- **Lived Experience:** Personal experience with substance use disorder and recovery
- **Recovery Status:** Must be in recovery (specific duration varies by program)
- **Education:** High school diploma or equivalent typically required
- **Background:** Personal lived experience is primary qualification

## Training Requirements

### Substance Use Recovery Coach Training

**Training Provider:**
- **Recovery Education Collaborative (REC)** - Commonwealth's new provider of Recovery Coach trainings

**Training Components:**
- Evidence-based recovery coach curriculum
- Peer support principles and practices
- Ethics and boundaries
- Recovery-oriented systems of care
- Trauma-informed approaches
- Cultural competency

**Format:**
- In-person and online options available
- Check with REC for current format offerings

## Certification Process

1. Complete prerequisite requirements (lived experience, recovery status)
2. Enroll in BSAS-approved recovery coach training
3. Complete all training hours and requirements
4. Pass certification examination (if required)
5. Submit application to MBSACC
6. Receive CARC credential

## Alternative Educational Pathway

### Northern Essex Community College (NECC)
**Peer Recovery Specialist Certificate Program**
- **Type:** 16-credit certificate program
- **Content:** Key recovery concepts including:
  - Addiction-recovery cycle
  - 12-step and other self-help recovery organizations
  - Community resources
  - Relapse prevention techniques
  - Coping skills training
- **Website:** https://www.necc.mass.edu/academics/credit-programs/human-services/peer-recovery-specialist-certificate/
- **Benefit:** Academic credentials plus certification preparation

## National Certification Option

### National Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (NCPRSS)
- **Certifying Body:** NAADAC, the Association of Addiction Professionals
- **Status:** Gold standard in peer support professional certifications
- **Recognition:** Nationally recognized credential
- **Benefit:** Portability across states

## Medicaid Reimbursement

Massachusetts has both:
- Mental Health (MH) peer certification with Medicaid reimbursement
- Substance Use Disorder (SUD) peer certification with Medicaid reimbursement

Certified Addictions Recovery Coaches can provide Medicaid-reimbursable services in qualified settings.

## How to Become a Training Provider

### For Substance Use Recovery Coach Training

**Contact:**
- Bureau of Substance Addiction Services (BSAS)
- Submit proposal for training program approval
- Must meet BSAS standards for recovery coach training
- Curriculum must cover required competencies

**Current System:**
- REC serves as primary training provider
- Limited number of approved providers ensures quality control
- Must align with BSAS recovery-oriented systems of care principles

## Distinction Between MH and SUD Peer Support

**Important:** Massachusetts maintains separate certification pathways:

**Mental Health (CPS):**
- Certified through DMH process
- 60-hour training through Transformation Center/Kiva Centers
- Focus on mental health recovery

**Substance Use (CARC):**
- Certified through MBSACC
- Training through Recovery Education Collaborative
- Focus on addiction recovery

**Co-Occurring:**
- Some individuals may pursue both certifications
- Working with co-occurring disorders may require both credentials or specialized training

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Massachusetts' Program
- **Dual System:** Separate pathways for mental health and substance use peer support
- **State-Designated Providers:** Commonwealth contracts with specific organizations (REC, Transformation Center)
- **Medicaid Recognition:** Both MH and SUD peer services are Medicaid-reimbursable
- **Academic Option:** NECC offers credit-bearing certificate program
- **National Option:** NCPRSS available for national recognition
- **Recovery-Oriented:** Strong emphasis on recovery-oriented systems of care

### Important Considerations
- **Know Your Path:** Clarify whether you want MH, SUD, or both certifications
- **Different Requirements:** MH and SUD have different training providers and hours
- **Medicaid Billing:** Certification enables Medicaid reimbursement
- **State Contracts:** Limited providers due to state contracting system
- **Academic Credits:** NECC program offers traditional college credits
- **National Portability:** Consider NCPRSS for work across state lines

### Best Practices for Applicants

**For Substance Use Peer Support (CARC):**
1. Contact Recovery Education Collaborative (REC) for training
2. Verify current recovery requirements
3. Check MBSACC for certification application process
4. Consider national NCPRSS certification for portability
5. Connect with BSAS for employment opportunities
6. Network with recovery community organizations

**For Mental Health Peer Support (CPS):**
1. Contact Transformation Center or Kiva Centers
2. Plan for 10-day (60-hour) intensive training
3. Connect with DMH for certification process
4. Explore employment with community mental health centers

**For Both Pathways:**
1. Verify Medicaid provider enrollment if seeking reimbursement
2. Consider academic program at NECC for enhanced credentials
3. Join professional peer support organizations
4. Attend conferences and continuing education events
5. Connect with experienced peers for mentorship

### Unique Aspects
- **Dual Certification System:** Unique separation of MH and SUD peer pathways
- **State-Contracted Training:** Limited providers ensure quality control
- **Academic Integration:** College credit option through NECC
- **Strong Medicaid Support:** Both pathways have reimbursement
- **Historical Leadership:** Massachusetts has long history of peer support services
- **Urban/Rural Options:** Training available across the state
