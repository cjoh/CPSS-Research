Official Certifications: Certified Peer Support Specialist I (CPSS I) & Certified Peer Support Specialist II (CPSS II)

Certification Overview

North Dakota offers two levels of peer support certification for individuals with lived experience of mental health disorders, substance use disorders, traumatic brain injury, or combination thereof, as well as for family members with lived experience supporting individuals with these conditions.

Certifying Body

North Dakota Health and Human Services (HHS)
Behavioral Health Division
Email: peersupport@nd.gov
Website: hhs.nd.gov/behavioral-health/peer-support/certification

Requirements

Eligibility Requirements

  • Age: Over 18 years of age
  • Residency: Living or working in North Dakota
  • Lived Experience: Personal lived experience with mental health, substance use disorder, traumatic brain injury, or any combination thereof OR family member with lived experience supporting an individual with these conditions
  • Must be willing to publicly identify as being in recovery or as a family member supporting recovery

Training Requirements

Total Hours: 40 hours

Duration: 5 days (8:30 AM - 5:00 PM daily)

Attendance: Full attendance required for successful completion

Locations: Fargo, Minot, Grand Forks, and Jamestown

Topics Include: Peer support principles and practices, recovery-oriented services, ethical considerations, communication and relationship building skills, self-care and boundaries, documentation and professionalism

Application Requirements

  • Completed division-approved peer support training (40 hours)
  • Three letters of recommendation:
    • One personal reference
    • One professional reference
    • One demonstrating commitment to recovery
  • Personal statement
  • Application fee ($50)

Certification Levels

Certified Peer Support Specialist I (CPSS I)

A peer support specialist who has completed division-approved peer support training and completed the certification process.

Certified Peer Support Specialist II (CPSS II)

A peer support specialist who has:

  • Provided a minimum of 1,500 hours of direct service as a peer support specialist
  • Completed peer support supervisor training
  • May serve as a supervisor for CPSS I specialists

Application Process

1

Complete Training

Complete 40 hours of division-approved peer support training

2

Gather Materials

Obtain three letters of recommendation and write personal statement

3

Submit Application

Submit complete application with all required documents and $50 fee to ND HHS Behavioral Health Division

4

Application Review

Applications are processed within 30 business days. Certification fee reimbursed if application not received within 60 days

Apply for Certification View Training Schedule

Renewal Requirements

Renewal Period: Every two years

Continuing Education: 20 hours during each two-year certification period

Ethics Requirement: Minimum 2 hours must be on the topic of peer support specialist ethics

Submit recertification application with documentation of continuing education hours before expiration date.
