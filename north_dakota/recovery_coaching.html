Legal Status Overview

Independent Practice Permitted

Recognition: Recognized by North Dakota State Board of Addiction Counseling (July 2016)

Regulatory Status: State certification program (not statutory licensure)

Independent Practice: Permitted - Can establish private practice

Legal Definition

"Recovery coaches are not therapists or mental health professionals but have direct, personal experience with addiction, mental health conditions, and recovery."

Recognition: North Dakota State Board of Addiction Counseling recognizes recovery coaches alongside addiction counselor trainees (ACT), licensed addiction counselors (LAC), and master addiction counselors (MAC) as of July 2016.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Recovery Coaches

  • Explicitly NOT therapists or mental health professionals
  • Based on lived experience with addiction, mental health conditions, and recovery
  • Provide peer support and recovery navigation
  • Operate under certification framework (not licensure)

Licensed Addiction Counselors (LAC) / Master Addiction Counselors (MAC)

  • Separate regulated professions under North Dakota law
  • Require statutory licensing
  • Authorized for clinical practice
  • Different scope of practice from recovery coaches

Key Distinction

Distinction based on lived experience vs. clinical training/licensure. Recovery coaches operate under certification framework separate from statutory licensing of addiction counselors.

Independent Practice Requirements

Certification Requirements

  • Meet basic eligibility requirements
  • Complete mandatory training program from approved providers:
    • University of North Dakota
    • Center for Addiction Recovery (CCAR)
    • Federation of Families
  • Participate in supervised hours of peer support work
  • Submit $50 application fee
  • Submit proof of completed peer support training
  • Provide three letters of recommendation
  • Submit personal statement about lived experience with recovery
  • Abide by peer support Code of Ethics

Recertification

  • Must be recertified every two years
  • Requires at least 20 hours of Continuing Education Units (CEU)

Independent Practice Status

PERMITTED - Can Establish Private Practice

Employment opportunities span from independent practice to collaborative roles in healthcare teams

  • Recovery coaches can operate their own private practice
  • Can command fees up to $150 hourly
  • No explicit prohibition on independent practice found

Scope of Practice

Important Distinctions

  • Recovery coaches ≠ Licensed Addiction Counselors (LAC)
  • Recovery coaches ≠ Master Addiction Counselors (MAC)
  • Recovery coaches ≠ Therapists or mental health professionals
  • Based on lived experience, not clinical training

Important Considerations

Practical Considerations for Independent Practice

  • Maintain clear documentation of non-clinical services
  • Adhere to peer support Code of Ethics
  • Maintain certification through biennial renewal
  • Complete required continuing education (20 hours every 2 years)
  • Establish appropriate referral relationships with licensed professionals
  • Consider professional liability insurance
  • Clearly communicate scope of services to clients

Recommendation

While independent practice is permitted, recovery coaches should maintain clear boundaries regarding clinical services and establish protocols for referring clients to licensed professionals when clinical needs arise.
