# North Dakota Peer Support Specialist Certification Information

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Support Specialist I (CPSS I)** and **Certified Peer Support Specialist II (CPSS II)**

## Certifying Body
**North Dakota Health and Human Services (HHS)**
- Behavioral Health Division
- Website: https://www.hhs.nd.gov/behavioral-health/peer-support/certification
- Email: peersupport@nd.gov
- Phone: Contact through email for most up-to-date contact information

## Certification Overview
North Dakota offers two levels of peer support certification for individuals with lived experience of mental health disorders, substance use disorders, traumatic brain injury, or combination thereof, as well as for family members with lived experience supporting individuals with these conditions.

## Detailed Requirements

### Eligibility Requirements
To qualify for certification, you must:
- Be **over 18 years of age**
- Be **living or working in North Dakota**
- Have completed education and training requirements
- Self-identify as either:
  - **Option A:** An individual with personal lived experience willing to publicly identify as being in recovery from a mental health disorder, substance use disorder, traumatic brain injury, or any combination thereof
  - **Option B:** A family member with personal lived experience with an individual with mental health disorder, substance use disorder, traumatic brain injury, or any combination thereof

### Training Requirements

#### Initial Peer Support Training
- **40 hours** of peer support specialist training
- Delivered over **5 days** (8:30 AM - 5:00 PM daily)
- **Full attendance required** for successful completion
- Available in 4 locations across the state: Fargo, Minot, Grand Forks, and Jamestown

#### Training Curriculum
The training program covers essential peer support competencies including:
- Peer support principles and practices
- Recovery-oriented services
- Ethical considerations in peer support
- Communication and relationship building skills
- Self-care and boundaries
- Documentation and professionalism

### Application Requirements
- Completed division-approved peer support training (40 hours)
- **Three letters of recommendation:**
  - One personal reference
  - One professional reference
  - One demonstrating commitment to recovery
- Personal statement
- Application fee payment

### Background Check
- May be required by individual employers or service settings
- Check with specific employers for their requirements

## Certification Levels

### Certified Peer Support Specialist I (CPSS I)
A peer support specialist who has completed division-approved peer support training and completed the certification process.

### Certified Peer Support Specialist II (CPSS II)
A peer support specialist who has:
- Provided a **minimum of 1,500 hours** of direct service as a peer support specialist
- Completed **peer support supervisor training**
- May serve as a supervisor for CPSS I specialists

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Training
Complete 40 hours of division-approved peer support training

### Step 2: Gather Application Materials
- Obtain three letters of recommendation
- Write personal statement
- Collect training completion certificate

### Step 3: Submit Application
Submit complete application with all required documents and fee to ND HHS Behavioral Health Division

### Step 4: Application Review
- Applications are processed within **30 business days**
- Notification sent upon completion of review
- Certification fee will be reimbursed if application is not received within 60 days of receiving the fee

## Application Fees
- **Application Fee:** $50
- **Certification Fee:** Contact peersupport@nd.gov for current fee information

## Recertification/Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
Certifications must be renewed every **two years**

### Continuing Education Requirements
- **20 hours** of continuing education during each two-year certification period
- **Minimum 2 hours** must be on the topic of peer support specialist ethics

### Recertification Process
Submit recertification application with documentation of continuing education hours before expiration date

### Recertification Resources
- Visit: https://www.hhs.nd.gov/peer-support/recertification
- Contact: peersupport@nd.gov

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
To become an approved training provider or to have your training program approved by the North Dakota Behavioral Health Division, you must work with ND HHS.

### Application Process
**Contact Information:** peersupport@nd.gov

### Requirements for Training Approval
If you have developed a peer support training program and wish to have it approved:
1. Contact the Behavioral Health Division at peersupport@nd.gov
2. Submit curriculum objectives and training materials for review
3. Demonstrate alignment with North Dakota's peer support competencies
4. Wait for division approval before advertising training as approved for ND certification

### Training Standards
- Must be minimum **40 hours** in length
- Must cover all required peer support competencies
- Must include appropriate evaluation and completion criteria
- Full attendance must be required for successful completion
