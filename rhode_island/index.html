Certification Overview

Rhode Island's Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) credential is designed for individuals with lived experience who want to support others in recovery. Certified Peer Recovery Specialists are credentialed by the Rhode Island Certification Board.

Rhode Island Certification Board

Website: ricertboard.org/certified-peer-recovery-specialist-cprs

Requirements

Education Requirements

  • Minimum high school diploma/GED from an accredited school (veterans may provide discharge documentation in lieu of high school diploma/GED)

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • Two years of sustained and continuous recovery time
  • Personal lived experience with a substance use and/or mental health disorder, or as a family member or loved one of a person with such disorders

Training Requirements

  • 46 hours of education/training specific to CPRS Domains:
    • 10 hours in Advocacy
    • 10 hours in Mentoring/Education
    • 10 hours in Recovery/Wellness Support
    • 16 hours in Ethical Responsibility

Work Experience Requirements

  • 500 hours of volunteer or paid experience specific to peer recovery services
  • 25 supervised hours specific to the domains (may be included in the total experience requirement)

Examination

  • Pass the IC&RC Examination for Peer Recovery Specialists
  • Computer-based exam with 75 multiple-choice questions
  • Offered on an on-demand basis at approved testing sites

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete 46-hour CPRS training through approved provider
  2. Gain Work Experience: Complete 500 hours of volunteer or paid experience (including 25 supervised hours)
  3. Submit Application: Apply to Rhode Island Certification Board
  4. Pass Examination: Take and pass IC&RC Examination
  5. Receive Certification: Obtain CPRS certification from Rhode Island Certification Board
