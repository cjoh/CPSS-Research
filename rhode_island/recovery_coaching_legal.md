# Rhode Island Recovery Coaching Legal Research

**Research Date:** November 5, 2025

## Legal Definition of Recovery Coaching

**Status:** Defined through certification board and state standards

**Definition:** Peer Recovery Specialists (or Recovery Coaches) are current or former consumers of behavioral health services who are trained and certified to offer support and assistance to those in the recovery and community-integration process.

**Official Term:** "Certified Peer Recovery Specialist" credentialed by Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB).

**Citation:** Rhode Island Department of Behavioral Healthcare, Developmental Disabilities and Hospitals (BHDDH) Provider Certification Standards; RICB standards

## Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary

**Scope of Practice:**
- Provide support and assistance in recovery and community-integration process
- Work in wide range of settings: community mental health centers, behavioral health programs, substance use treatment facilities, peer-run organizations, community based organizations, emergency rooms, courts, homeless shelters, and outreach programs
- Based on lived experience as consumers of behavioral health services
- **Specific statutory boundaries not detailed in search results**

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Certification administered by Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB)
- Standards developed and administered by third-party, non-profit certification provider
- Distinct from licensed clinical professionals regulated under Title 5 (health and safety) and Title 216 (Department of Health regulations)

**Citation:** RICB certification standards; RI BHDDH oversight

## Independent Practice Requirements

**Certification Authority:**
- Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB) - "dedicated to public protection by offering competency-based credentials to professionals"
- Establishes, administers, and monitors credentials for professionals working with addictions, behavioral health, community health, and prevention

**Certification Requirements:**
- Must complete Peer Based Recovery Support Services (PBRSS) training and certification
- Credentialed by Rhode Island Certification Board
- **Specific training hours, examination requirements, and eligibility criteria not detailed in search results**

**Recent Standards:**
- Rhode Island recently issued Provider Certification Standards for Peer Based Recovery Support Services (PBRSS)
- Details not available in search results

**Citation:** RI BHDDH Provider Certification Standards (212-RICR-10-10-1); RICB credentialing

## Legal Citations

**Regulatory Framework:**
- **212-RICR-10-10-1** - Rhode Island Code of Regulations, Department of Behavioral Healthcare, Developmental Disabilities and Hospitals (BHDDH) rules
- **No specific statute found** in Rhode Island General Laws governing recovery coaches

**Certification Authority:**
- Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB) - non-governmental credentialing body
- Rhode Island Department of Behavioral Healthcare, Developmental Disabilities and Hospitals (BHDDH) - oversight

**Related Regulations (for comparison):**
- **216-RICR-40-05-7** - Clinical Social Workers and Independent Clinical Social Workers (licensed professionals)

**Key Distinction:** Recovery coaches certified through RICB are separate from statutory licensed professionals.

## Summary

**Regulatory Status:** STATE CERTIFICATION REQUIRED through RICB

**Independent Practice:** UNCLEAR - Information not available in search results; organizational employment model suggested

**Coaching vs. Therapy:** Certified through separate system from licensed professionals; based on lived experience providing support vs. clinical treatment

**Key Finding:** Rhode Island requires certification through Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB) and has issued specific Provider Certification Standards for Peer Based Recovery Support Services (PBRSS), but detailed requirements and independent practice permissions are not clear from available search results.
