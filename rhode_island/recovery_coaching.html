Legal Status Overview

Independent Practice Status Unclear

State-Defined Term: Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (credentialed by RICB)

Regulatory Status: State certification required through RICB

Independent Practice: Unclear - organizational employment model suggested

Legal Definition

"Peer Recovery Specialists (or Recovery Coaches) are current or former consumers of behavioral health services who are trained and certified to offer support and assistance to those in the recovery and community-integration process."

Official Term: "Certified Peer Recovery Specialist" credentialed by Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB).

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Certified Peer Recovery Specialist Scope

  • Provide support and assistance in recovery and community-integration process
  • Based on lived experience as consumers of behavioral health services
  • Work in wide range of settings
  • Certification administered by Rhode Island Certification Board (RICB)

Work Settings

  • Community mental health centers
  • Behavioral health programs
  • Substance use treatment facilities
  • Peer-run organizations
  • Community based organizations
  • Emergency rooms
  • Courts
  • Homeless shelters
  • Outreach programs

Licensed Clinical Professionals (Different Requirements)

  • Clinical Social Workers (LICSW) - can practice independently
  • Mental Health Counselors
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Regulated under Title 5 (health and safety) and Title 216 (Department of Health regulations)

Certification Requirements
