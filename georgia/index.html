Certification Overview

Georgia has celebrated 25 years of Peer Support leadership with approximately 4,600 CPS trained and certified specialists. Multiple certification pathways available for substance use disorder recovery support.

Certifying Body

Georgia Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Disabilities (DBHDD)
DBHDD is the sole certification body for Certified Peer Specialists in Georgia
Website: dbhdd.georgia.gov

Certification Options

1. CARES (Certified Addiction Recovery Empowerment Specialist)

Provider: Georgia Council for Recovery

Training: 40 hours including lectures and interactive workshops

Medicaid Billable: Yes

Certification: After passing final examination

Website: gc4recovery.org

2. CPS-Addictive Disease (CPS-AD)

Certified by DBHDD for individuals with lived experience with addiction practicing recovery

Requirement: 2 years of abstinence from alcohol and drugs

3. Peer Recovery Coach (CPRC)

Provider: Alcohol & Drug Abuse Certification Board of Georgia (ADACBGA)

Training: 100 hours education/training

For individuals with personal lived experience in recovery from addiction, mental illness, or co-occurring disorders

Website: adacbga.org

Application Process

  1. Choose certification pathway (CARES, CPS-AD, or CPRC)
  2. Complete required training through approved provider
  3. Meet recovery time requirements (2 years abstinence for CPS-AD)
  4. Pass certification examination
  5. Receive certification from DBHDD or ADACBGA
CARES Program ADACBGA Certifications

Employment & Reimbursement

CARES services are Medicaid billable. Employment opportunities in treatment facilities, recovery community organizations, community mental health centers, and hospital-based programs.
