STATUS: LIMITED - May Work as Contractor for Licensed Entities

Legal Status Overview

Status: LIMITED - May work as independent contractor for licensed entities

State-Defined Term: Registered Peer Recovery Specialist

Legal Citation: Virginia Code § 54.1-2400.1

Registration: Through Board of Counseling ($30 fee)

Unique Feature: Can work as employee OR independent contractor, but must be for/with licensed entities

Legal Definition

Virginia defines "registered peer recovery specialist" in statute, making it one of the few states with statutory recognition of peer recovery support.

Virginia Code § 54.1-2400.1

"Registered peer recovery specialist" means an individual registered by the Board of Counseling who:

  • Provides peer recovery support services
  • Is registered through the Virginia Board of Counseling
  • Meets credentialing requirements (NCPRSS + DBHDS training)
  • Works for or with qualifying licensed entities

Registration Requirements

Three Pathways to Registration

Pathway 1: NCPRSS + DBHDS Training

  • National Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (NCPRSS) from NAADAC
  • PLUS: Complete DBHDS peer recovery specialist training

Pathway 2: State Reciprocity + DBHDS Training

  • Valid peer recovery specialist certification from another state
  • State must have reciprocity agreement with Virginia
  • PLUS: Complete DBHDS peer recovery specialist training

Pathway 3: Virginia Certification + DBHDS Training

  • Virginia-specific peer recovery specialist certification
  • PLUS: Complete DBHDS peer recovery specialist training

Registration Process

  • Authority: Virginia Board of Counseling
  • Fee: $30 registration fee
  • Application: Submit proof of credentialing and training completion

Employment and Independent Contractor Model

Virginia's Unique Contractor Model

Important: Virginia allows registered peer recovery specialists to work as EITHER employees OR independent contractors, BUT with important limitations.

You May Work As:

  • Employee: Directly employed by qualifying entity
  • Independent Contractor: Contracted by qualifying entity

Critical Limitation:

Whether employee or contractor, you must work for or with one of the qualifying licensed entities listed below.

Qualifying Entities for Employment/Contracting

Registered peer recovery specialists may work for or with:

  1. Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Services (DBHDS)
  2. DBHDS-licensed provider - Organizations licensed by DBHDS to provide behavioral health services
  3. Health Professions licensed practitioner - Licensed counselors, psychologists, social workers, etc.
  4. Health licensed facility - Licensed healthcare facilities

What You CANNOT Do

  • Fully Autonomous Practice: Cannot operate completely independent recovery coaching practice without affiliation with qualifying entity
  • Direct-to-Consumer Without Affiliation: Cannot provide peer recovery services directly to public without working for/with licensed entity
  • Work Outside Qualifying Entities: Cannot contract with non-qualifying organizations or individuals

Independent Contractor Opportunities

How Independent Contracting Works in Virginia

Option 1: Contract with DBHDS-Licensed Providers

  • Treatment centers, outpatient programs, residential facilities
  • Provide peer recovery services under their licensure
  • Set contractor rates and terms

Option 2: Contract with Licensed Practitioners

  • Licensed counselors, psychologists, social workers
  • Provide peer recovery services as part of their practice
  • Work as independent contractor supporting their clients

Option 3: Contract with Licensed Facilities

  • Hospitals, medical centers, health clinics
  • Provide peer recovery services to their patients
  • Contractor arrangement under their licensure

Option 4: Work with DBHDS

  • State-level peer recovery initiatives
  • DBHDS programs and services

Scope of Practice

Permitted Activities

  • Providing peer recovery support services based on lived experience
  • Recovery coaching and mentoring
  • Advocacy for individuals in recovery
  • Connection to recovery resources and supports
  • Facilitation of peer support groups
  • Recovery education and skill-building
  • Support for recovery planning (non-clinical)
  • Assistance navigating systems and services

Prohibited Activities

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Cannot diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Treatment Planning: Cannot create clinical treatment plans
  • Therapy/Counseling: Cannot provide psychotherapy or clinical counseling
  • Medical Advice: Cannot provide medical or psychiatric advice
  • Presenting as Licensed Professional: Cannot represent as counselor, therapist, or clinician

Practical Guidance

Steps to Practice in Virginia

  1. Obtain Credential: Complete NCPRSS (or equivalent pathway)
  2. Complete DBHDS Training: Required peer recovery specialist training
  3. Register with Board of Counseling: Submit application and $30 fee
  4. Identify Qualifying Entity: Determine which licensed entity you'll work for/with
  5. Choose Employment Model: Decide between employee or independent contractor
  6. Formalize Relationship: Employment agreement or contractor agreement
  7. Obtain Insurance: Professional liability insurance (especially for contractors)
  8. Maintain Registration: Keep credentials and registration current

For Independent Contractors

Important Considerations:

  • Draft clear contractor agreements defining scope and terms
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Establish business entity (LLC, etc.) for liability protection
  • Maintain records of contractor relationships
  • Ensure all work is done "for or with" qualifying licensed entities
  • Don't market or provide services independently without entity affiliation
