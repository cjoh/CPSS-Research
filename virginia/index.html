Certification Overview

Virginia's Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) program provides training and certification for individuals with lived experience who want to support others in their recovery journey. The program is managed by DBHDS (Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Services).

Requirements

Eligibility Requirements

  • Minimum of high school diploma/GED from an accredited school
  • At least two years of recovery from substance use challenges or co-occurring substance use and mental health challenges

Training Requirements

  • 72-hour DBHDS CPRS Training Curriculum
  • Includes 60 hours of contact time and 12 hours of outside homework
  • Includes quizzes and a final test

Work Experience Requirements

  • 500 hours of volunteer or paid experience specific to peer recovery services
  • 200 hours of direct practice
  • Experience must include all aspects of peer recovery services

Certification Examination

  • Must pass a certification exam administered by Virginia Certification Board

Application Process

  1. Complete 72-Hour Training: Complete DBHDS CPRS Training through approved provider
  2. Gain Work Experience: Complete 500 hours of volunteer or paid experience (including 200 hours direct practice)
  3. Apply for Certification: Submit application to Virginia Certification Board
  4. Pass Certification Exam: Successfully complete CPRS certification examination
  5. Register with Board of Counseling: For Medicaid billing purposes, register with Virginia Department of Health Professions, Board of Counseling

Fees and Costs

  • Application Fee: $175
  • 2025 Scholarship: $150 scholarship available through September 30, 2025
  • Applicant Cost (with scholarship): $25

Renewal Requirements

  • Renewal Period: Every two years
  • Continuing Education: 20 hours every two years (6 hours must be in ethics)
  • Work History: Submit work history for the two years prior to renewal
