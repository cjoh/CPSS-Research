Recovery Coaching

Varies by State

Regulation Status: Most states have voluntary certification; a few require licensure or supervision.

  • 3 states require licensure
  • 26+ states offer certification
  • Peer-based, lived experience model
  • Distinct from therapy/counseling

Key Finding: Recovery coaching supplements clinical care; coaches must ensure clients have therapists and make appropriate referrals.

Life Coaching (Recovery Clients)

All States

Regulation Status: Life coaching is unregulated in all 51 jurisdictions.

  • Can work with recovery clients
  • Recovery as context, not target
  • Client must have clinical support
  • Cannot treat addiction itself

Key Principle: You coach someone WHO IS in recovery on life goals; you don't treat their recovery/addiction.

Case Management

Not Standalone Profession

Regulation Status: NOT regulated as standalone profession; regulated through underlying license.

  • No "case manager license" exists
  • Requires RN, LCSW, LPC, or related license
  • CCM certification industry standard
  • 22 states in Social Work Compact

Key Finding: Case management is a function performed by licensed professionals, not a separately licensed profession.

Professional Interventionist

All States

Regulation Status: Completely unregulated at state and federal level.

  • No license required in any state
  • Voluntary certifications: CIP, BRI, CAI
  • Professional liability insurance essential
  • Cannot diagnose or provide therapy

Key Finding: While unregulated, professional standards are high. Certification and proper training are industry expectations.

‚öñÔ∏è Universal Ethical Requirements Across All Practice Areas

1. Client Must Have Clinical Support: For recovery-related work, clients must have therapists, counselors, or other licensed clinical providers.

2. Stay in Your Lane: Do not diagnose, treat, or provide clinical services unless you have appropriate license.

3. Make Outbound Referrals: Maintain robust referral network and refer immediately for clinical needs, crises, or issues beyond your scope.

4. Professional Liability Insurance: Essential for all practice areas. Protects both you and your clients.

5. Clear Agreements: Written agreements clarifying what you do and don't provide, scope limitations, and client responsibilities.

6. Continuing Education: Stay current on laws, regulations, ethical standards, and best practices.

7. Documentation: Maintain appropriate records while respecting privacy and ethical boundaries.
