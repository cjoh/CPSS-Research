# Maryland Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)**

Also available: **Certified Peer Recovery Specialist - Family (CPRS-F)** for family members

## Certifying Body
**Maryland Addiction and Behavioral-health Professionals Certification Board (MABPCB)**

**Contact Information:**
- Organization: MABPCB
- Website: https://www.mabpcb.com
- Email: Contact through website
- CPRS Page: https://www.mabpcb.com/certified-peer-recovery-specialist-cprs

**State Coordination:**
- Maryland Behavioral Health Administration
- Peer Recovery ListServ: Administered by Adelaide Weber at adelaide.weber@maryland.gov

## Personal Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Lived Experience:** Must self-identify as a person in long-term recovery from a behavioral health disorder (mental health and/or substance use disorder)
- **Recovery Duration:** Minimum of two (2) years or more in recovery
- **Education:** Proof of High School Diploma or General Equivalency Degree (GED) required

## Training Requirements

### NEW Requirements Effective September 1, 2025

**IMPORTANT CHANGE:** New Harm Reduction domain added for 2025

**Total Training Hours:** 50 hours (increased from 46 hours)

**Domain Distribution:**
- **Advocacy:** 10 hours
- **Ethics:** 16 hours (includes 6 for recertification)
- **Mentoring and Education:** 10 hours
- **Recovery and Wellness Support:** 10 hours
- **Harm Reduction:** 4 hours (NEW - effective September 1, 2025)

### Supervision Requirements

**NEW Requirement Effective September 1, 2025:**
- **Total Supervision:** 30 hours (increased from 25 hours)
- **Harm Reduction Supervision:** Additional 5 hours required
- Supervision must cover all five domains including the new Harm Reduction domain

## Experience Requirements

### Supervised Service Hours
- **Minimum:** 500 service hours (paid or volunteer)
- **Setting:** Recovery coaching setting
- **Timeframe:** Within the past two years
- **Documentation:** Hours must be documented and supervised

## Examination Requirements

### IC&RC Certification
- The Maryland CPRS credential is an **International Certification and Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC)** approved certification
- Allows for potential reciprocity with other IC&RC states
- Nationally recognized standard

### Exam Information
- Contact MABPCB for current exam details
- Exam required for initial certification

## Application Process

1. Verify eligibility (2 years recovery, high school diploma/GED)
2. Complete 50 hours of MABPCB-approved domain training (effective Sept 1, 2025)
3. Complete 500 hours of supervised service in recovery coaching setting
4. Complete 30 hours of peer supervision covering all domains
5. Pass IC&RC examination
6. Submit application to MABPCB
7. Receive CPRS certification

## Fees

Specific fee information should be confirmed with MABPCB. Contact through website for current application and exam fees.

## Certification Period

### Initial Certification
- **Duration:** Two (2) years
- Certification is valid for two years from date of issuance

### Recertification
- Must apply for recertification **prior to expiration date**
- **No exam required** if recertify before expiration or within one year of expiration
- **After one year:** May need to retake exam

## Continuing Education Requirements

### Recertification Requirements (Every 2 Years)
- **Total:** 20 hours of MABPCB-approved peer recovery specialist specific education
- **Ethics:** 6 hours must be earned in Ethical Responsibility training
- **Remaining 14 hours:** Peer recovery specialist topics across the domains

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Maryland's Program
- **IC&RC Approved:** Nationally recognized certification with reciprocity potential
- **Two-Year Certification:** Longer validity period than some states
- **Comprehensive Training:** 50 hours across five domains
- **Harm Reduction Focus:** New 2025 requirement reflects current best practices
- **No Recert Exam:** Can maintain certification without retaking exam
- **Multiple Providers:** Various training options and schedules
- **Family Option:** CPRS-F available for family members of those in recovery
- **Professional Standards:** Strong ethics component (16 hours)

### Important 2025 Changes

**Effective September 1, 2025:**
1. **New Harm Reduction Domain**
   - 4 hours of training required
   - Reflects evolving best practices in peer support
   - Total training increases from 46 to 50 hours

2. **Increased Supervision**
   - Additional 5 hours for Harm Reduction domain
   - Total supervision increases from 25 to 30 hours
   - Must cover all five domains

3. **Planning Considerations**
   - Anyone certifying after September 1, 2025 must meet new requirements
   - Those certifying before September 1, 2025 follow previous requirements
   - Recertification will likely require Harm Reduction CE hours

### Best Practices for Applicants

**For New Applicants (Post-September 1, 2025):**
1. Ensure training includes all 50 hours with Harm Reduction domain
2. Plan for 30 hours of supervision across all five domains
3. Document 500 service hours carefully
4. Subscribe to Maryland BHA Peer Recovery ListServ for updates
5. Choose training provider with full domain coverage
6. Start supervision early in your 500-hour experience
7. Connect with certified peers for mentorship

**For Current Certificate Holders:**
1. Note certification expiration date
2. Complete recertification before expiration (no exam required)
3. Ensure 20 CE hours include 6 in ethics
4. Consider Harm Reduction training for professional development
5. Subscribe to ListServ for training opportunities
6. Maintain documentation of all CE hours

### Unique Aspects
- **Harm Reduction Integration:** Progressive approach to peer support
- **IC&RC Recognition:** Enhances professional mobility
- **Two-Year Cycle:** Allows for sustained professional development
- **Family Specialist Option:** Recognizes unique role of family peers
- **500-Hour Requirement:** Ensures substantial practical experience
- **Strong Ethics Foundation:** 16 hours training, 6 hours CE requirement
- **Multiple Training Pathways:** Flexibility in how to complete requirements
