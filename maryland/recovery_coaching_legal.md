# Maryland Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) - State-defined

**Definition:** IC&RC-approved certification for individuals in long-term recovery providing recovery support services.

**Administered by:** Maryland Addiction and Behavioral-health Professionals Certification Board (MABPCB)

## Coaching vs. Therapy
**Role:** Support based on lived experience. "Do not deal with diagnosis or trauma, but help create plans for future, set goals, achieve them."

**NOT therapists** - clear distinction from clinical practice.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** EXEMPT from licensing requirements

**Important:** Peer support services are **exempt from licensing requirements under COMAR 10.63** (community-based behavioral health programs).

**Certification Requirements:**
- Age 18+, reside in MD 51% of time
- Self-identify as person in recovery (2+ years)
- High school diploma/GED
- **50 domain hours training** (updated 2024 - includes new 4-hour Harm Reduction domain)
  - 10 hours Advocacy
  - 16 hours Ethical Responsibility
  - 10 hours Mentoring/Education
  - 10 hours Recovery/Wellness
  - 4 hours Harm Reduction (NEW 2024)
- 500 service hours documented (with 25 hours supervision by Registered Peer Supervisor)
- Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery Specialist exam

**Renewal:**
- Every 2 years
- 20 hours MABPCB-approved CE (minimum 6 hours ethics)

**State Employment:** State positions require certification within 12 months of hire.

**Citation:** COMAR 10.63 (exemption); MABPCB standards

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** YES - through state certification
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Clearly distinguished; not clinical
- **Independent practice?** EXEMPT from licensing requirements
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- Exempt from COMAR 10.63 licensing (significant advantage)
- New 2024 requirement: 50 hours training (added Harm Reduction domain)
- Can work in various settings: medical organizations, justice system, public health
