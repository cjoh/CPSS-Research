Certification Overview

A Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) in Maryland is a person in long-term recovery from a behavioral health disorder who uses their lived experience to support others in developing their personal recovery plans. The Maryland CPRS credential is an International Certification and Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC) approved certification, providing national recognition and potential reciprocity with other states.

Requirements

Personal Requirements

  • Recovery Status: Must self-identify as a person in long-term recovery from a behavioral health disorder (mental health and/or substance use disorder)
  • Recovery Duration: Minimum of two (2) years or more in recovery
  • Education: Proof of High School Diploma or General Equivalency Degree (GED) required

Training Requirements (Effective September 1, 2025)

Total Hours: 50 hours (increased from 46 hours)

Domain Distribution:

  • Advocacy: 10 hours
  • Ethics: 16 hours (includes 6 for recertification)
  • Mentoring and Education: 10 hours
  • Recovery and Wellness Support: 10 hours
  • Harm Reduction: 4 hours (NEW - effective September 1, 2025)

Experience Requirements

  • Service Hours: 500 service hours (paid or volunteer) in recovery coaching setting within the past two years
  • Supervision: 30 hours (increased from 25 hours) covering all five domains including Harm Reduction
  • Documentation: All hours must be properly documented and supervised

Examination

  • Exam Type: IC&RC examination required for initial certification
  • National Recognition: IC&RC approval allows for potential reciprocity with other IC&RC states

Application Process

1

Verify Eligibility

Ensure you have 2 years recovery and high school diploma/GED

2

Complete Training

Complete 50 hours of MABPCB-approved domain training including new Harm Reduction domain

3

Service & Supervision

Complete 500 service hours and 30 hours of supervision covering all domains

4

Pass Exam & Apply

Pass IC&RC examination and submit application to MABPCB

Visit MABPCB 2025 Requirements

Certification Period & Renewal

Initial Certification: Two (2) years from date of issuance

Recertification: Must apply prior to expiration date. No exam required if recertifying before expiration or within one year of expiration.

Continuing Education (Every 2 Years)

  • Total: 20 hours of MABPCB-approved peer recovery specialist specific education
  • Ethics: 6 hours must be earned in Ethical Responsibility training
  • Remaining 14 hours: Peer recovery specialist topics across the domains

IC&RC Reciprocity

Maryland CPRS is IC&RC approved, which may allow for reciprocity with other IC&RC member states. Contact MABPCB for reciprocity application process. Verify requirements with target state before assuming automatic transfer.
