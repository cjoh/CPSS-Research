# Connecticut Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Names
**Two Main Pathways:**
1. **Recovery Support Specialist (RSS)** - for mental health, addiction, and trauma recovery
2. **Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) / Peer Recovery Coach (PRC)** - focused on addiction recovery

## Certifying Bodies

### For Recovery Support Specialist (RSS):
**Advocacy Unlimited**
- Only state-authorized RSS training and certification program
- Website: https://advocacyunlimited.org/programs/rss-training/

**Connecticut Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services (DMHAS)**
- Recognizes RSS certification
- Website: https://portal.ct.gov/dmhas

### For Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS):
**Connecticut Certification Board, Inc. (CCB)**
- Administers CPRS certification
- Annual renewal required
- Website: https://ctcertboard.org/

**Connecticut Community for Addiction Recovery (CCAR)**
- Provides Recovery Coach Academy training
- Over 110,000 individuals trained worldwide
- Website: https://addictionrecoverytraining.org/

## Certification Overview

### Recovery Support Specialist (RSS)
An 80-hour training program that prepares individuals to provide mental health, addiction, and trauma recovery resources as peer support specialists. Can work in both mental health and addiction services systems.

### Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)
A 5-day intensive training focused on addiction recovery, providing core competencies to guide and support others pursuing long-term recovery from substance use.

## Detailed Requirements

### Recovery Support Specialist (RSS)

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Experience with mental health challenges, addiction, and/or trauma (personal or supporting others)
- Commitment to recovery-oriented principles
- Willingness to use lived experience to help others

**Training Requirements:**
- **Total Hours**: 80 hours
- **Components**:
  - Peer support principles
  - Stress management
  - Job readiness
- **Examination**: State certification exam required
- **Provider**: Advocacy Unlimited (only state-authorized program)

### Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

**Training Requirements:**
- **Duration**: 5-day intensive training
- **Provider**: CCAR Recovery Coach Academy
- **Focus**: Addiction recovery spectrum
- **Certification**: Through Connecticut Certification Board

## Application and Certification Process

### For Recovery Support Specialist (RSS)

**Step 1: Meet Eligibility Requirements**
- Lived experience with mental health, addiction, or trauma
- Personal experience or supporting others in recovery

**Step 2: Apply for RSS Training**
- Complete application at: https://advocacyunlimited.org/rsstraining/
- Submit during application period (November 1 - January 31 for April class)
- Pay $200 training fee

**Step 3: Complete 80-Hour Training**
- Attend all course days (10 days total)
- Participate in peer support, stress management, and job readiness training
- Complete all coursework requirements

**Step 4: Pass State Certification Exam**
- Take examination (April 27, 2026 for April class)
- Pass state certification exam
- Qualify for RSS certification

**Step 5: Graduate and Receive Certification**
- Attend graduation ceremony (May 1, 2026 for April class)
- Receive RSS certification
- Begin working as Recovery Support Specialist

### For Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

**Step 1: Complete CCAR Recovery Coach Training**
- Register for 5-day intensive training
- Complete CCAR Recovery Coach Academy program
- Receive training certificate

**Step 2: Apply for CPRS Certification**
- Submit application to Connecticut Certification Board
- Provide training documentation
- Pay certification fee

**Step 3: Maintain Annual Certification**
- Renew through Connecticut Certification Board annually
- Complete continuing education requirements

## Important Notes

### Key Considerations
- Connecticut has dual certification pathways
- RSS is more comprehensive (mental health + addiction + trauma)
- CPRS focuses specifically on addiction recovery
- Advocacy Unlimited is sole authorized RSS provider
- Both certifications recognized by Connecticut DMHAS
- Annual renewal required for CPRS
- Strong continuing education requirements (60 hours/3 years)

### Choosing Between RSS and CPRS
**Choose RSS if:**
- Want to work in mental health AND addiction services
- Prefer comprehensive trauma-informed approach
- Seeking state-authorized certification
- Available for 10-day training schedule

**Choose CPRS if:**
- Focusing specifically on addiction recovery
- Prefer intensive 5-day training format
- Want nationally recognized Recovery Coach Academy credential
- Seeking flexibility in training schedule

### Professional Development
- Participate in Recovery University programs
- Attend state peer support conferences
- Join professional peer networks
- Access DMHAS continuing education opportunities
- Network with other RSS/CPRS professionals
