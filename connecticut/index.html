Two Main Pathways: RSS & CPRS/Peer Recovery Coach

Certification Overview

Connecticut offers two main pathways for peer support certification. The RSS covers mental health, addiction, and trauma recovery while CPRS focuses on addiction recovery specifically.

For Recovery Support Specialist (RSS)

Advocacy Unlimited
Only state-authorized RSS training and certification program
Phone: (860) 505-7581 or 1-800-573-6929
Website: advocacyunlimited.org

For Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

CCAR (Connecticut Community for Addiction Recovery)
Recovery Coach Academy - 5-day intensive training
Website: addictionrecoverytraining.org

Connecticut Certification Board, Inc. (CCB)
Administers CPRS certification (annual renewal)
Website: ctcertboard.org

Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

5-Day Intensive Training

Provider: CCAR Recovery Coach Academy

Over 110,000 individuals trained worldwide

Focus: Addiction recovery spectrum

Certification: Through Connecticut Certification Board

Annual Renewal Required

RSS Requirements

  • Experience with mental health challenges, addiction, and/or trauma
  • Commitment to recovery-oriented principles
  • 80 hours training through Advocacy Unlimited
  • Pass state certification exam

RSS Application Process

  1. Meet eligibility requirements (lived experience)
  2. Apply during application period (November 1 - January 31 for April class)
  3. Pay $200 training fee
  4. Complete 80-hour training (10 days)
  5. Pass state certification exam (April 27, 2026)
  6. Attend graduation (May 1, 2026) and receive RSS certification
Apply for RSS Training CCAR Training

Renewal Requirements

RSS: 60 hours continuing education every three years

CPRS: Annual renewal through CCB | 60 hours CE every three years

Employment Opportunities

RSS can work in both mental health AND addiction services systems. CPRS focuses on addiction recovery services. Settings include mental health services, addiction services, integrated behavioral health programs, community mental health centers, substance use treatment facilities, hospital-based programs, and recovery community centers.

Which Certification?

Choose RSS if:

  • Want to work in mental health AND addiction services
  • Prefer comprehensive trauma-informed approach
  • Seeking state-authorized certification
  • Available for 10-day training schedule

Choose CPRS if:

  • Focusing specifically on addiction recovery
  • Prefer intensive 5-day training format
  • Want nationally recognized Recovery Coach Academy credential
  • Seeking flexibility in training schedule
