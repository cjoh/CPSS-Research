Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS)

Certification Overview

Certified Peer Support Specialists in Utah use their lived experience in recovery from mental health and/or substance use disorder to support others on their recovery journey. The certification is managed by Utah's Office of Substance Use and Mental Health.

Utah Office of Substance Use and Mental Health

Website: sumh.utah.gov - Peer Support Specialists

Utah Peer Network: utahpeernetwork.org/certification

Requirements

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be 18 years of age or older
  • Individual who uses their lived experience in recovery from mental health and/or substance use disorder

Application Requirements

Must submit typed "essays" covering:

  1. A history of your recovery journey
  2. What you are doing to remain in recovery
  3. In what ways have you assisted others using your own experience as an example

Training Requirements

  • 40 hours over five days of CPSS training
  • Offered in various locations with new hybrid options available
  • Topics include: Recovery stories, Utah Peer Support Code of Ethics, motivational interviewing, navigating peer work's unique challenges, and many other useful tools
  • Must pass exam at end of training week to receive certification

Application Process

  1. Complete 40-Hour Training: Attend and complete five-day CPSS training program
  2. Pass Examination: Successfully pass certification exam administered during training
  3. Submit Application: Apply to the State's Office of Substance Use and Mental Health with required essays
  4. Receive Certification: Obtain CPSS certification valid throughout Utah

Renewal Requirements

  • Renewal Period: Every two years
  • Total Continuing Education: 20 hours every two years

Continuing Education Breakdown

  • Ethics Training: 2 hours
  • Peer Support Services: 6 hours pertaining specifically to peer support services
  • Suicide Prevention: 1 hour
  • General MH/SUD Training: 11 hours of general mental health and/or substance use disorder training
