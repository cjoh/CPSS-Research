Certification Overview

Peer specialists help instill hope of recovery from behavioral health challenges and use their experience to help others engage in recovery. Texas offers Mental Health Peer Specialist (MHPS) and Recovery Support Peer Specialist (RSPS) certifications through the Texas Certification Board.

Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC)

Website: Texas HHSC Peer Support Services

PeerForce (Texas Peer Development Hub): peerforce.org

Requirements

Eligibility Requirements

  • High school graduation or GED
  • Must be over 18 years of age
  • Personally experiencing mental health or substance use challenges
  • Willingness to appropriately share recovery story

Training Requirements

Step 1: Core Training

  • 24-hour CORE training via facilitated interactive webinar (Zoom)
  • Prerequisite to both MHPS and RSPS training

Step 2: Supplemental Training

  • 40 to 46-hour in-person or online workshop
  • Must pass knowledge assessment at the end of the course

Supervised Work Experience

  • Complete 250 internship hours under supervision of a Peer Specialist Supervisor (PSS)
  • Initial certification valid for six months, during which you must complete the 250 hours

Application Process

  1. Complete Online Orientation: Download and sign attestation of completion from HHSC webpage
  2. Complete Training: Finish both Core (24 hours) and Supplemental (40-46 hours) training from Certified Training Entity
  3. Apply for Certification: Submit application through Texas Certification Board using Certemy digital application
  4. Complete Supervised Work Experience: Finish 250 hours under supervision of Peer Specialist Supervisor
  5. Receive Full Certification: Obtain MHPS or RSPS certification from Texas Certification Board
