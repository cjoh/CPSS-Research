STATUS: ALLOWED - Independent Practice Permitted

Legal Status Overview

Status: ALLOWED - Independent practice permitted

State-Defined Term: Peer Recovery Specialist (PRS) and Recovery Support Peer Specialist (RSPS)

Independent Practice: PERMITTED - Can open private practice and work independently

Legal Definition

Texas defines recovery coaching through the Texas Certification Board of Addiction Professionals (TCBAP), which is an IC&RC member board. Two primary certifications exist:

Peer Recovery Specialist (PRS)

A person in recovery from substance use disorder who uses their lived experience to provide peer support, advocacy, and recovery-oriented services.

Recovery Support Peer Specialist (RSPS)

A person with lived experience in recovery who provides mentoring, advocacy, and recovery support services to individuals in or seeking recovery.

Certification Requirements

Total Training: 46 Hours

  • Advocacy: 10 hours
  • Mentoring: 10 hours
  • Recovery Support: 10 hours
  • Ethical Responsibility: 16 hours

Additional Requirements

  • Personal recovery experience
  • Pass TCBAP certification examination
  • Maintain continuing education requirements

Independent Practice

Independent Practice IS PERMITTED

Key Distinction: Recovery coaches in Texas can operate independently, unlike LPC Associates who must work under supervision.

Permitted Activities for Independent Practice:

  • Open and operate private recovery coaching practice
  • Set fees for services (typical range: $50-150 per hour)
  • Work independently without clinical supervision
  • Provide peer support and recovery coaching services
  • Offer advocacy and mentoring services
  • Facilitate peer support groups
  • Provide recovery support services

Comparison: Recovery Coaches vs. LPC Associates

Recovery Coaches (PRS/RSPS):

  • Can work independently
  • Can open private practice
  • Set their own fees
  • No supervision requirement

LPC Associates (Licensed Professional Counselor Associates):

  • MUST work under supervision of fully licensed LPC
  • CANNOT practice independently
  • CANNOT open private practice
  • Required to have clinical supervision

Scope of Practice Boundaries

What Recovery Coaches MUST AVOID

To maintain legal practice and avoid unauthorized practice of counseling or therapy:

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Cannot diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Treatment Planning: Cannot create clinical treatment plans requiring professional judgment
  • Psychotherapy: Cannot provide therapy or clinical counseling services
  • Presenting as Licensed Professional: Cannot represent themselves as licensed counselors, therapists, or clinicians
  • Clinical Assessment: Cannot conduct psychological or clinical assessments
  • Medication Management: Cannot provide advice about psychiatric medications

What Recovery Coaches CAN DO

  • Share lived experience of recovery
  • Provide peer support and encouragement
  • Offer advocacy services
  • Provide mentoring and guidance
  • Assist with recovery goal-setting (non-clinical)
  • Help connect clients to resources
  • Facilitate peer support groups
  • Provide recovery education
  • Support development of recovery skills
  • Offer accountability and check-ins

Fee Structure

Typical Fee Range

Independent Practice Fees: $50-150 per hour

Recovery coaches can set their own fees based on:

  • Level of experience and training
  • Geographic location and market rates
  • Service complexity and duration
  • Specialized expertise or certifications

Legal Citations

Primary Authority

Texas Certification Board of Addiction Professionals (TCBAP)

  • IC&RC member board
  • Authority over peer recovery specialist certifications
  • Website: www.tcbap.org

Related Statutes

  • Texas Occupations Code, Chapter 503 - Professional Counseling (does not regulate recovery coaches)
  • No specific recovery coach licensing statute - Certification through TCBAP is voluntary but recommended

Practical Guidance

Starting an Independent Practice

  1. Obtain Certification: Complete 46-hour training and pass TCBAP exam
  2. Business Structure: Register business entity (LLC, sole proprietorship, etc.)
  3. Liability Insurance: Obtain professional liability insurance
  4. Clear Service Agreements: Use written agreements defining scope of services
  5. Marketing Compliance: Clearly identify as recovery coach, not therapist/counselor
  6. Maintain Boundaries: Stay within peer support scope, refer for clinical needs
  7. Continuing Education: Maintain TCBAP certification requirements

Risk Management Recommendations

  • Maintain clear documentation of services provided
  • Use informed consent forms explaining non-clinical nature of services
  • Keep referral list for clinical services
  • Stay current on TCBAP ethical standards
  • Join professional associations for support and resources
