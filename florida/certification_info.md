# Florida Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Names
- **Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS)** - Standard certification
- **Certified Recovery Peer Specialist Provisional (CRPS-P)** - Provisional certification
- **Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS)** - Alternative credential

## Certifying Body
**Florida Certification Board (FCB)**
- Website: https://flcertificationboard.org/
- CRPS Page: https://flcertificationboard.org/certifications/certified-recovery-peers-specialist/

**Florida Department of Children & Families (DCF)**
- Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health
- Oversees peer support services statewide

## Certification Overview
The Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS) is awarded to individuals who demonstrate competency in peer-to-peer recovery support through the Florida Certification Board. CRPS professionals have demonstrated competency in Advocacy, Recovery Support, Mentoring, and Professional Responsibility.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
- Personal lived experience with mental health or substance use challenges
- Specialized training to provide support to others facing similar struggles
- Willing to self-identify as person in recovery
- Willing to use lived experience to help others

### Educational Requirements
- No specific degree requirement
- Must complete required training hours
- Pass competency examination

### Background Check
- Background screening may be required by employer
- Contact FCB for specific requirements

## Training Requirements

### Core Training: 40 Hours

**Required Content Distribution:**
- **Core Content**: 28 hours
- **Peer Whole Health and Resilience**: 8 hours
- **Electives**: 4 hours
- **Total**: 40 hours minimum

### Core Competency Areas
All CRPS must demonstrate competency in:
- Advocacy
- Recovery Support
- Mentoring
- Professional Responsibility

### CRPS Endorsements
CRPS endorsements reflect the peer's specific lived experiences:
- Adult
- Family
- Veteran
- Youth
- Criminal Justice

## Application and Certification Process

### Two Pathways Available

#### Standard Application Pathway (CRPS)
**Step 1: Complete 40-Hour Training**
- Select FCB-approved training provider
- Complete required 40 hours:
  - 28 hours core content
  - 8 hours whole health/resilience
  - 4 hours electives

**Step 2: Submit Application**
- Apply through Florida Certification Board
- Provide training documentation
- Pay application fee

**Step 3: Pass Examination**
- Schedule competency examination
- Pass FCB CRPS exam
- Demonstrate competency in all areas

**Step 4: Receive Certification**
- FCB issues CRPS credential
- Select appropriate endorsement
- Begin working as CRPS

#### Provisional Application Pathway (CRPS-P)
For those working toward full certification:
- May provide services for up to 1 year
- Must be working toward certification
- Must be supervised by qualified professional or certified peer specialist with 2+ years experience
- **Legal Requirement** per Florida Statutes Chapter 397 Section 417

## Renewal Requirements

### Recertification Period
**Every 2 years** (typical certification board cycle)

### Continuing Education
- Complete required continuing education hours
- Maintain competency in all domain areas
- Professional development activities
- Contact FCB for specific CE hour requirements

### Renewal Process
1. Complete required continuing education
2. Submit renewal application to FCB
3. Pay renewal fee
4. Maintain good standing
5. Update endorsements if applicable

## Employment and Reimbursement

### Medicaid and DCF Billing
- **Medicaid**: Services provided by CRPS are billable through Medicaid
- **DCF**: Services billable through Department of Children & Families
- **Important**: ONLY Certified Recovery Peer Specialists (CRPS) can bill for covered services

### Employment Settings
- Substance use disorder treatment facilities
- Community mental health centers
- Integrated behavioral health clinics
- Hospital-based programs
- Community-based organizations
- Correctional facilities and reentry programs
- Recovery community centers

### Legal Requirements
Per Florida Statutes Chapter 397.417:
- Peer specialists providing recovery support services must be certified
- Exception: May work up to 1 year uncertified if:
  - Working toward certification
  - Supervised by qualified professional OR
  - Supervised by certified peer specialist with 2+ years full-time experience

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Step 1: Review FCB Requirements
- Visit: https://flcertificationboard.org/education-training/providers/
- Review eligible training guidelines
- Understand FCB approval standards

### Step 2: Develop Curriculum
Must include:
- 28 hours core content
- 8 hours peer whole health and resilience
- 4 hours electives
- Total 40 hours minimum
- All four competency areas (Advocacy, Recovery Support, Mentoring, Professional Responsibility)

### Step 3: Submit Provider Application
- Complete training provider application
- Provide curriculum for review
- Include instructor qualifications
- Describe quality assurance measures
- Submit to FCB for approval

### Step 4: Meet Quality Standards
- Qualified instructors with lived experience
- Evidence-based curriculum
- Appropriate training facilities or virtual platform
- Valid assessment tools
- Certificate issuance process
- Evaluation mechanisms

### Step 5: Receive FCB Approval
- FCB reviews and approves curriculum
- Organization listed on FCB website as approved provider
- Assigned FCB provider number
- Begin offering certified training
- Issue certificates counting toward CRPS certification

## Notable Features of Florida Program

### Key Advantages
- Medicaid AND DCF billing available
- 2025 scholarship program covering fees
- Multiple endorsement options
- Provisional pathway for new peers
- Legal recognition in state statutes
- Statewide network of training providers

### Legal Recognition
Florida Statutes specifically recognize peer specialists and require certification, demonstrating strong state support for the peer workforce.

### Scholarship Opportunity
The 2024-2025 scholarship program funded by DCF makes certification accessible by covering application, exam, renewal, upgrade, and reinstatement fees.

### Endorsement System
Florida's endorsement system (Adult, Family, Veteran, Youth, Criminal Justice) allows specialization and recognition of specific lived experience.

## Career Pathways

### Entry Level: CRPS-P (Provisional)
- Complete 40-hour training
- Work under supervision for up to 1 year
- Gain experience while working toward full certification
- Build skills and confidence

### Standard: CRPS
- Achieve full certification
- Work independently in peer support role
- Select appropriate endorsement(s)
- Serve diverse populations

### Advanced: CRSS or Specialized Roles
- Pursue CRSS credential
- Advance to peer supervisor (with 2+ years experience)
- Train new peer specialists
- Program coordinator
- Policy advocate

## Important Notes

### Key Considerations
- Florida requires certification to bill Medicaid and DCF
- 40-hour training requirement is relatively brief compared to some states
- Endorsement system recognizes specialized lived experience
- Provisional certification allows work while completing full certification
- 2025 scholarship makes certification financially accessible
- Multiple training providers throughout state

### Application Strategy
- Apply for 2025 scholarship program early (July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025)
- Select training provider based on location and schedule
- Choose endorsement(s) that match your lived experience
- Consider provisional pathway if employment opportunity available
- Plan for 2-year renewal cycle

### Professional Development
- Join Peer Support Coalition of Florida
- Attend state peer support conferences
- Network with other CRPS professionals
- Pursue additional endorsements
- Access continuing education through FCB approved providers
- Stay informed about scholarship opportunities
