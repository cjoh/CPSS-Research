Certification Overview

The Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS) is awarded by the Florida Certification Board. CRPS professionals demonstrate competency in Advocacy, Recovery Support, Mentoring, and Professional Responsibility.

Certifying Body

Florida Certification Board (FCB)
Website: flcertificationboard.org

Application Fee: Contact FCB
Examination Fee: Contact FCB
Training Costs: Vary by provider

Training Requirements: 40 Hours

Required Content Distribution:

  • Core Content: 28 hours
  • Peer Whole Health and Resilience: 8 hours
  • Electives: 4 hours
  • Total: 40 hours minimum

CRPS Endorsements

Endorsements reflect the peer's specific lived experiences:

  • Adult
  • Family
  • Veteran
  • Youth
  • Criminal Justice

Application Process

  1. Complete 40-hour training through FCB-approved provider
  2. Apply to Florida Certification Board
  3. Pass FCB CRPS exam
  4. Select appropriate endorsement(s)
  5. Receive CRPS certification
CRPS Certification Info Find Training

Renewal: Every 2 Years

Complete required continuing education hours and submit renewal application

Employment & Reimbursement

Important: Florida requires certification to bill Medicaid and DCF. Services provided by CRPS are billable through Medicaid.

Legal Recognition

Florida Statutes Chapter 397.417 specifically recognizes peer specialists and requires certification, demonstrating strong state support for the peer workforce.
