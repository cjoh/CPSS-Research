# Florida Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition of Coaching

**Status:** LEGALLY DEFINED IN STATUTE

**Definition:** Florida Statutes § 397.417 defines "Peer specialist" as:

> "A person who has been in recovery from a substance use disorder or mental illness for at least 2 years who uses his or her personal experience to provide services in behavioral health settings to support others in their recovery, or a person who has at least 2 years of experience as a family member or caregiver of an individual who has a substance use disorder or mental illness."

**Recovery support** is defined as:

> "Services designed to strengthen or assist individuals to regain skills, develop the environmental supports necessary to help the individual thrive in the community, and meet life goals that promote recovery from alcohol and drug use."

**Citation:**
- Florida Statutes § 397.417 (2019, 2021, 2025)
- Florida Statutes § 397.311 (definitions)

## Coaching vs. Therapy Boundary

**What does state law say?**
Florida law provides clear boundaries:

### Scope of Practice for Peer Specialists:

**Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS):**
- Recovery Management
- Practical Living Skills and Social Development
- Resource Linkage and Followup
- Re-engagement, Crisis Support and Safety
- Professional Responsibilities

**Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS):**
- Advocacy
- Recovery Support
- Mentoring
- Professional Responsibility

### Prohibited Activities:
Recovery coaches/peer specialists:
- Do NOT deal with diagnosis or trauma
- Do NOT provide clinical treatment
- Do NOT provide mental health therapy
- Do NOT prescribe treatment

### Permitted Activities:
- Help create plans for the future
- Set goals and support achieving them
- Share lived experience
- Navigate treatment and service systems
- Support sobriety maintenance

**Citation:** Florida Certification Board competency domains; Florida Statutes § 397.417

## Independent Practice Requirements

**Can recovery coaches operate independently?**
**STATUS: MUST WORK IN LICENSED SETTINGS WITH SUPERVISION**

### Certification Requirement:
Florida Statutes § 397.417 states:

> "The department shall require that a peer specialist providing recovery support services be certified; however, an individual who is not certified may provide recovery support services as a peer specialist for up to 1 year if he or she is working toward certification and is supervised by a qualified professional or by a certified peer specialist who has at least 2 years of full-time experience as a peer specialist at a licensed behavioral health organization."

**Key Requirements:**
1. Must be certified (or working toward certification within 1 year)
2. If uncertified, must be supervised
3. Must work at licensed behavioral health organization

### Certification Options:

**1. Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS):**
- High school diploma, GED or equivalent
- 1,000 hours work/volunteer experience in addiction-related field
- 75 hours training (five classes)
- Pass certification exam

**2. Certified Recovery Peer Specialist (CRPS):**
- Personal experience with mental health or substance use challenges
- Specialized training
- Demonstrated competency in four domains
- High school diploma or GED

### Regulatory Authority:
- **Florida Department of Children and Families, Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health** - Administers scholarship program and oversight
- **Florida Certification Board (FCB)** - Third-party, non-profit certification provider that develops and administers standards

### Scholarship Program:
Open July 1, 2024 through June 30, 2025 for applications, exams, renewals, upgrades, and reinstatements.

**Interventionists:** No separate regulatory framework found. Would fall under same requirements.

## Summary

**Is "recovery coaching" legally defined?**
- **YES** - "Peer specialist" is defined in Florida Statutes § 397.417
- Recovery support services are statutorily defined
- Different from generic "recovery coach" - Florida uses peer specialist terminology

**Coaching vs. Therapy boundary?**
- **CLEARLY DEFINED** - Peer specialists:
  - Do NOT diagnose or provide clinical treatment
  - Focus on lived experience and peer support
  - Work within defined competency domains
  - Support recovery, not provide therapy

**Independent practice allowed?**
- **NO** - Must work within:
  - Licensed behavioral health organization
  - Under supervision (if not certified or within first year)
  - Cannot practice independently as sole proprietor

**Interventionists regulated differently?**
- No separate regulatory framework found

## Practical Implications

### To Work as Peer Specialist in Florida:

**Requirements:**
1. Meet 2-year recovery requirement
2. Complete CRSS (75 hours) or CRPS training
3. Pass certification exam
4. Obtain employment at licensed behavioral health organization
5. Work under supervision initially

**Cannot Do:**
- Open independent practice/LLC
- Work outside licensed behavioral health settings
- Provide services without certification (beyond 1-year grace period)
- Diagnose or provide clinical treatment

**Can Do (Within Licensed Setting):**
- Provide peer recovery support services
- Use lived experience to support others
- Facilitate recovery groups
- Provide resource linkage
- Crisis support
- Recovery management assistance

### Continuing Education:
Not explicitly stated in search results but typically required for certification renewal.

### Employment Settings:
- Licensed substance abuse treatment facilities
- Behavioral health organizations
- Certified community behavioral health clinics
- Programs licensed under Chapter 397

## Florida-Specific Considerations

1. **Grace Period:** 1-year grace period to obtain certification while working (must be supervised)

2. **Two Certification Pathways:** CRSS (substance use focus) and CRPS (mental health/co-occurring focus)

3. **Scholarship Funding:** State funding available for certification through DCF

4. **Licensed Setting Requirement:** Unlike some states, Florida requires work in licensed organizations

5. **Clear Statutory Framework:** Florida has more developed statutory framework than many states
