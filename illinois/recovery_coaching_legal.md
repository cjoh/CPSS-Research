# Illinois Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Two certifications available:
1. Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS)
2. Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)

**Distinction from therapy:** "Unlike therapists or counselors, recovery coaches don't provide clinical treatment or diagnose conditions."

**Administered by:** Illinois Department of Human Services, Division of Behavioral Health

## Coaching vs. Therapy
**Clear Distinction:**
> "A recovery coach isn't a clinical professional; they don't provide mental health therapy, diagnose conditions, or prescribe treatment."

**What coaches do:** Act as guides sharing personal experiences, support building new habits, provide accountability.

**Regulatory Warning:** Illinois authorities have sanctioned unlicensed persons who step into realm of licensed mental health care.

**Citation:** Illinois DHS regulations

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Certification available; must avoid licensed professional activities

**Employment Settings:** Mental health centers, hospitals, prisons/detention centers, community health centers, halfway houses, veterans centers, rehab centers.

**Key Principle:** Cannot engage in activities requiring licensure (therapy, psychotherapy services).

**Citation:** Illinois Licensed Professional Practice Act

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** Through certification programs (CRSS, CPRS)
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Clearly distinguished; peer support not clinical treatment
- **Independent practice?** Must avoid regulated professional activities
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- Two certification pathways available
- Clear regulatory distinction from therapy
- State has sanctioned unlicensed practice
- Work in various institutional settings

**Contact:** Illinois DHS: dhs.state.il.us
