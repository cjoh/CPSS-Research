Certification Overview

The Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS) fulfills a unique role in the support of and recovery from mental health issues and substance use disorders. A PRSS is a person in recovery from a mental health diagnosis and/or substance use disorder who has been trained to work with others on their individual road to recovery. Oklahoma also offers Family PRSS (F-PRSS) certification for family members supporting loved ones in recovery.

Critical Employment Requirement

You cannot become a certified PRSS unless you are employed by or volunteering with a state-contracted agency. You must be employed or intern within 6 months from application date with the state or a behavioral service provider contracting with the state.

Requirements

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • Demonstrated recovery from a mental health diagnosis, substance use disorder, or both
  • Willingness to publicly identify as a person in recovery

Education Requirements

  • High school diploma or GED equivalent
  • Must obtain official transcript showing graduation date

Training Requirements

  • 40-hour PRSS Core Training (5 days)
  • Must attend all days and modules
  • Absence lasting over one day requires retaking entire 40-hour training
  • Training is FREE (meals and lodging not included)

Application Timeline

Once you complete your online application, you have only 6 months to complete the entire application process before your application expires.

Application Process

  1. Secure Employment: Obtain employment or internship with state-contracted behavioral health provider
  2. Complete Online Application: Submit application through ODMHSAS system and pay $25 application fee
  3. Sign Code of Ethics: Sign and submit PRSS Code of Ethics with Application Inventory Form
  4. Register for Training: Contact PRSSteam@odmhsas.org to enroll and create account in ODMHSAS Training Institute
  5. Complete 40-Hour Training: Attend all 5 days of PRSS Core training
  6. Pass Examination: Successfully complete exam and share recovery story
  7. Receive Certification: Obtain PRSS certification from ODMHSAS

  • Renewal Period: Annual
  • Continuing Education: 12 hours per year (3 hours must be in Ethics)

ODMHSAS provides PRSS training directly through the ODMHSAS Training Institute. Organizations interested in becoming training partners should contact:

Important Distinction

ODMHSAS certifies organizations as program types, not individuals. Organizations must become certified providers to employ PRSS professionals.
