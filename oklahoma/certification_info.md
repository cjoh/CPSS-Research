**Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS)** and **Family Peer Recovery Support Specialist (F-PRSS)**

## Certification Overview
The Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS) fulfills a unique role in the support of and recovery from mental health issues and substance use disorders. A PRSS is a person in recovery from a mental health diagnosis and/or substance use disorder who has been trained to work with others on their individual road to recovery. Oklahoma also offers Family PRSS (F-PRSS) certification for family members supporting loved ones in recovery.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements
- Have **demonstrated recovery** from a mental health diagnosis, substance use disorder, or both
- Be willing to publicly identify as a person in recovery

### Education Requirements
- **High school diploma or GED equivalent**
- Must be able to obtain official transcript showing graduation date

### Character Requirements
- Possess **good moral character**

### Employment Requirements
**CRITICAL REQUIREMENT:** Must be employed by or intern with (within 6 months from application date):
- The state, OR
- A behavioral service provider contracting with the state to provide behavioral health services, OR
- An advocacy agency contracting with the state to provide behavioral health services, OR
- A behavioral health services provider certified by ODMHSAS

**Important:** You **cannot** become a certified PRSS unless you are employed by or volunteering with a state-contracted agency.

### Training Requirements

#### PRSS Core Training (40 hours)
- **40-hour training block** covering:
  - Recovery principles and practices
  - Ethics and boundaries
  - Mental health topics
  - Substance use disorder topics
  - Peer support competencies

#### New Training Structure (2024+)
- First **2.5 days** include training for both general Peers and Family Peers
- After 2.5 days, Family Peers separate to focus on specific F-PRSS content
- Family Peer content now integrated into PRSS Core training

#### Attendance Requirements
- **Must attend all days and modules** of the 5-day training
- Absence lasting **over one day** requires retaking the entire 40-hour training block
- Full attendance is mandatory

#### Training is Free
- No cost for training itself
- Must provide own meals and lodging if necessary

### Code of Ethics
- Must agree to respect and observe the **PRSS Code of Ethics**
- Code of Ethics must be signed and emailed with Application Inventory Form **before** being assigned to training

### Examination Requirements
- Pass an examination after completing training
- Successfully share your recovery story based on ODMHSAS standards

### Application Timeline
Once you complete your online application, you have **only 6 months** to complete the entire application process before your application expires.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Secure Employment
Obtain employment or internship with state-contracted behavioral health provider

### Step 2: Complete Online Application
Submit application through ODMHSAS system
- Pay $25 application fee
- Sign and submit PRSS Code of Ethics with Application Inventory Form

### Step 3: Register for Training
- Contact PRSSteam@odmhsas.org to enroll
- Create account in ODMHSAS Training Institute
- Register through training department

### Step 4: Complete 40-Hour Training
Attend all 5 days of PRSS Core training

### Step 5: Pass Examination
Successfully complete exam and share recovery story

### Step 6: Receive Certification
Obtain PRSS certification from ODMHSAS

## Application Fees
- **Initial Application Fee:** $25
- **No training fee** (training is free)

## Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
Certifications must be renewed **annually**

### Continuing Education Requirements
- **12 hours** of continuing education units per year
- **3 hours** must be in Ethics

### Renewal Fee
- **Standard Renewal Fee:** $15
- **Late Fee:** $25 (added to $15 renewal fee if submitting renewal paperwork after December 31st)
- **Total if Late:** $40

### Renewal Process
Submit renewal paperwork and continuing education documentation before December 31st each year

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
ODMHSAS provides PRSS training directly through the ODMHSAS Training Institute. Organizations interested in becoming training partners should contact ODMHSAS.

### Supervisor Training
**PRSS Supervisors** are required to complete PRSS Supervisor Training (no certification required for supervisors).

### Provider Certification
**Website:** https://oklahoma.gov/odmhsas/policy/provider-certification.html
**New Programs Overview:** https://oklahoma.gov/odmhsas/policy/provider-certification/new-programs-overview-of-odmhsas-certification-process.html.html

#### Important Distinction:
- ODMHSAS certifies **organizations** as program types, not individuals
- Organizations must become certified providers to employ PRSS professionals
- Individual behavioral health providers require separate licensure
