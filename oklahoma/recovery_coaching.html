Legal Status Overview

Independent Practice Status Unclear

State-Defined Term: Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS)

Regulatory Status: State certification required under OAC § 450:53

Independent Practice: Unclear - insufficient information available

Legal Definition

Terminology: Oklahoma uses "Peer Recovery Support Specialist" (PRSS) rather than "recovery coach."

Regulatory Reference: PRSS certification is governed by Oklahoma Administrative Code (OAC) 450:53, though the full text was not available in search results.

Limited Information Available

The full text of OAC 450:53 and complete certification requirements were not available in research. This page reflects only the information that could be confirmed.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Peer Recovery Specialists

  • Utilized in healthcare settings like Certified Community Behavioral Health Clinics (CCBHCs)
  • Help individuals transition from emergency departments or hospitals to community care
  • Operate as part of care transition and support services
  • Certified through ODMHSAS (separate from state licensing boards)

Licensed Behavioral Health Professionals

  • Governed by Oklahoma State Board of Behavioral Health Licensure under Title 59
  • Licensed Professional Counselors
  • Licensed Clinical Social Workers
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists

Key Distinction

PRSS certification administered separately through ODMHSAS. Specific scope of practice limitations not detailed in available search results.

Independent Practice Requirements

Certification Authority

  • Oklahoma Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services (ODMHSAS)
  • Certification governed by OAC Title 450, Chapter 53

Organizational Certification Focus

Important Distinction: ODMHSAS certifies ORGANIZATIONS as specific program types, not individuals as standalone practitioners.

  • Some individual certifications offered through separate processes
  • Facilities must retain records to verify compliance with PRSS training and certification requirements
  • Organizational certification focus suggests employment-based model

Independent Practice Status

Status: UNCLEAR

Search results do not provide explicit information about independent practice permissions.

  • Organizational certification focus suggests employment-based model

Certification Requirements

Specific training hours, examination, and eligibility requirements not detailed in search results

Important Considerations

Important Note

The absence of detailed information in search results does NOT mean unregulated practice. OAC 450:53 exists and governs PRSS certification - practitioners must obtain and review the full regulations before practicing.

Comparison Context

  • Licensed behavioral health professionals in Oklahoma are governed by Title 59 with clear independent practice authority
  • PRSS certification appears to operate under different framework through ODMHSAS
  • Organizational certification focus suggests different practice model than licensed independent practitioners
