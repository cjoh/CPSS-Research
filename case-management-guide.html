Executive Summary

Key Finding: Case Management is NOT Uniformly Regulated

Critical Insight: Unlike professions such as nursing or social work, "case management" itself is not regulated as a standalone profession in most states.

Instead, regulation comes through:

  • Underlying Professional Licensure (RN, LCSW, MSW, LPC, etc.)
  • Voluntary National Certifications (CCM, ACM, ANCC)
  • Setting-Specific Requirements (Medicaid, hospitals, insurance)
  • State-Specific Certifications (limited to a few states)

What This Means for Case Managers:

You likely DON'T need a specific "case manager license"

You likely DO need:

  • Underlying professional license (RN, LCSW, LPC, etc.) for clinical case management
  • CCM or equivalent certification (employer preference or state requirement)
  • Bachelor's or Master's degree in social work, nursing, or related field
  • Supervised experience hours (varies by certification)

Four Regulatory Models

Model 1: Underlying Profession

Most Common

State regulates the underlying profession (nursing, social work, counseling), not case management specifically.

Example: Hospital case manager must be RN; mental health case manager must be LMSW.

Model 2: Voluntary Certification

All States

Employers require or prefer CCM or equivalent certification for professional advancement.

Example: "RN with CCM certification preferred"

Model 3: State-Specific Cert

Limited States

State creates specific case management certification for certain settings.

States: Utah (BHCM), Florida (CBHCM), Texas (various)

Model 4: Social Work Compact

22 States

LCSWs can practice across compact state lines (in-person and telehealth).

Benefit: Multi-state practice privilege

National Certifications

CCM: Certified Case Manager

Issued By: Commission for Case Manager Certification (CCMC)

Status: Most widely recognized; industry standard

Accreditation: NCCA-accredited

Eligibility (Must Meet All):

  • Licensure OR Education:
    • Current licensure (RN, LCSW, LPC, etc.) OR
    • Bachelor's/graduate degree in health/human services
  • Experience (one of):
    • 12 months supervised by CCM, OR
    • 24 months unsupervised, OR
    • 12 months as supervisor
  • Good Moral Character

Exam:

  • 180 multiple-choice questions
  • Computer-based testing year-round
  • Covers principles, practices, healthcare management

Maintenance:

  • 80 CE hours every 5 years OR re-take exam

ACM: Accredited Case Manager

Issued By: American Case Management Association

Eligibility: RNs and Social Workers only

Focus: Medical/healthcare case management

Requirements:

  • Active RN or social work license
  • Specified case management experience
  • Pass ACM examination
  • Continuing education for renewal

ANCC: Nursing Case Management

Issued By: American Nurses Credentialing Center

Certification: CMGT-BC

Eligibility: Registered Nurses only

Requirements:

  • Active RN license
  • Bachelor's degree in nursing or related field
  • 2,000 hours case management practice in last 3 years
  • 30 hours continuing education in case management
  • Pass ANCC exam

Best For:

  • RNs specializing in case management
  • Hospital and healthcare settings
  • Nursing-specific career advancement

Social Work Licensure Compact

22 States Participate (as of 2025)

What it means: LCSWs can practice across compact state lines for both in-person and telehealth services.

Compact States:

Alabama, Arizona, Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, New Hampshire, Ohio, Rhode Island, South Dakota, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Washington

Benefits:

  • Single license, multi-state privilege
  • Particularly important for telehealth case management
  • No need for separate licenses in each compact state
  • Simplified interstate practice

State-Specific Certifications

Utah: Behavioral Health Case Management Certificate

Effective: January 2025

Requirement: For behavioral health case managers in Utah

In addition to: Underlying professional license

Florida: Certified Behavioral Health Case Manager (CBHCM)

Requirement: For Medicaid-billable behavioral health services

Setting: Behavioral health agencies

Texas: Various Position Requirements

Requirement: Certain state positions require CCM or equivalent

Setting: State agencies and Medicaid programs

Professional DOs and DON'Ts

✓ DO:

  • Obtain underlying professional license (RN, LCSW, etc.)
  • Pursue CCM certification for career advancement
  • Maintain professional liability insurance
  • Complete continuing education requirements
  • Stay within your scope of practice
  • Document all case management activities
  • Coordinate care across providers
  • Advocate for client needs appropriately
  • Follow HIPAA and confidentiality standards
  • Join professional associations (CMSA, etc.)

✗ DON'T:

  • Practice without appropriate underlying license
  • Provide clinical services outside your scope
  • Diagnose conditions without proper credentials
  • Bill for services without proper certification
  • Practice in a state without proper licensure
  • Misrepresent your credentials or certifications
  • Violate client confidentiality
  • Engage in dual relationships with clients
  • Accept commissions or kickbacks from providers
  • Guarantee specific outcomes to clients

Universal Ethical Principles

Required for All Case Managers:

  • Professional Licensure: Maintain active, unrestricted license in your discipline
  • Scope of Practice: Work only within your professional boundaries
  • Client Advocacy: Prioritize client needs while coordinating care
  • Confidentiality: Maintain strict HIPAA compliance and privacy standards
  • Cultural Competency: Provide culturally sensitive, inclusive services
  • Continuing Education: Stay current on best practices and regulations
  • Professional Boundaries: Avoid conflicts of interest and dual relationships
  • Documentation: Maintain thorough, accurate case records
  • Coordination: Work collaboratively with healthcare team members
  • Ethics: Follow professional codes of ethics (NASW, ANA, CMSA, etc.)

Getting Started in Case Management

Recommended Path:

  1. Obtain Bachelor's or Master's degree in nursing, social work, or related field
  2. Get underlying professional license (RN, LCSW, LPC, etc.)
  3. Gain supervised case management experience (12-24 months)
  4. Pursue CCM certification (or ACM/ANCC if eligible)
  5. Obtain professional liability insurance
  6. Check state-specific requirements for your location
  7. Join professional associations (CMSA, NASW, ANA, etc.)
  8. Commit to ongoing continuing education
  9. Consider Social Work Compact if applicable
