Understanding the Categories:

  • Yes: Permitted without restrictions
  • No: Not permitted or requires specific license
  • With Certification: Requires state certification to practice independently
  • For Medicaid/Specific Settings: Required for specific billing or employment settings only

Important: Even in unregulated states, ethical practice requires professional liability insurance, clear scope definitions, and ensuring clients have concurrent clinical support.

Universal Ethical Principles for Recovery Professionals

Required Across All Practice Areas:

  • Client Must Have Clinical Support: For recovery work, clients need concurrent support from licensed therapists or counselors
  • Stay in Your Lane: Never diagnose, treat mental health conditions, or provide clinical services without proper licensure
  • Make Referrals: Build and actively use a comprehensive referral network for client needs outside your scope
  • Professional Liability Insurance: Maintain appropriate insurance coverage for all practice areas
  • Clear Written Agreements: Provide written scope clarification and service agreements to all clients
  • Continuing Education: Stay current on laws, ethics, and best practices through ongoing professional development
  • Confidentiality & HIPAA: Understand and maintain appropriate confidentiality standards
  • No Dual Relationships: Avoid conflicts of interest and inappropriate personal relationships with clients
  • Cultural Competency: Respect diversity and provide culturally sensitive services
  • Supervision When Required: Obtain appropriate clinical supervision as mandated by your role
