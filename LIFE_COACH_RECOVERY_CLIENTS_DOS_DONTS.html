CRITICAL CONTEXT

Who This Guide Is For

Life coaches (not specifically "recovery coaches") who:

  • Work with clients on life goals, career, relationships, personal development
  • Have clients who happen to be in recovery from substance use/addiction
  • Want to know: "Can I work with this person? What can I discuss? What's off-limits?"

The Core Question

"I'm a life coach. My client mentioned they're in recovery. Can I still work with them?"

General Answer: YES, in most states, BUT you must understand the boundaries.

Universal Framework: The "Context vs. Target" Principle

‚úÖ RECOVERY AS CONTEXT (Generally Okay):

  • Client is in recovery ‚Üí You coach them on career goals
  • You're aware of their recovery ‚Üí You support life goals around it
  • Recovery is part of their life ‚Üí You help them build life that supports recovery

‚ùå RECOVERY AS TARGET (Requires License):

  • You're treating the addiction
  • You're modifying behaviors to address the substance use disorder
  • You're creating strategies to maintain sobriety (that's clinical)
  • You're diagnosing or assessing their addiction

Key Principle Across All States

You can coach someone WHO IS in recovery.

You cannot treat their recovery, addiction, or substance use disorder.

STATE-BY-STATE GUIDE

ALABAMA

Can life coaches work with clients in recovery? YES, with boundaries

‚úÖ YOU CAN:

  • Work with clients who are in recovery on life goals (career, relationships, personal development)
  • Be aware they're in recovery and support their life goals around that
  • Discuss how their recovery program fits into their life schedule
  • Help them set and achieve non-clinical life goals
  • Say: "I'm glad you're in recovery and have clinical support. Let's work on your career transition."

‚ùå YOU CANNOT:

  • Treat emotional and mental disorders or substance use disorders
  • Use counseling or psychotherapy to evaluate and treat their addiction
  • Create relapse prevention plans (that's clinical)
  • Diagnose substance use disorder or assess their addiction severity
  • Say: "Let me help you treat your addiction"

Required Client Situation:

  • ‚úÖ Client is in recovery (stable, not actively using in crisis)
  • ‚úÖ Client has or is willing to get therapist/counselor
  • ‚úÖ Client understands you're a life coach, not treating their addiction

‚ö†Ô∏è CALIFORNIA - HIGHEST RISK STATE

Can life coaches work with clients in recovery? YES, BUT CALIFORNIA IS HIGHEST RISK STATE - BE EXTRA CAREFUL

Critical California Note:

  • California uses "PRACTICE PROTECTION" not just "title protection"
  • It doesn't matter what you call yourself - if you're DOING the activity, you need license
  • If you're modifying behaviors TO TREAT addiction ‚Üí need license
  • If you're coaching on life goals FOR SOMEONE in recovery ‚Üí generally okay

California Best Practices:

  • More conservative here than other states
  • Clear written disclaimers
  • Document client's clinical support
  • Stay far from clinical language
  • When in doubt, refer out
  • Professional liability insurance ESSENTIAL

Note: Similar guidelines apply across all 50 states + DC. The key principles remain consistent:

  • Life coaching is unregulated in all states
  • You can work with clients in recovery on life goals
  • You cannot provide therapy, diagnosis, or clinical treatment
  • Clients must have clinical support for their recovery

UNIVERSAL BEST PRACTICES: ALL STATES

Pre-Engagement Checklist

Before working with a client in recovery, ensure:

1. ‚úÖ Client Status:

  • ‚òê Client is in stable recovery (not actively using in crisis)
  • ‚òê Client has completed or is in formal treatment
  • ‚òê Client has established sobriety/recovery time

2. ‚úÖ Clinical Support:

  • ‚òê Client has licensed therapist, counselor, or clinical provider
  • ‚òê Client is willing to maintain this relationship
  • ‚òê You have contact info (optional but recommended with ROI)

3. ‚úÖ Scope Clarity:

  • ‚òê Client understands you're a life coach, not treating addiction
  • ‚òê Client understands coaching vs. therapy distinction
  • ‚òê Client agrees to written coaching agreement with disclaimers

4. ‚úÖ Your Preparedness:

  • ‚òê You have referral network for crisis/clinical needs
  • ‚òê You have professional liability insurance
  • ‚òê You understand your state's boundaries

Language Framework: All States

‚úÖ SAFE LANGUAGE - Use This:

  • "Let's work on your career goals."
  • "You're in recovery - that's great. Let's talk about [other life goals]."
  • "I'm glad you have a therapist for your recovery. I'll support you on [career/relationships/development]."
  • "Your recovery is important. Let's make sure your other goals support that."
  • "Tell me about your life goals now that you're in recovery."
  • "How can we build the life you want while you maintain your recovery program?"

‚ùå UNSAFE LANGUAGE - Never Use This:

  • "I'll help you beat your addiction."
  • "Let me treat your substance use disorder."
  • "I'll provide recovery counseling."
  • "You don't need a therapist, I can help you stay sober."
  • "I'll diagnose your addiction level."
  • "Let's work on your sobriety strategies." (clinical)
  • "I'll help you modify your behaviors around substance use." (clinical)
  • "I provide addiction treatment services."

The "Context vs. Target" Test

Ask yourself: What am I targeting?

‚úÖ OKAY - Recovery as Context:

  • Client in recovery ‚Üí I'm coaching them on career
  • Client's recovery is background ‚Üí I'm helping with life goals
  • Recovery activities are part of their schedule ‚Üí I'm helping them plan around it
  • They have recovery foundation ‚Üí I'm building other goals on it

‚ùå NOT OKAY - Recovery as Target:

  • I'm working on their sobriety strategies (clinical)
  • I'm helping them stay clean (clinical)
  • I'm treating their addiction (clinical)
  • I'm creating their relapse prevention plan (clinical)
  • I'm assessing their recovery progress (clinical)

Don't call yourself "recovery coach" without proper credentials in these states. Stay in general life coaching territory.

Includes: Alabama, Arkansas, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts (LICENSED), Michigan, Minnesota (DEFINED), Mississippi, Missouri, Montana (LICENSED), and others.

SUMMARY: THE THREE-LEVEL TEST

Level 1: Can I Work With This Client?

YES if:

  • ‚úÖ Client is in stable recovery (not actively using in crisis)
  • ‚úÖ Client has or will get licensed clinical provider
  • ‚úÖ Client wants life coaching, not addiction treatment
  • ‚úÖ Your state allows general life coaching (all states)
  • ‚úÖ You have referral network

NO if:

  • ‚ùå Client actively using without medical supervision
  • ‚ùå Client refuses clinical support
  • ‚ùå Client in acute crisis
  • ‚ùå Client wants addiction treatment
  • ‚ùå You'd be positioned as their addiction provider

Level 2: What Can I Discuss?

YES - Can discuss:

  • ‚úÖ Their life goals (career, relationships, development)
  • ‚úÖ How recovery activities fit into life schedule
  • ‚úÖ Building life that supports their recovery foundation
  • ‚úÖ Goal-setting around life areas
  • ‚úÖ Accountability for life goals
  • ‚úÖ Recovery as context for their life

NO - Cannot discuss (clinical territory):

  • ‚ùå Treating their addiction
  • ‚ùå Creating relapse prevention strategies (clinical)
  • ‚ùå Assessing their recovery progress (clinical)
  • ‚ùå Diagnosing addiction severity
  • ‚ùå Modifying behaviors to treat disorder
  • ‚ùå Recovery as the target of your work

Level 3: What's My Role?

Your role IS:

  • ‚úÖ Life coach working with someone who happens to be in recovery
  • ‚úÖ Supporting life goals while they maintain clinical support for recovery
  • ‚úÖ Helping them build life they want in recovery
  • ‚úÖ Accountability partner for life goals
  • ‚úÖ Support for life transitions

Your role IS NOT:

  • ‚ùå Their addiction treatment provider
  • ‚ùå Their therapist or counselor
  • ‚ùå Their recovery coach (if that's regulated in your state)
  • ‚ùå Their clinical support system
  • ‚ùå Replacement for licensed providers

CONCLUSION

Yes, You Can Work With Clients in Recovery

The answer is YES in all states, with these critical conditions:

  1. Client must have clinical support - This is non-negotiable
  2. You're coaching on LIFE, not treating ADDICTION - Stay clear on this
  3. Recovery is context, not target - You support someone WHO IS in recovery, not treat the recovery
  4. Follow your state's boundaries - Some states regulate "recovery coach" as specific role
  5. Stay in life coaching lane - Don't drift into clinical territory

The Key Distinction

Life coaching someone who is in recovery = Generally okay in all states

Treating addiction/providing recovery counseling = Requires license in all states

When in Doubt

  • Require client to have therapist
  • Stay focused on life goals, not disorder treatment
  • Use clear disclaimers
  • Document appropriately
  • Refer when needed
  • Be more conservative in high-risk states (CA, MA, MT, IL, UT)

Your Value

As a life coach, you provide tremendous value to clients in recovery:

  • Supporting their career goals
  • Helping rebuild relationships
  • Assisting with life transitions
  • Providing accountability for goals
  • Helping them build the life they want

You just can't treat the addiction itself. Leave that to the licensed professionals.
