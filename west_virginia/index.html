Overview

West Virginia certifies Peer Recovery Support Specialists (PRSS) through the West Virginia Certification Board for Addiction and Prevention Professionals (WVCBAPP), which is affiliated with the International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC). The PRSS credential is designed for individuals in recovery who wish to support others through peer-based services.

New for 2025

PRSS-MH (Mental Health) Credential - Mental Health Peer Recovery Support Specialist credential coming in 2025. Will be separate from addiction-focused PRSS. Check WVCBAPP website for updates and requirements.

Eligibility Requirements

Basic Requirements

  1. Personal Recovery Statement: A written statement of personal recovery from substance use disorder
  2. Education: High school diploma, GED, or higher education credential
  3. Age: Must be 18 years or older
  4. Recovery Status: Must be in active recovery and able to demonstrate lived experience

Experience Requirements

500 Hours of Volunteer or Paid Experience

  • Must be specific to the four PRSS domains
  • Can be volunteer or part-time work
  • Must be provided under direct supervision
  • Cannot be hours earned during treatment
  • Cannot be hours in support meetings (e.g., AA, NA meetings don't count)
  • Must document supervised work experience in the four PR domains

Four PRSS Domains

The domains cover core competencies in:

  1. Recovery support and advocacy
  2. Mentoring and education
  3. Recovery community engagement
  4. Professional responsibility and ethics

Education & Supervision Requirements

Required Education Hours

46 Clock Hours of Education

  • Must be specific to the required PRSS domains
  • Can include nationally recognized Peer Support Specialist Certification training
  • Recovery Coach Basics (McShin Model) PRSS Training
  • NAADAC-approved trainings
  • Other WVCBAPP-approved training programs

Required Supervision Hours

25 Hours of Supervision

  • Must be specific to the PRSS domains
  • Minimum of 6 hours in each of the four domains
  • Must be provided at least four times a month
  • Provided by organization's documented and qualified supervisory staff
  • Supervision must occur while working or volunteering in the capacity of a PRSS

Application Process

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Obtain Required Education
    • Complete 46 hours of domain-specific education
    • Attend NAADAC or WVCBAPP-approved training
  2. Gain Required Experience
    • Complete 500 hours of supervised volunteer or paid experience
    • Document experience in all four domains
    • Cannot use treatment or meeting attendance hours
  3. Complete Supervision Requirements
    • Obtain 25 hours of supervision (minimum 6 hours per domain)
    • Receive supervision at least 4 times per month
    • Work with qualified supervisor
  4. Prepare Personal Recovery Statement
    • Write statement of personal recovery
    • Submit with application
  5. Submit Application to WVCBAPP
    • Download application from wvcbapp.org/applications
    • Include all required documentation
    • Submit education verification
    • Submit supervision verification
    • Pay application fee
  6. Schedule and Pass Exam
    • Register for IC&RC PRSS exam
    • Bring valid government-issued photo ID
    • Pass the examination
  7. Receive Certification
    • WVCBAPP issues certification upon passing

Examination Requirements

IC&RC Peer Recovery Exam

  • Must pass the international standard exam
  • Administered through IC&RC
  • Valid government-issued photo ID required for testing

Practice Exam

  • Available for preparation
  • Helps candidates prepare for certification exam

Fees

Initial Certification

  • Application Fee: Contact WVCBAPP for current fees
  • Examination Fee: Set by IC&RC (contact WVCBAPP)

Becoming an Approved Provider

  • Approved Provider Application Fee: $324 (valid for 3 years)
  • Sponsoring Body Training Fee: $54 per training

Recertification

  • Recertification Fee: Every two years
  • Late Fee: Additional fee if late (contact WVCBAPP for specifics)

Recertification Requirements

Recertification Cycle

Every Two Years

Continuing Education Requirements

20 Hours of Continuing Education

  • Must include 6 hours of NAADAC/NCC AP National Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (NCPRSS) specific ethics
  • Remaining 14 hours can be related to peer support practice
  • Must be completed during two-year certification period

CE Documentation

  • Maintain certificates of completion
  • Submit CE documentation with recertification application
  • Track hours throughout certification period

Becoming an Approved Training Provider

Requirements to Become Provider

Step 1: Application

  • Complete Approved Provider Application at least 30 days before training date
  • Available at: wvcbapp.org/approved-trainings

Step 2: Pay Fees

  • Approved Provider Application Fee: $324 (valid for 3 years)
  • Sponsoring Body Training Fee: $54 per training

Step 3: Meet Standards

  • Training must cover required 46 hours of content
  • Must address all four PRSS domains
  • Curriculum must meet WVCBAPP standards
  • Must include assessment/evaluation component

Step 4: Approval Process

  • Submit application and fees
  • WVCBAPP reviews curriculum
  • Approval granted if standards met
  • Provider added to approved list

Approved Provider Benefits

  • Listed on WVCBAPP approved providers page
  • Can issue continuing education credits (CEUs)
  • Certificate hours count toward PRSS certification
  • Three-year approval period

Scope of Practice

What PRSS Professionals Do

  • Provide recovery support services based on lived experience
  • Mentor and support individuals in recovery
  • Facilitate recovery groups and activities
  • Connect individuals to recovery resources
  • Model recovery principles and practices
  • Advocate for individuals in recovery

Professional Responsibilities

  • Maintain ethical standards
  • Participate in supervision
  • Complete continuing education
  • Follow WVCBAPP Code of Ethics
  • Maintain professional boundaries
  • Practice within scope of PRSS role
