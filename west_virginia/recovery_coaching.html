Legal Status Overview

Status: PROHIBITED for independent practice (Medicaid services)

State-Defined Term: Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (CPRS)

Certification Body: West Virginia Certification Board for Addiction and Prevention Professionals (WVCBAPP) - IC&RC member

Certification Type: Voluntary (but required for Medicaid-funded positions)

Legal Definition and Certification

Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist (CPRS)

West Virginia uses IC&RC standards through WVCBAPP for peer recovery certification. The CPRS provides recovery support services based on lived experience in recovery.

Key Characteristics:

  • Voluntary certification (not state-mandated license)
  • IC&RC-aligned certification through WVCBAPP
  • Required for Medicaid-funded positions
  • Must work under supervision for Medicaid services

Certification Requirements

WVCBAPP CPRS Certification Requirements

Personal Recovery:

  • Minimum 2 years of continuous demonstrated recovery
  • Willingness to self-identify as person in recovery

Education:

  • 46 hours of education in four core domains

Experience:

  • 500 hours of peer support experience

Supervision:

  • 25 hours of supervision

Examination:

  • Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery exam

Four Core Domains (46 Hours Education)

  1. Advocacy: Advocating for individuals and recovery-oriented systems
  2. Mentoring: Sharing lived experience and guiding recovery journey
  3. Recovery Support: Providing recovery-focused services and resources
  4. Ethical Responsibility: Professional ethics and boundaries

Employment and Practice Restrictions

MEDICAID SERVICES: Independent Practice PROHIBITED

For Medicaid-Funded Services: Peer recovery support specialists CANNOT practice independently.

Mandatory Requirements for Medicaid Services:

  • Supervision: Must work under general supervision of master's-level behavioral health professional
  • Employer: Must be employed by Community Behavioral Health Center (CBHC) or Licensed Behavioral Health Center (LBHC)
  • Cannot: Bill Medicaid independently or operate private practice for Medicaid-covered services

Private-Pay Services: Status UNCLEAR

Medicaid-Funded Practice Requirements

Qualifying Employers for Medicaid Services

To provide Medicaid-reimbursable peer recovery services, must work for:

1. Community Behavioral Health Center (CBHC)

  • Licensed by WV Department of Health and Human Resources
  • Provides community-based behavioral health services
  • Medicaid-enrolled provider

2. Licensed Behavioral Health Center (LBHC)

  • State-licensed facility providing behavioral health treatment
  • Meets WV licensing requirements
  • Medicaid-enrolled provider

Supervision Requirement

Supervisor Qualifications: Master's-level behavioral health professional

Qualifying Supervisors May Include:

  • Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW)
  • Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC)
  • Licensed Psychologist
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist
  • Other master's-level licensed behavioral health professionals

Supervision Purpose:

  • Quality oversight of services
  • Support for peer recovery specialist
  • Ensure appropriate scope of practice
  • Medicaid compliance

Scope of Practice

Permitted Activities (Within Employment Setting)

Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialists may provide:

  • Peer support based on lived experience
  • Recovery coaching and mentoring
  • Advocacy for individuals in recovery
  • Connection to recovery resources and community supports
  • Facilitation of peer support groups
  • Recovery education and skill development
  • Support for recovery goal-setting
  • Assistance navigating systems and services
  • Role modeling recovery lifestyle

Prohibited Activities

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Cannot diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Treatment Planning: Cannot create clinical treatment plans
  • Therapy/Counseling: Cannot provide psychotherapy or clinical counseling
  • Medical Advice: Cannot provide medical or psychiatric advice
  • Independent Medicaid Billing: Cannot bill Medicaid independently
  • Unsupervised Medicaid Services: Cannot provide Medicaid services without master's-level supervision

Practical Guidance

To Practice as CPRS in West Virginia (Medicaid Services)

  1. Meet Recovery Requirement: 2+ years continuous recovery
  2. Complete Education: 46 hours training in four core domains
  3. Gain Experience: 500 hours peer support experience
  4. Receive Supervision: 25 hours of supervision
  5. Pass Examination: IC&RC Peer Recovery exam through WVCBAPP
  6. Obtain CPRS Certification: Complete WVCBAPP certification process
  7. Secure Employment: Obtain position with CBHC or LBHC
  8. Work Under Supervision: Establish supervision with master's-level professional
  9. Maintain Certification: Complete continuing education requirements
