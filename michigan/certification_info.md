# Michigan Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Recovery Mentor (CPRM)**

## Certifying Body
**Michigan Certification Board for Addiction Professionals (MCBAP)**

**Contact Information:**
- Organization: MCBAP
- Website: https://mcbap.com
- CPRM Page: https://mcbap.com/mcbap-certifications/
- Email: Contact through website

**State Recognition:**
- Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS)
- Office of Recovery Oriented Systems of Care (OROSC)
- Peer Recovery Information: https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs/keep-mi-healthy/mentalhealth/drugcontrol/peer-recovery-information

## Personal Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Recovery Status:** Must have been successful in own recovery from substance use disorder
- **Recovery Duration:** Minimum of 2 years of continuous successful recovery/sobriety typically expected
- **Personal Commitment:** Statement of personal commitment to recovery required
- **Residency:** Must live or work within the State of Michigan fifty-one percent (51%) of the time
- **Education:** Minimum of high school or GED diploma

## Training Requirements

### Domain-Specific Education
**Minimum:** 25 hours specific to IC&RC Domains
**Domain Distribution (minimum 5 hours in each):**
- Advocacy
- Mentoring and Education
- Recovery Processes and Wellness Planning
- Ethical Responsibility
- Clinical Ethics: 6 hours in MCBAP-approved recovery clinical ethical behavior and decision making

## Experience Requirements

### Supervised Service Hours
- **Minimum:** 500 hours providing SUD peer recovery support services
- **Setting:** Services to those with substance use disorder
- **Supervision:** Minimum of 25 or more hours documented supervision
- **Documentation:** All hours must be properly documented

## Examination Requirements

### IC&RC Peer Recovery (PR) Examination
- **Type:** International Certification and Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC) exam
- **Status:** IC&RC Reciprocal credential
- **Benefit:** Allows for potential reciprocity with other IC&RC states

## Professional Requirements

### Ethics and Standards
- **Code of Ethics:** Must sign and adhere to MCBAP Recovery Support Code of Ethical Standards
- **Professional Conduct:** Maintain professional boundaries and ethical practice
- **Continuing Professional Development:** Ongoing education and training

## Application Process

1. Verify eligibility (2 years recovery, residency, education)
2. Complete 25 hours of IC&RC domain-specific education
3. Include 6 hours of MCBAP-approved clinical ethics training
4. Complete 500 hours of supervised SUD peer recovery support work
5. Obtain 25+ hours of documented supervision
6. Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery examination
7. Sign MCBAP Recovery Support Code of Ethical Standards
8. Submit application to MCBAP
9. Receive CPRM certification

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Michigan's Program
- **IC&RC Approved:** Nationally recognized certification with reciprocity potential
- **500-Hour Requirement:** Substantial practical experience required
- **2-Year Recovery Minimum:** Ensures stability in recovery
- **Documented Supervision:** 25+ hours ensures quality mentoring
- **51% Residency:** Flexible for border residents
- **Multiple Training Options:** Various providers and schedules available
- **Bundle Options:** Cost-effective training packages available
- **Strong Ethics Component:** 6 hours specifically on clinical ethical behavior

### Important Considerations
- **Recovery Time:** 2 years continuous recovery is standard expectation
- **Supervision Documentation:** Keep detailed records of supervision hours
- **IC&RC Exam:** National exam ensures standardization
- **500 Hours:** Plan for this substantial time commitment
- **Domain Coverage:** Must complete minimum 5 hours in each domain
- **Ethics:** 6-hour clinical ethics requirement is in addition to domain ethics hours
- **Residency:** Must live/work in Michigan 51%+ of time

### Best Practices for Applicants

**Before Starting:**
1. Ensure you have 2 years continuous recovery
2. Verify Michigan residency/work status (51%+)
3. Have high school diploma/GED ready
4. Connect with potential supervisors

**During Training:**
1. Choose training provider carefully - consider bundles for cost savings
2. Keep detailed records of all training hours and domains covered
3. Begin accumulating 500 service hours early
4. Arrange for qualified supervision from the start
5. Document all supervision sessions thoroughly
6. Join peer support networks for support and mentorship

**Certification Process:**
1. Verify all domain hours are complete (minimum 5 each)
2. Ensure 6 hours of MCBAP clinical ethics are documented
3. Confirm 500 service hours with documentation
4. Have 25+ supervision hours documented
5. Prepare for IC&RC exam
6. Submit complete application to MCBAP
7. Sign and commit to Code of Ethical Standards
