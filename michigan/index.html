Official Certification: Certified Peer Recovery Mentor (CPRM)

Certification Overview

A Certified Peer Recovery Mentor (CPRM) in Michigan is a person who has been successful in their own recovery from substance use disorder and has been trained and certified to provide peer support services. The Michigan CPRM credential is an IC&RC Reciprocal credential, providing national recognition and potential reciprocity with other IC&RC states.

Certifying Body

Michigan Certification Board for Addiction Professionals (MCBAP)
Website: mcbap.com
CPRM Page: MCBAP Certifications
State Recognition: Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS), Office of Recovery Oriented Systems of Care (OROSC)

Requirements

Personal Requirements

  • Recovery Status: Successful in own recovery from substance use disorder
  • Recovery Duration: Minimum of 2 years of continuous successful recovery/sobriety typically expected
  • Residency: Must live or work within Michigan fifty-one percent (51%) of the time
  • Education: Minimum of high school or GED diploma
  • Personal Commitment: Statement of personal commitment to recovery required

Training Requirements

Minimum: 25 hours specific to IC&RC Domains

Domain Distribution (minimum 5 hours in each):

  • Advocacy
  • Mentoring and Education
  • Recovery Processes and Wellness Planning
  • Ethical Responsibility
  • Clinical Ethics: 6 hours in MCBAP-approved recovery clinical ethical behavior and decision making

Experience Requirements

  • Service Hours: 500 hours providing SUD peer recovery support services
  • Supervision: Minimum of 25 or more hours documented supervision
  • Documentation: All hours must be properly documented

Examination

  • Exam Type: IC&RC Peer Recovery (PR) examination
  • Recognition: IC&RC Reciprocal credential allows for potential reciprocity with other IC&RC states

Ethics and Professional Standards

Must sign and adhere to MCBAP Recovery Support Code of Ethical Standards. Maintain professional boundaries, ethical practice, and ongoing continuing professional development.

IC&RC Reciprocity

Michigan CPRM is IC&RC approved, allowing for potential reciprocity with other IC&RC member states. Contact MCBAP for reciprocity application process. Verify requirements with target state before assuming automatic transfer.
