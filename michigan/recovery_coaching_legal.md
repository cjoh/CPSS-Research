# Michigan Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Certified Peer Recovery Mentor (CPRM)

**Definition:** "For persons who have been successful in their own recovery and are prepared to share their experience with others at earlier stage of recovery from alcohol and/or drug dependency, working as part of organized clinical team."

**Administered by:** Michigan Certification Board for Addiction Professionals (MCBAP)

## Coaching vs. Therapy
Work as part of organized clinical team. Share personal recovery experience. Distinct from clinical treatment.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Certification required; part of clinical team

**Requirements:**
- Live or work in Michigan 51% of time
- Successful in own recovery
- Prepared to share experience
- Complete CPRM Academy (80-hour training) or MCBAP-approved alternative
- $65 non-refundable two-year certification fee

**Renewal:**
- Every 2 years before expiration
- 10 hours per year MCBAP-approved CE
- Must include 6 hours professional ethics education

**Citation:** MCBAP certification standards; Michigan Dept of Health and Human Services oversight

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** Through state certification (CPRM)
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Part of clinical team; peer support role
- **Independent practice?** Work as part of organized clinical team
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- Work as part of clinical team
- 80-hour training requirement
- Biennial certification
- Ethics education required
