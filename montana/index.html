Certification Overview

Montana's Certified Behavioral Health Peer Support Specialist (CBHPSS) program provides certification for individuals with lived experience who want to support others in recovery. Certification is managed by Montana's Board of Behavioral Health.

Montana Board of Behavioral Health

Website: boards.bsd.dli.mt.gov - CBHPSS

Montana's Peer Network: mtpeernetwork.org

Requirements

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • Identify as having a behavioral health condition
  • "In recovery" for 2 years, defined partially as no hospitalizations longer than 72 hours and no incarceration

Training Requirements

  • Board approved 40-hour course with emphasis on ethics
  • Pass training exam
  • Training now requires: 3 part-day instructor led virtual sessions, homework assignments, and 3 in-person days

Background Check

  • Background check required for certification

Training Providers

Montana's Peer Network (MPN)

  • Website: mtpeernetwork.org/trainings
  • Peer Support 101: Meets National Practice Standards and Montana guidelines for CBHPSS Certification
  • Primary training provider instrumental in developing CBHPSS workforce in Montana
  • Provides both certification training and continuing education

Behavioral Health Training Program

  • Website: Montana Office of Rural Health - Behavioral Health Training Program
  • Currently FULL for 2024/2025 academic year
  • Availability expected September 2025

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete 40-hour Board approved training
  2. Pass Training Exam: Successfully complete exam
  3. Background Check: Complete required background check
  4. Apply for Certification: Submit application to Montana Board of Behavioral Health
