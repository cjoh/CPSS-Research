Certification Overview

Certified Recovery Peer Advocates (CRPAs) draw from personal experience with substance use and professional training to provide non-clinical support services as identified in the patient's treatment or recovery plan. Peers work in a variety of settings including OASAS outpatient programs under the supervision of a qualified healthcare professional, hospital emergency departments, and various home and community-based service settings.

Important 2025 Update

New IC&RC exam version launching July 3, 2025, with new Harm Reduction section. Candidates planning to test after 7/3/25 should take additional Harm Reduction training.

Personal Recovery Lived experience with substance use disorder and recovery

Application Fee $100

Training Components

50-Hour Foundational Training Includes:

  • Recovery Coach Training: 30 hours
  • Peer Ethics: 16 hours
  • Medication Supported Recovery (MSR): 4 hours

Five Performance Domains Covered:

  1. Advocacy
  2. Ethical Responsibility
  3. Mentoring/Education
  4. Recovery/Wellness Support
  5. Harm Reduction (new section as of July 2025)

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete all 50 hours of NYCB-approved foundational training through an approved provider
  2. Gain Experience: Complete 500 hours of related volunteer or work experience with 25 hours of qualified supervision
  3. Submit Application:
    • Create account at Certemy portal: nycb.certemy.com
    • Upload training certificates showing completion of all required hours
    • Submit documentation of experience and supervision hours
    • Pay application fee
  4. Schedule Exam: Register for the IC&RC Peer Recovery exam through the testing portal
  5. Pass Exam: Successfully pass the IC&RC certification examination
Apply Online via Certemy Visit NYCB Website

Renewal Requirements

Renewal Deadline: Applications must be submitted at least 45 days before expiration to avoid late fees

Requirements:

  • 28 hours of approved recovery-specific continuing education
  • 4 hours must be in peer and clinical ethics training
  • All continuing education must be from NYCB-approved providers
  • Renewal Fee: $100

Late Fees:

  • Less than 45 days before expiration OR less than 6 months after: $25
  • 6-12 months after expiration: $50
  • Over 1 year after expiration: $100 + $100 renewal fee = $200 total

Becoming a Training Provider

IUA-NYCB Trainer Registry

Website: asapnys.org/trainers-training

Email: NYCB_Inquiries@IUANY.org

Requirements:

  • Join the NYCB Trainer Registry as a member
  • Training must cover 54 KSAs (Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities) required for CRPA
  • Submit training curriculum for approval review
  • Training approval is not automatic or guaranteed

Benefits of Registry Membership:

  • Free review of registry member trainings submitted under NYCB Training Approval program
  • Free listing of approved trainings on dedicated NYCB webpages
  • Personalized digital badge confirming training approval status
  • Badge can be placed on training certificates and promotional materials

Application Timeline:

  • Applications completed before the 15th of the month: Reviewed that calendar month
  • Applications completed on or after the 16th: Roll over to following month for review

OASAS Certification (for Clock-Hour Credit)

Website: oasas.ny.gov/training/certified-education-and-training-providers

Requirements:

  1. Apply for OASAS certification as education and training provider
  2. Attend a "Train the Trainers" event hosted by OASAS
  3. Only OASAS-certified providers can offer OASAS credentialing clock-hour credit
