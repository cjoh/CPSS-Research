# New York Peer Support Specialist Certification Information

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Recovery Peer Advocate (CRPA)**

## Certifying Body
**New York Certification Board (NYCB)** - Managed by InUnity Alliance (formerly ASAP)
- Website: https://www.asapnys.org/ny-certification-board/
- Email: NYCB_Inquiries@IUANY.org
- Contact: Elisabeth Kranson, Director of Certification - ekranson@iuany.org
- Contact: Cathie Gifford, Program Associate - cgifford@iuany.org

**Oversight:** New York State Office of Addiction Services and Supports (OASAS)
- Website: https://oasas.ny.gov/recovery/become-certified-recovery-peer-advocate

## Certification Overview
Certified Recovery Peer Advocates (CRPAs) draw from personal experience with substance use and professional training to provide non-clinical support services as identified in the patient's treatment or recovery plan. Peers work in a variety of settings including OASAS outpatient programs under the supervision of a qualified healthcare professional, hospital emergency departments, and various home and community-based service settings.

## Detailed Requirements

### Education Requirements
- High school diploma or equivalent (GED)
- Completion of 50 hours of NYCB-approved foundational training

### Training Curriculum Components (50 hours total)
1. **Recovery Coach Training** (30 hours)
2. **Peer Ethics** (16 hours)
3. **Medication Supported Recovery (MSR)** (4 hours)

### Five Performance Domains Covered:
1. Advocacy
2. Ethical Responsibility
3. Mentoring/Education
4. Recovery/Wellness Support
5. Harm Reduction (new section as of July 2025)

### Experience Requirements
- **Minimum 500 hours** of related volunteer or work experience in peer support services
- Must be completed under qualified supervision

### Supervision Requirements
- **Minimum 25 hours** of supervision by qualified supervisory staff during the 500-hour experience requirement

### Personal Recovery Requirement
- Must have personal lived experience with substance use disorder and recovery

### Examination Requirements
- Pass the **International Certification and Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC)** Peer Recovery certification exam
- Exam fee: $83
- **IMPORTANT UPDATE:** New IC&RC exam version launching July 3, 2025, with new Harm Reduction section
- Candidates planning to test after 7/3/25 should take additional Harm Reduction training

### Background Check
- Criminal background check may be required by individual employers or programs

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Training
Complete all 50 hours of NYCB-approved foundational training through an approved provider

### Step 2: Gain Experience
Complete 500 hours of related volunteer or work experience with 25 hours of qualified supervision

### Step 3: Submit Application
1. Create account at Certemy portal: https://nycb.certemy.com/entry/login
2. Upload training certificates showing completion of all required hours
3. Submit documentation of experience and supervision hours
4. Pay application fee

### Step 4: Schedule Exam
Register for the IC&RC Peer Recovery exam through the testing portal

### Step 5: Pass Exam
Successfully pass the IC&RC certification examination

### Application Fees
- **Standard Application Fee:** $100
- **IC&RC Exam Fee:** $83
- **Total Initial Cost:** $183 (plus training costs)

### Late Fees
- Applications submitted less than 45 days before expiration OR less than 6 months after expiration: $25 late fee
- Applications submitted 6-12 months after expiration: $50 late fee
- Applications submitted over 1 year after expiration: $100 late fee + $100 standard renewal fee ($200 total)

## Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
Certifications must be renewed according to the expiration date on your certification

### Renewal Application Timing
- Applications must be submitted at least **45 days before expiration** to avoid late fees
- Submit renewal through Certemy portal: https://nycb.certemy.com/entry/login

### Continuing Education Requirements
- **28 hours** of approved recovery-specific education
- Must include **4 hours** of peer and clinical ethics training
- All continuing education must be from NYCB-approved providers

### Renewal Fee
- **Standard Renewal Fee:** $100
- Late fees apply if submitted after deadline (see Late Fees section above)

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
To become an approved CRPA training provider in New York, you must work with both NYCB and OASAS.

### New York Certification Board (NYCB) Approval Process

#### IUA-NYCB Trainer Registry
**Website:** https://www.asapnys.org/trainers-training/
**Email:** NYCB_Inquiries@IUANY.org

#### Requirements:
1. Join the NYCB Trainer Registry as a member
2. Training must cover 54 KSAs (Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities) required for CRPA
3. Submit training curriculum for approval review
4. Training approval is not automatic or guaranteed

#### Benefits of Registry Membership:
- Free review of registry member trainings submitted under NYCB Training Approval program
- Free listing of approved trainings on dedicated NYCB webpages
- Personalized digital badge confirming training approval status
- Badge can be placed on training certificates and promotional materials

#### Application Timeline:
- Applications completed before the 15th of the month: Reviewed that calendar month
- Applications completed on or after the 16th: Roll over to following month for review

#### Training Certificate Requirements:
- Must include individualized NYCB training approval badge (issued upon approval)
- Badge confirms training meets NYCB standards

### OASAS Certification (for Clock-Hour Credit)

**Website:** https://oasas.ny.gov/training/certified-education-and-training-providers

#### Requirements:
1. Apply for OASAS certification as education and training provider
2. Attend a "Train the Trainers" event hosted by OASAS
3. Only OASAS-certified providers can offer OASAS credentialing clock-hour credit

#### Contact for OASAS Training Provider Certification:
Office of Addiction Services and Supports
**Training Information:** https://oasas.ny.gov/training
