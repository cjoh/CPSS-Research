Legal Definition

North Carolina uses "Certified Peer Support Specialist" (NCCPSS) rather than "recovery coach."

North Carolina Certified Peer Support Specialist (NCCPSS)

"People living in recovery from mental illness and/or substance use disorder who support others who can benefit from their experiences. They have met North Carolina eligibility requirements, completed training, and are certified through an application process."

Recovery Coaching

"Peer-based addiction recovery support service that is NON-CLINICAL and designed to engage others beyond recovery initiation through stabilization and into recovery maintenance."

Key Characteristic

Recovery coaching is explicitly NON-CLINICAL support.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Recovery Coaching (Non-Clinical)

  • Non-clinical support services
  • Peer-based addiction recovery support
  • Recovery engagement and stabilization
  • Maintenance support beyond initial recovery
  • Based on lived experience

Licensed Clinical Practice

Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselors (LCMHC) are regulated under NC Gen. Stat. Chapter 90, Article 24

  • Clinical assessment and diagnosis
  • Clinical treatment planning
  • Psychotherapy and counseling
  • Licensed professional activities

Important Note

No specific statutory language found defining the exact boundary between coaching and therapy. NCCPSS certification is administered separately by NC DHHS, creating a clear distinction from licensed professionals, but specific boundaries not statutorily defined.

Independent Practice Requirements

Certification Requirements

  • Must be 18 years or older
  • Verifiable one-year recovery experience
  • High school diploma, GED, or college/university transcript
  • Certificate proving 40 hours of state-approved NCCPSS training (completed within past two years)
  • Additional 20-hour coursework in relevant topics (psychology, counseling, substance use, social work)
  • Effective July 1, 2024: Three (3) hours of Ethics Training required
  • Two letters of recommendation

Training Cost

North Carolina residents pay $330 to $370 for state-approved training

Certification Duration

Two years; NCCPSS responsible for maintaining certification by renewing by due date

Important Limitations

No Reciprocity

The NCCPSS Program does NOT accept certification from other states and does NOT have reciprocity with other states.

Legal Citations

Statutory Framework

  • NC Gen. Stat. Chapter 90, Article 24 - Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselors Act (does NOT govern NCCPSS)
  • No specific statute found governing recovery coaches or peer support specialists

Administrative Program

  • NC DHHS Division of Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities and Substance Abuse Services administers NCCPSS certification
  • This is a certification program, NOT a statutory licensure program

Regulatory Authority

  • North Carolina Department of Health and Human Services (NC DHHS)
  • UNC Behavioral Health Springboard (training approval)

Important Considerations

The absence of clear statutory regulation does not mean unrestricted practice. Certified peer support specialists must adhere to certification standards and ethical guidelines, and should avoid any activities that could constitute unauthorized practice of licensed professions.

Best Practices

  • Review full NCCPSS certification guidelines and Code of Ethics
  • Maintain "non-clinical" designation in all services
  • Establish clear referral relationships with licensed professionals
  • Understand that exact parameters may require further clarification from NC DHHS
