Certification Overview

The NCCPSS Program certifies persons in recovery from serious mental illness (SMI) and substance use disorders (SUD) to support others on their recovery journey. North Carolina's program covers both mental health and substance use disorder recovery, with specialists using their lived experience to provide peer support services.

⚠ Important Notice

North Carolina does NOT accept other states' Peer Support Certifications. You must obtain NC certification to work in North Carolina.

Ethics Training Update (Effective July 1, 2024)

3 hours of Ethics Training now required for both initial certification and recertification. This counts toward the 20 hours of additional training required.

Personal Recovery Requirements

Minimum 18 months of lived experience in recovery from serious mental illness (SMI) and/or substance use disorder (SUD)

Must be in recovery for at least 18 months from all SMI and/or SUD

Important: Applicants with co-occurring mental health and substance use disorders must be in recovery from BOTH to qualify for certification

Training Requirements

Initial Certification Training (50 hours total)

  • Minimum 40 hours of face-to-face instruction
  • 10 hours completed through pre-work, homework, and/or in-person learning
  • Must be completed through NCCPSS Approved Course Training

Ethics Training (3 hours)

  • Required as of July 1, 2024
  • Counts toward the 20 hours of additional training required

Additional Training (20 hours)

  • Must include approved Ethics and Peer Support Boundaries training
  • Required for initial certification

Application Process

  1. Complete Approved Course Training: Complete 50 hours of NCCPSS Approved Course Training through a certified trainer
  2. Complete Additional Training: Complete 20 hours of additional training including 3 hours of ethics training
  3. Prepare Application Materials:
    • Obtain two reference letters
    • Sign Code of Ethics Policy
    • Gather training certificates
  4. Submit Application: Submit complete application with all required documents and $20 fee to NCCPSS Program
  5. Application Review: NCCPSS Program reviews application and issues certification if approved

Recertification Requirements

NC Certified Peer Support Specialists must renew their certification every two years

Renewal Notification:

  • CPSS will receive email notification 60-90 days before certificate expiration
  • Must renew by the last day of the month in which certification expires

Continuing Education Requirements:

  • 20 hours of additional training during the two-year period
  • 3 hours of Ethics and/or Peer Support Boundaries training (included in the 20 hours)

Late Recertification:

  • Available for 30 days after expiration date
  • Late fee of $20 applies (total $40 with processing fee)
  • After 30 days, certification lapses

Lapsed Certification:

If certification lapses (not renewed within 30-day late period):

  • Must reapply as new applicant
  • Since July 1, 2020: Must provide new training certificate for an approved course to reinstate

Becoming a Certified Trainer

Basic Requirements:

  1. Must be a current North Carolina Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS)
  2. CPSS certification must be current at all times to train NCCPSS courses
  3. Must meet all criteria established by the NCCPSS Program
  4. Must be certified for each specific Approved Course you wish to teach

Important Notes:

  • A Certified Trainer may teach for more than one Approved Course
  • Must be separately certified under each course taught
  • CPSS certification must remain current; if it lapses, cannot conduct training

Application Process:

  1. Obtain Trainer Certification Application Form from NCCPSS Program
  2. Complete application with all required information
  3. Submit to NCCPSS Program for review
  4. Program works with Course Owners to process and maintain certification
