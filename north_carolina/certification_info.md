# North Carolina Peer Support Specialist Certification Information

## Official Certification Name
**North Carolina Certified Peer Support Specialist (NCCPSS or CPSS)**

## Certifying Body
**North Carolina Certified Peer Support Specialist (NCCPSS) Program**
- Housed at: UNC-Chapel Hill School of Social Work, Behavioral Health Springboard (BHS)
- Website: https://pss.unc.edu/
- Oversight: NC Division of Mental Health, Developmental Disabilities, and Substance Use Services (DMH/DD/SUS)
- State Website: https://www.ncdhhs.gov/divisions/mental-health-developmental-disabilities-and-substance-use-services/certified-peer-support-specialists/

## Certification Overview
The NCCPSS Program certifies persons in recovery from serious mental illness (SMI) and substance use disorders (SUD) to support others on their recovery journey. North Carolina's program covers both mental health and substance use disorder recovery, with specialists using their lived experience to provide peer support services.

**Important Note:** North Carolina does NOT accept other states' Peer Support Certifications. You must obtain NC certification to work in North Carolina.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements
- **Minimum 18 months** of lived experience in recovery from serious mental illness (SMI) and/or substance use disorder (SUD)
- Must be in recovery for at least **18 months from all SMI and/or SUD**
- Applicants with co-occurring mental health and substance use disorders must be in recovery from **BOTH** to qualify for certification

### Education Requirements
- High school diploma or equivalent (GED)

### Training Requirements

#### Initial Certification Training
- **50 hours total** through NCCPSS Approved Course Training
  - Minimum **40 hours** of face-to-face instruction
  - **10 hours** completed through pre-work, homework, and/or in-person learning

#### Ethics Training (Effective July 1, 2024)
- **3 hours** of Ethics Training required for certification
- This counts toward the 20 hours of additional training required

#### Additional Training
- **20 hours** of Additional Training
- Must include approved Ethics and Peer Support Boundaries training

### Application Requirements
- Completed NCCPSS Approved Course Training (50 hours)
- Completed 20 Hours of Additional Training
- Two (2) reference letters
- Signed Code of Ethics Policy
- Payment of application fee ($20)

### Background Check
- Requirements may vary by employer

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Approved Course Training
Complete 50 hours of NCCPSS Approved Course Training through a certified trainer

### Step 2: Complete Additional Training
Complete 20 hours of additional training including ethics training

### Step 3: Prepare Application Materials
- Obtain two reference letters
- Sign Code of Ethics Policy
- Gather training certificates

### Step 4: Submit Application
Submit complete application with all required documents and fees to NCCPSS Program

### Step 5: Application Review
NCCPSS Program reviews application and issues certification if approved

## Application Fees
- **Standard Processing Fee:** $20
- **Late Recertification Fee:** $20 late fee + $20 processing fee = **$40 total**

## Recertification/Renewal Requirements

### Renewal Period
NC Certified Peer Support Specialists must renew their certification **every two years**

### Renewal Notification
- CPSS will receive email notification 60-90 days before certificate expiration
- Must renew by the **last day of the month** in which certification expires

### Continuing Education Requirements
- **20 hours** of additional training during the two-year period
- **3 hours** of Ethics and/or Peer Support Boundaries training (included in the 20 hours)

### Late Recertification
- Available for **30 days** after expiration date
- Late fee of $20 applies (total $40 with processing fee)
- After 30 days, certification lapses

### Lapsed Certification
- If certification lapses (not renewed within 30-day late period)
- **Must reapply as new applicant**
- Since July 1, 2020: Must provide new training certificate for an approved course to reinstate

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

### Overview
To become authorized to train for the NCCPSS Program, you must become a Certified Trainer (CT).

### Certified Trainer Requirements

#### Basic Requirements
1. Must be a current North Carolina Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS)
2. CPSS certification must be **current at all times** to train NCCPSS courses
3. Must meet all criteria established by the NCCPSS Program
4. Must be certified for each specific Approved Course you wish to teach

#### Important Notes
- A Certified Trainer may teach for more than one Approved Course
- Must be separately certified under each course taught
- CPSS certification must remain current; if it lapses, cannot conduct training

### Application Process

#### Trainer Certification Application
1. Obtain Trainer Certification Application Form from NCCPSS Program
   - Available at: https://pss.unc.edu/sites/default/files/downloads/AC_NCCPSS_Trainer_Certification_Application_Form_3-2023.pdf

2. Complete application with all required information

3. Submit to NCCPSS Program for review

4. Program works with Course Owners to process and maintain certification
