Legal Framework

RCW 18.225 - Behavioral Health Professions

Washington's primary statute governing behavioral health professionals is RCW 18.225 (Mental Health Counselors, Marriage and Family Therapists, Social Workers).

Key Finding:

Recovery coaches are NOT included in the list of regulated professions under this chapter.

Regulated Professions (Not Recovery Coaches):

  • Licensed Mental Health Counselors
  • Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Licensed Social Workers
  • Licensed Psychologists
  • Other behavioral health professionals

Independent Practice

Apparent Permission by Default

In the absence of specific statutory prohibition, recovery coaching appears to be permitted as independent practice in Washington.

What This Suggests:

  • No state certification, training, or examination required
  • No mandatory supervision requirement
  • No specific employment setting requirement
  • Can operate independent recovery coaching practice
  • Can set own fees and terms of service

Key Risks of Unregulated Status

While unregulated status allows independent practice, it creates important risks:

Lack of Protection:

  • No Title Protection: Anyone can call themselves a "recovery coach"
  • No Quality Standards: No state-mandated training or competency requirements
  • No Regulatory Guidance: No clear scope of practice definitions
  • No Oversight: No regulatory body providing guidance or discipline

Liability Exposure:

  • Greater potential for civil liability without regulatory framework
  • No clear safe harbor for scope of practice
  • More difficult to establish standard of care in legal disputes

Scope of Practice Boundaries

What Recovery Coaches MUST AVOID

Even without specific recovery coach regulations, you CANNOT engage in activities constituting licensed professional practice:

Licensed Professional Activities (PROHIBITED):

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Diagnosing mental health or substance use disorders (requires license)
  • Psychotherapy: Providing therapy or clinical counseling (requires license)
  • Clinical Treatment: Creating treatment plans requiring professional judgment (requires license)
  • Psychological Assessment: Conducting clinical assessments (requires license)
  • Medication Advice: Providing psychiatric medication guidance (requires license)
  • Presenting as Licensed Professional: Representing yourself as counselor, therapist, or other licensed professional

Recovery Coaching Activities (Generally Permitted)

Activities that focus on peer support, lived experience, and non-clinical support:

  • Sharing lived experience of recovery
  • Providing peer support and encouragement
  • Recovery coaching and mentoring
  • Advocacy services
  • Connecting individuals to resources
  • Facilitating peer support groups
  • Recovery education and skill-building
  • Non-clinical goal-setting and planning
  • Accountability and check-ins

Risk Management in Unregulated Environment

Recommended Protective Measures

Because Washington does not regulate recovery coaches, it's especially important to implement strong risk management practices:

1. Voluntary Training and Certification

  • Obtain national certification (e.g., IC&RC, NAADAC)
  • Complete recognized recovery coach training programs
  • Document your training and qualifications

2. Professional Liability Insurance

  • CRITICAL: Obtain comprehensive professional liability insurance
  • Ensure policy covers recovery coaching activities
  • Maintain adequate coverage limits

3. Clear Service Agreements

  • Use written agreements with all clients
  • Clearly define non-clinical nature of services
  • Specify scope and limitations of services
  • Include informed consent language

4. Scope of Practice Documentation

  • Develop and follow clear scope of practice guidelines
  • Document services provided
  • Maintain referral list for clinical services
  • Never engage in activities requiring licensure

5. Ethical Standards

  • Adopt recognized ethical code (e.g., IC&RC Code of Ethics)
  • Follow ethical guidelines consistently
  • Document ethical decision-making

6. Business Structure

  • Consider LLC or other liability-limiting structure
  • Separate personal and business assets
  • Maintain proper business records
