# Washington State - Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS) Certification

## Overview
Washington State established the Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS) profession through legislation 2SSB 5555 passed in 2023. The certification became officially available on July 1, 2025, with the Washington State Department of Health (DOH) issuing certifications and the Health Care Authority (HCA) providing training.

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS)**

## Certifying Bodies
- **Washington State Department of Health (DOH)** - Issues certifications
- **Washington State Health Care Authority (HCA)** - Provides approved training and testing

## Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Pathway
To be eligible for CPSS certification, you must:

1. **Lived Experience**: Self-identify as a person with lived experience with mental health or substance use services with at least one year of living in recovery, OR be a parent or legal guardian of a child (under 18) with lived experience with mental health or substance use services

2. **Recovery Status**: Must demonstrate at least one year of recovery

3. **Training Completion**: Successfully complete the 80-hour HCA-approved CPSS training

4. **Examination**: Pass both the state CPSS oral and written exams

### Alternative Pathway - Certified Peer Support Specialist Trainee
For those who need supervised experience before full certification:

- Complete the Certified Peer Specialist Trainee application through DOH
- Pay the trainee application fee
- Complete 1,000 hours of supervised experience as a certified peer support specialist trainee
- Then apply for full CPSS certification

## Training Requirements

### 2025 Updates - IMPORTANT
**As of 2025, the training requirement increased from 40 hours to 80 hours.**

### Training Components

1. **Prerequisite Course**:
   - Complete the online prerequisite course
   - Send certification of completion to peer application support team

2. **80-Hour Core Training**:
   - HCA-approved certified peer support specialist training
   - Virtual and hybrid formats available (began March 2025)
   - Covers knowledge and skills necessary for effective peer support
   - Based on SAMHSA national standards

3. **Gap Training**:
   - Applicants who previously completed the 40-hour peer counselor training must complete HCA gap training
   - Available online without fee

### Training Format
- Virtual and hybrid trainings began in March 2025
- Training is coordinated and scheduled by HCA
- No cost for the training, training materials, or testing

## Application Process

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Complete Prerequisite Course**
   - Take online prerequisite course
   - Submit certification of completion to: **Peersupportapps@hca.wa.gov**

2. **Submit Application**
   - Complete the peer specialist application
   - Include Attestation of Recovery form (DOH 670-255)
   - Online applications anticipated Fall 2025
   - Paper applications available until online system launches

3. **Training Acceptance**
   - Be accepted by HCA for CPSS training
   - Approved applicants contacted by HCA peer outreach team

4. **Complete Training**
   - Successfully complete 80-hour HCA-approved training

5. **Pass Examinations**
   - Take and pass state CPSS written exam
   - Take and pass state CPSS oral exam

6. **Receive Certification**
   - DOH issues certification after successful completion

## Scope of Practice

### What Certified Peer Support Specialists Do
- Work with individuals and parents of children receiving mental health or substance use disorder services
- Provide peer support based on lived experience
- Support individuals in their recovery journey
- Use recovery-oriented principles
- Promote self-directed recovery and wellness

### Practice Standards
- Must work within defined scope of practice
- Follow ethical guidelines
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Participate in ongoing professional development
