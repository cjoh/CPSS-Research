Executive Summary

Key Finding: Intervention is NOT a Regulated Profession

Critical Insight: Professional intervention (family intervention for substance use, addiction, mental health) is NOT regulated at the state or federal level in any U.S. jurisdiction.

  • NO state requires a license to practice as an interventionist
  • NO state certification is mandatory
  • NO state examination is required
  • NO educational prerequisites are legally mandated
  • ANYONE can legally call themselves an "interventionist"

The Professional Framework

While intervention isn't regulated, serious professional interventionists typically have:

Education

  • Bachelor's degree in psychology, social work, counseling, or related field (common but not required)
  • Master's degree (many professionals have this)
  • Some have underlying clinical licenses (LCSW, LPC, etc.)

Training

  • Formal intervention training (14-150 hours depending on certification)
  • Evidence-based models (Johnson, ARISE, Systemic, Love First)
  • Family systems, motivational interviewing, substance use disorders

Certification (Voluntary)

  • CIP (Certified Intervention Professional)
  • BRI I/II (Board Registered Interventionist)
  • CAI (Certified ARISE Interventionist)

Insurance

  • Professional liability insurance (strongly recommended)
  • Business insurance

Voluntary Certifications

CIP: Certified Intervention Professional

Issued By: Pennsylvania Certification Board (with Association of Intervention Specialists)

Status: Globally recognized; introduced 2015

Requirements:

  • 150 hours training minimum:
    • 20 hrs: Intervention theory & practice
    • 20 hrs: Substance use disorders
    • 20 hrs: Mental health disorders
    • 12 hrs: Family systems
    • 12 hrs: Motivational interviewing
    • 12 hrs: Process addictions
    • 12 hrs: Case management
    • 12 hrs: Behavioral health ethics
    • 6 hrs: Cultural competency
  • 100 hours supervised work experience
  • Pass CIP examination
  • Continuing education for renewal

BRI: Board Registered Interventionist

Issued By: Various state certification boards

Two Levels: BRI I and BRI II

BRI I Requirements:

  • License or certification in counseling or related field
  • 2 years experience in the field
  • Professional liability insurance
  • 14 hours training (intervention history, family therapy, models)

BRI II Requirements:

  • All BRI I requirements
  • 5 total years experience conducting interventions
  • 14 additional training hours (co-occurring disorders, process addictions)

CAI: Certified ARISE Interventionist

Issued By: ARISE Network

Model: ARISE Intervention (evidence-based, invitational)

Requirements:

  • Complete ARISE Intervention Training
  • 24 Group Internship sessions
  • Professional liability insurance
  • Clinical licensure OR membership in professional organization

Focus: ARISE-specific methodology, invitational approach (vs. confrontational), family systems-based

State-by-State Legal Status

All 50 States + DC: UNREGULATED

NO STATE regulates intervention as a profession. The following applies to ALL jurisdictions:

  • ✅ NO license required
  • ✅ NO state certification mandatory
  • ✅ NO educational requirements
  • ✅ NO state examination
  • ✅ Anyone can legally practice as interventionist

Professional DOs and DON'Ts

✓ DO:

  • Get voluntary certification (CIP, BRI, CAI)
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Complete comprehensive training
  • Use evidence-based intervention models
  • Maintain clear boundaries with clients
  • Provide written service agreements
  • Make appropriate referrals
  • Continue professional development
  • Document all services thoroughly
  • Follow ethical guidelines

✗ DON'T:

  • Diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Provide therapy or clinical treatment
  • Prescribe medications or treatment plans
  • Practice without liability insurance
  • Guarantee intervention outcomes
  • Use coercive or confrontational tactics
  • Engage in dual relationships with clients
  • Work outside your scope of competence
  • Misrepresent credentials or experience
  • Practice if you have underlying license restrictions

Universal Ethical Principles

Required for All Interventionists:

  • Professional Liability Insurance: Essential protection for your practice
  • Stay in Your Lane: Never diagnose or provide clinical services without proper licensure
  • Evidence-Based Practice: Use recognized intervention models with research support
  • Written Agreements: Provide clear service agreements to all clients
  • Confidentiality: Maintain appropriate privacy standards
  • Referral Network: Build relationships with treatment providers and clinicians
  • Continuing Education: Stay current on best practices and ethical standards
  • Cultural Competency: Respect diversity in all client interactions
  • No Guarantees: Be honest about intervention success rates and limitations
  • Family-Centered: Work with entire family system, not just identified patient

Getting Started as an Interventionist

Recommended Path:

  1. Complete comprehensive intervention training (150+ hours recommended)
  2. Pursue CIP, BRI, or CAI certification
  3. Obtain professional liability insurance
  4. Gain supervised experience with established interventionists
  5. Study evidence-based intervention models
  6. Build referral network with treatment centers
  7. Develop business infrastructure and contracts
  8. Join professional associations (AIS, etc.)
  9. Commit to ongoing continuing education
