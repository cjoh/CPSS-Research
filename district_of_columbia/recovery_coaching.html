Legal Status Overview

RESTRICTED - SUPERVISION REQUIRED

State-Defined Term: Peer Specialist / Recovery Coaching and Mentoring

Regulatory Framework: DBH Certification with DCMR administrative regulations

Independent Practice: Likely restricted - supervision by qualified practitioner required

Legal Definition

District of Columbia defines Peer Specialists as self-identified people who are successful in the recovery process and use their experience to support others with similar experiences.

Peer Specialists: Through mutual respect and shared understanding based on similar experiences, they help people get into treatment, navigate aspects of the system of care and identify community resources necessary to make informed choices about their individual journeys of wellness and paths to recovery.

Recovery Coaching and Mentoring: Staff coaching and mentoring clients in assessing their current situation, defining personal goals, targeting areas for improvement, creating effective life-action plans, understanding relapse risk factors, and overcoming barriers to recovery.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

DC maintains clear distinctions between peer support and licensed clinical practice:

Prohibited Activities

  • Providing clinical treatment or therapy
  • Diagnosing mental health or substance use conditions
  • Developing clinical treatment plans independently
  • Practicing without supervision from qualified practitioner
  • Prescribing or recommending medications

Permitted Activities

  • Peer-to-peer support based on lived experience
  • Helping navigate system of care
  • Assisting with treatment engagement
  • Providing hope and role modeling
  • Connecting to community resources
  • Supporting recovery journey (non-clinical)

Independent Practice Requirements

Supervision Requirement

Critical Finding: A qualified practitioner provides clinical and administrative supervision to a Certified Peer Specialist.

The supervision requirement suggests that certified peer specialists in DC work under the supervision of qualified practitioners, which limits fully independent practice.

Certification Requirements

  • Certification must be approved by District of Columbia's Department of Health
  • Minimum of high school education or equivalent
  • Personal experience with recovery
  • Must pass certification exam after training
  • Complete Peer Specialist Certification Training Program (DBH-approved)
  • Training aligned with SAMHSA national standards

Qualified Practitioners (Supervisors)

Supervision may be provided by:

  • Psychiatrists
  • Psychologists
  • Independent clinical social workers
  • Advanced practice registered nurses
  • Registered nurses
  • Licensed professional counselors
  • Independent social workers
  • Addiction counselors

Scope of Practice

What Peer Specialists Do

  • Care navigation services
  • Providing referrals and crisis referrals
  • Supporting other behavioral health providers
  • Assessments and collaborative planning (non-clinical)
  • Goal setting with clients
  • Understanding factors that may increase relapse risks
  • Overcoming barriers that may inhibit recovery process

Important Considerations

Practice Restrictions

  • Full independent private practice without supervision appears unlikely
  • Must work under supervision of qualified practitioner
  • Likely requires organizational affiliation or contracted relationship with supervising entity
  • Employment or contracted relationship with behavioral health organizations, licensed practitioners, government agencies, or qualified facilities typically required

Likely Practice Settings

  • Behavioral health organizations
  • Community mental health centers
  • Government agencies (DBH, etc.)
  • Non-profit organizations providing peer services
  • Licensed practitioner offices (contracted)
