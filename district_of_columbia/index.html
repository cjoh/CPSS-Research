Overview

The District of Columbia Department of Behavioral Health (DBH) offers Peer Specialist Certification through the Consumer and Family Affairs Administration. The program provides comprehensive training for individuals with lived experience of mental illness and/or substance use disorders who wish to support others in their recovery journey. Training is offered at least three times per year with a competitive application process.

2025 Program Schedule

The DBH Peer Specialist Certification Training Program has three sessions scheduled in 2025. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis for upcoming sessions.

Official Certification Names

  • Certified Peer Specialist - For individuals with lived experience
  • Family Peer Specialist - For family members of individuals with behavioral health challenges
  • Youth Peer Specialist - For youth with lived experience

Eligibility Requirements

Basic Requirements

  1. Residency: Must be a District of Columbia resident
  2. Age: At least 18 years of age
  3. Education: High school diploma, GED, or higher
  4. Recovery Status: In recovery from mental illness and/or substance use disorder for two years or longer
  5. Lived Experience: Direct personal experience with mental health or substance use services

Important: Two-Year Recovery Requirement

DC requires at least two years of recovery - this is more stringent than some states. Make sure you meet this requirement before applying.

Application Requirements

When applying, candidates must submit:

  • Completed online application
  • Current resume
  • Proof of DC residency
  • Evidence of education (high school diploma, GED, or higher)
  • Two letters of reference (no older than one year)

Training Requirements

Core Curriculum Training

  • Comprehensive peer specialist training
  • Builds on participant's lived experience
  • Foundational competencies consistent with SAMHSA national standards
  • Recovery-oriented principles and practices
  • Ethics and professional boundaries
  • Wellness and self-care strategies
  • Cultural competency
  • Advocacy and empowerment skills

Field Practicum

80-Hour Field Practicum Required

  • Supervised practical experience after classroom training
  • Application of classroom learning
  • Competency demonstration
  • Must be completed to receive certification

Training Frequency

  • Trainings held at least three times per year
  • Applications accepted on rolling basis for upcoming sessions
  • Competitive selection process

Application Process

Competitive Application

Applications are ranked on a points basis with the following criteria:

Points Awarded For:

  1. Previous peer-related training
  2. Job experience in peer support or related fields
  3. Excellence of written responses to application questions
  4. Quality of essay question responses
  5. Demonstrated understanding of recovery principles
  6. Commitment to peer support work

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Verify Eligibility
    • Confirm DC residency
    • Ensure 2+ years of recovery
    • Verify educational requirements met
    • Confirm age requirement (18+)
  2. Prepare Application Materials
    • Gather proof of residency
    • Locate education documents
    • Request two letters of reference (must be current within 1 year)
    • Prepare current resume
    • Write thoughtful essay responses
  3. Submit Online Application
    • Complete application at DBH website
    • Include all required documentation
    • Submit essay responses
    • Provide reference letters
    • Upload all required documents
  4. Application Review
    • DBH reviews and ranks applications
    • Competitive scoring process
    • Top-ranked applicants selected
    • Selection notification sent
  5. Attend Training
    • Complete all classroom training sessions
    • Participate fully in training activities
    • Meet attendance requirements
  6. Complete Practicum
    • Complete 80-hour supervised field practicum
    • Document practicum hours
    • Demonstrate competencies
  7. Pass Certification Exam
    • Take DBH certification examination
    • Pass exam to receive certification
  8. Receive Certification
    • DBH issues Peer Specialist Certification
    • Begin work as Certified Peer Specialist

Waiver Option

Waiver Eligibility

For experienced peer support workers, a waiver process is available.

Waiver Requirements:

  1. Age: At least 18 years of age
  2. Education: High school diploma, GED, or higher
  3. Recovery Status: In recovery from mental illness and/or substance use disorder for two years or longer

AND ONE of the following:

  • Work Experience: Currently or formerly employed in a peer capacity within DC behavioral health system or its contractors for a minimum of five years
  • Existing Certification: Have current certification from another state or jurisdiction (in good standing)

Waiver Testing

  • Three waiver testing sessions scheduled in 2025
  • Test assesses knowledge and competencies
  • Bypass training requirement if qualified and pass test
  • Contact DBH for waiver application process

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much does the training cost?

A: Contact DBH at (202) 673-4377 or dbh.peersupport@dc.gov for current fee information.

Q: What if I'm not selected for training?

A: You can reapply for the next training session. Strengthen your application and try again.

Q: Can I get certified if I moved to DC recently?

A: You must be a DC resident. Proof of residency required. Length of residency requirements may vary - contact DBH.

Q: What's the difference between Peer Specialist and Family Peer Specialist?

A: Peer Specialists have personal lived experience. Family Peer Specialists are family members of individuals with behavioral health challenges. Different focus and training.

Q: What if I only have one year of recovery?

A: DC requires two years minimum. Continue your recovery journey and apply when you meet the requirement.

Q: Can I use my certification in other states?

A: Possibly, depending on reciprocity agreements. Contact receiving state's certification body. May need additional requirements.

Professional Practice Guidelines for District of Columbia

Regulation Level: District of Columbia has minimal specific regulations for recovery professionals. Ethical self-regulation and professional standards are critical.

Important: These guidelines apply to Recovery Coaches, Life Coaches, Interventionists, and Case Managers practicing in District of Columbia.

Recovery Coaches & Peer Support Specialists

✓ DO:

  • Ensure all clients have concurrent licensed clinical support
  • Maintain clear boundaries between peer support and therapy
  • Use coaching/peer support language, not clinical terminology
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Provide written coaching agreements defining scope
  • Make referrals when clients need clinical intervention
  • Pursue relevant certifications (CRSS, CPS, CPRS)
  • Share lived experience appropriately and helpfully
  • Maintain continuing education in recovery support
  • Build referral network with licensed professionals

✗ DON'T:

  • Diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Provide therapy or clinical treatment
  • Replace clinical care with coaching alone
  • Work with actively using clients without medical oversight
  • Use language that implies you're providing therapy
  • Practice without professional liability insurance
  • Work outside your scope of competence
  • Handle psychiatric crises without referring to professionals
  • Misrepresent your credentials or training
  • Engage in dual relationships with clients

Life Coaches Working with Recovery Clients

✓ DO:

  • Require clients to have therapist/addiction counselor
  • Focus on goal-setting and forward-looking coaching
  • Stay in life coaching scope (career, relationships, wellness)
  • Make immediate referrals for clinical issues
  • Use clear coaching contracts specifying you're not providing therapy
  • Maintain professional liability insurance
  • Build strong referral network of licensed professionals
  • Understand the line between coaching and counseling
  • Document referrals and scope boundaries
  • Consider ICF or other coaching certification

✗ DON'T:

  • Coach clients in active addiction without clinical support
  • Address trauma or clinical issues in coaching sessions
  • Diagnose or treat mental health conditions
  • Position coaching as alternative to therapy
  • Work with clients in psychiatric crisis
  • Use therapeutic techniques requiring clinical license
  • Coach clients who refuse to see licensed professionals
  • Guarantee recovery outcomes
  • Practice addiction counseling without license
  • Cross boundaries into clinical territory

Professional Interventionists

✓ DO:

  • Pursue professional certification (CIP, BRI, CAI)
  • Complete comprehensive intervention training (100+ hours)
  • Obtain professional liability insurance (essential)
  • Use evidence-based intervention models
  • Provide clear service agreements to families
  • Maintain referral relationships with treatment centers
  • Practice family-centered, compassionate intervention
  • Stay current on intervention best practices
  • Document all intervention services thoroughly
  • Follow ethical guidelines and professional standards

✗ DON'T:

  • Diagnose substance use or mental health disorders
  • Provide clinical treatment or therapy
  • Practice without professional liability insurance
  • Use coercive or overly confrontational tactics
  • Guarantee intervention outcomes to families
  • Work without proper training in intervention models
  • Misrepresent your credentials or success rates
  • Engage in unethical referral arrangements
  • Practice if you have active addiction issues
  • Cross professional boundaries with clients

Case Managers

✓ DO:

  • Obtain underlying professional license (RN, LCSW, LPC, etc.)
  • Pursue CCM or equivalent certification
  • Maintain professional liability insurance
  • Work within your professional scope of practice
  • Coordinate care across all providers effectively
  • Document all case management activities thoroughly
  • Follow HIPAA and confidentiality requirements
  • Advocate appropriately for client needs
  • Complete continuing education requirements
  • Join professional associations (CMSA, NASW, etc.)

✗ DON'T:

  • Practice without appropriate underlying license
  • Provide clinical services outside your scope
  • Diagnose conditions without proper credentials
  • Bill for services without proper certification
  • Practice across state lines without proper licensure
  • Violate client confidentiality or HIPAA
  • Accept inappropriate incentives from providers
  • Misrepresent credentials or certifications
  • Guarantee specific outcomes to clients
  • Engage in dual relationships with clients
