# District of Columbia - Peer Specialist Certification

## Overview
The District of Columbia Department of Behavioral Health (DBH) offers Peer Specialist Certification through the Consumer and Family Affairs Administration. The program provides comprehensive training for individuals with lived experience of mental illness and/or substance use disorders who wish to support others in their recovery journey. Training is offered at least three times per year with competitive application process.

## Official Certification Names
- **Certified Peer Specialist** - For individuals with lived experience
- **Family Peer Specialist** - For family members of individuals with behavioral health challenges
- **Youth Peer Specialist** - For youth with lived experience

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements
1. **Residency**: Must be a District of Columbia resident
2. **Age**: At least 18 years of age
3. **Education**: High school diploma, GED, or higher
4. **Recovery Status**: In recovery from mental illness and/or substance use disorder for **two years or longer**
5. **Lived Experience**: Direct personal experience with mental health or substance use services

### Application Requirements
When applying, candidates must submit:
- Completed online application
- Current resume
- Proof of DC residency
- Evidence of education (high school diploma, GED, or higher)
- Two letters of reference (no older than one year)

## Application Process

### Competitive Application
Applications are **ranked on a points basis** with the following criteria:

**Points Awarded For:**
1. Previous peer-related training
2. Job experience in peer support or related fields
3. Excellence of written responses to application questions
4. Quality of essay question responses
5. Demonstrated understanding of recovery principles
6. Commitment to peer support work

### Step-by-Step Application Process

**Step 1: Verify Eligibility**
- Confirm DC residency
- Ensure 2+ years of recovery
- Verify educational requirements met
- Confirm age requirement (18+)

**Step 2: Prepare Application Materials**
- Gather proof of residency
- Locate education documents
- Request two letters of reference (must be current within 1 year)
- Prepare current resume
- Write thoughtful essay responses

**Step 3: Submit Online Application**
- Complete application at: https://dbh.dc.gov/service/peer-specialist-certification-program
- Include all required documentation
- Submit essay responses
- Provide reference letters
- Upload all required documents

**Step 4: Application Review**
- DBH reviews and ranks applications
- Competitive scoring process
- Top-ranked applicants selected
- Selection notification sent

**Step 5: Attend Training**
- Complete all classroom training sessions
- Participate fully in training activities
- Meet attendance requirements

**Step 6: Complete Practicum**
- Complete 80-hour supervised field practicum
- Document practicum hours
- Demonstrate competencies

**Step 7: Pass Certification Exam**
- Take DBH certification examination
- Pass exam to receive certification

**Step 8: Receive Certification**
- DBH issues Peer Specialist Certification
- Begin work as Certified Peer Specialist

### Application Timeline
- **Applications Accepted**: Throughout the year for upcoming sessions
- **Training Sessions**: At least three times annually
- **Application Deadlines**: Vary by session (check website for specific dates)
- **Selection Notification**: After application review period
- **Training Duration**: Several weeks plus 80-hour practicum

## Waiver Option

### Waiver Eligibility
For experienced peer support workers, a waiver process is available.

**Waiver Requirements:**
1. **Age**: At least 18 years of age
2. **Education**: High school diploma, GED, or higher
3. **Recovery Status**: In recovery from mental illness and/or substance use disorder for two years or longer

**AND ONE of the following:**
- **Work Experience**: Currently or formerly employed in a peer capacity within DC behavioral health system or its contractors for a **minimum of five years**
- **Existing Certification**: Have current certification from another state or jurisdiction (in good standing)

### Waiver Testing
- Three waiver testing sessions scheduled in 2025
- Test assesses knowledge and competencies
- Bypass training requirement if qualified and pass test
- Contact DBH for waiver application process

## Important Notes

### DC Residency Requirement
- Must be a DC resident to apply
- Proof of residency required with application
- May include utility bill, lease agreement, or DC ID

### Two-Year Recovery Requirement
- Must have at least two years of recovery
- More stringent than some states
- Demonstrates stability and recovery foundation
- Self-attestation required

### Competitive Selection
- Not all applicants selected for training
- Applications ranked and scored
- Quality of application materials matters
- Previous peer training/experience helpful but not required
- Can reapply for subsequent sessions if not selected

### 80-Hour Practicum
- Required after classroom training
- Supervised field experience
- Must be completed to receive certification
- Placement coordinated through DBH
- Demonstrates practical competency

### Three Certification Types
DC offers certification for:
1. **Peer Specialists** - Adults with lived experience
2. **Family Peer Specialists** - Family members of individuals with behavioral health needs
3. **Youth Peer Specialists** - Young people with lived experience

Each has specific training and requirements. Specify which certification you're seeking in application.

### Waiver Option for Experienced Workers
Five years of peer work experience in DC or current certification from another state may qualify you for waiver testing:
- Bypass training requirement
- Take competency exam
- Faster pathway for experienced professionals
- Must still meet recovery and education requirements

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How much does the training cost?
**A:** Contact DBH at (202) 673-4377 or [email protected] for current fee information.

### Q: Can I work as a peer specialist while in training?
**A:** Depends on employer. Some may allow work under supervision while pursuing certification.

### Q: What if I'm not selected for training?
**A:** You can reapply for the next training session. Strengthen your application and try again.

### Q: Can I get certified if I moved to DC recently?
**A:** You must be a DC resident. Proof of residency required. Length of residency requirements may vary - contact DBH.

### Q: What's the difference between Peer Specialist and Family Peer Specialist?
**A:** Peer Specialists have personal lived experience. Family Peer Specialists are family members of individuals with behavioral health challenges. Different focus and training.

### Q: Is the training full-time?
**A:** Training schedule varies by session. Contact DBH for specific schedule details.

### Q: Can I use my certification in other states?
**A:** Possibly, depending on reciprocity agreements. Contact receiving state's certification body. May need additional requirements.

### Q: What if I only have one year of recovery?
**A:** DC requires two years minimum. Continue your recovery journey and apply when you meet the requirement.

### Q: Can I count my certification exam as continuing education?
**A:** No, initial certification exam doesn't count toward CE. CE is for recertification after you're certified.

### Q: Do I need to be working in peer support to get certified?
**A:** No, certification training is open to eligible DC residents meeting requirements, whether currently employed or not.
