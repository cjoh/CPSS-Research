Official Certification: Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist (MPSS)

Certification Overview

CalMHSA, in partnership with County Behavioral Health Plans, is the certifying entity for the state-approved Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Certification. This certification covers both mental health and substance use disorder recovery support. Peer Support Services became a Medi-Cal benefit effective July 1, 2022.

Certifying Body

California Mental Health Services Authority (CalMHSA)
Website: calmhsa.org/peer-certification
Certification Portal: capeercertification.org

Requirements

Basic Qualifications

  • At least 18 years of age
  • High school diploma or GED
  • Self-identify as having lived recovery experience (mental health, substance use, or family/caregiver)
  • Willing to share experience and dedicated to recovery
  • Agree to Code of Ethics

Training Requirements

80 Hours of Core Training

Must complete through CalMHSA-approved training provider covering 17 core competencies established by DHCS:

  • Hope, recovery, and wellness
  • Advocacy and psychiatric rehabilitation
  • Service delivery and addiction recovery principles
  • Professional ethics and boundaries
  • Trauma-informed care and cultural competency
  • Crisis intervention and documentation
  • Self-care and peer support principles
  • Mental health, substance use, and co-occurring disorders

State Examination

  • Duration: 2.5 hours
  • Format: Proctored examination
  • Questions: 120 multiple choice questions
  • Content: Covers all 17 core competency areas

Application Process

  1. Complete 80-hour training through CalMHSA-approved provider
  2. Submit application via capeercertification.org
  3. Upload training completion certificate (valid 2 years)
  4. Schedule and pass 2.5-hour state examination
  5. Receive CalMHSA-issued MPSS Certificate

Recertification

Every 2 years

  • Complete required continuing education hours
  • Submit renewal application through certification portal
  • Pay renewal fee
  • Maintain adherence to Code of Ethics

Medi-Cal Recognition & Employment

Medi-Cal Provider Status (Effective July 1, 2022)

DHCS recognizes Certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialists as a Medi-Cal provider type. Peer Support Services are a Medi-Cal benefit and services provided by certified MPSS are Medi-Cal billable.

Employment Settings:

  • County behavioral health departments
  • Community mental health clinics
  • Substance use disorder treatment facilities
  • Hospital-based programs
  • Community-based organizations
  • Private practice settings (with appropriate oversight)

Key Features

  • First state to recognize peer support specialists as distinct Medi-Cal provider type
  • Direct billing for peer support services
  • Covers both mental health AND substance use disorders
  • Training certificate valid 2 years from completion
  • Portable certification throughout California
