# California Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist (MPSS)** - for both mental health and substance use disorder recovery

## Certifying Body
**California Mental Health Services Authority (CalMHSA)**
- **Website**: https://www.calmhsa.org/peer-certification/
- **Certification Portal**: https://www.capeercertification.org/

**California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS)**
- Recognizes certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialists as a Medi-Cal provider type
- Peer Support Services became a Medi-Cal benefit effective July 1, 2022

## Certification Overview
California Mental Health Services Authority (CalMHSA), in partnership with County Behavioral Health Plans, is the certifying entity for the state-approved Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Certification (PSSC) program. This certification covers both mental health and substance use disorder recovery support.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
Must self-identify as having experience with the process of recovery from:
- Mental illness, OR
- Substance use disorder, OR
- As the parent, caregiver, or family member of a consumer of these services

### Basic Qualifications
- **Age**: At least 18 years of age
- **Education**: High school diploma or equivalent degree (GED)
- **Recovery Experience**: Self-identify as having lived recovery experience
- **Willingness**: Be willing to share experience and have strong dedication to recovery
- **Ethics**: Agree in writing to adhere to the Code of Ethics

## Training Requirements

### Core Training
**Total Hours: 80 hours**
- Must complete through a CalMHSA-approved training equity (entity)
- Training covers 17 core competencies established by DHCS
- Format varies by provider (in-person, virtual, or hybrid)

### 17 Core Competencies

Training must include concepts such as:
1. Hope, recovery, and wellness
2. The role of advocacy
3. Psychiatric rehabilitation skills
4. Service delivery
5. Addiction recovery principles
6. Defined practices
7. Professional ethics and boundaries
8. Trauma-informed care
9. Cultural competency
10. Crisis intervention
11. Documentation
12. Self-care and wellness
13. Peer support principles
14. Recovery-oriented systems
15. Mental health conditions
16. Substance use disorders
17. Co-occurring disorders

## Certification Examination

### State Examination Details
- **Duration**: 2.5 hours
- **Format**: Proctored examination
- **Questions**: 120 multiple choice questions
- **Content**: Covers all 17 core competency areas
- **Passing Score**: Set by DHCS standards
- **Administration**: Through CalMHSA-approved testing centers

## Application and Certification Process

### Step-by-Step Process

**1. Complete 80-Hour Training**
   - Select CalMHSA-approved training provider
   - Register and complete 80-hour training program
   - Receive training completion certificate (valid 2 years)

**2. Submit Initial Certification Application**
   - **Website**: https://www.capeercertification.org/
   - Create account on CalMHSA certification portal
   - Complete online application
   - Upload training completion certificate
   - Pay application fee

**3. Schedule and Pass State Examination**
   - Receive exam eligibility notification
   - Schedule 2.5-hour proctored exam
   - Pass exam covering 17 core competencies
   - Receive exam results

**4. Receive Certification**
   - CalMHSA issues Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist Certificate
   - Begin working as MPSS
   - Can bill Medi-Cal for services

## Renewal Requirements

### Recertification Period
**Every 2 years**

### Renewal Requirements
- Must renew certification every two years
- Continuing education requirements (contact CalMHSA for specific hours)
- Renewal fee (contact CalMHSA for amount)
- Maintain adherence to Code of Ethics
- Active practice or continuing professional development

### Renewal Process
1. Complete required continuing education
2. Submit renewal application through certification portal
3. Pay renewal fee
4. Maintain good standing

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become a CalMHSA-approved training provider:

### Step 1: Contact CalMHSA
- **Website**: https://www.calmhsa.org/peer-certification/
- **Certification Portal**: https://www.capeercertification.org/
- Request information about training provider approval process

### Step 2: Develop Curriculum
Must include:
- All 17 DHCS-established core competencies
- 80 hours of comprehensive training
- Assessment and evaluation tools
- Quality assurance measures
- Evidence-based practices

### Step 3: Submit Application
- Complete training provider application
- Submit curriculum for review
- Provide instructor qualifications
- Include organizational information
- Describe training delivery methods

### Step 4: Meet Quality Standards
- Qualified instructors with peer support expertise and lived experience
- Appropriate training facilities or virtual platform
- Valid assessment tools for 17 competencies
- Certificate issuance process
- Evaluation and feedback mechanisms

### Step 5: Receive Approval
- CalMHSA reviews and approves curriculum
- Organization added to approved training provider list
- Listed on capeercertification.org website
- Begin offering certified training
- Issue 80-hour training certificates valid for 2 years

## Notable Features of California Program

### Medi-Cal Provider Status
- First state to recognize peer support specialists as distinct Medi-Cal provider type
- Direct billing for peer support services
- Integrated into healthcare delivery system

### Statewide Standardization
- Single certification system for entire state
- CalMHSA provides centralized oversight
- Consistent standards across all counties
- Portable certification throughout California

### Training Certificate Validity
- 80-hour training certificates valid for 2 years from completion
- Allows time to complete certification process
- Flexibility for working professionals

### Comprehensive Scope
- Covers both mental health AND substance use disorders
- Includes family/caregiver lived experience
- Recognizes diverse pathways to recovery
