Certification Overview

Mississippi's Certified Peer Support Specialist Professional (CPSSP) credential is provided through the Mississippi Department of Mental Health. A person wanting to become a peer specialist must complete a 5-day training program and pass a written exam.

Mississippi Department of Mental Health (DMH)

Phone: 601-359-1288

Website: dmh.ms.gov/service-options/peer-support

Requirements

Education Requirements

  • Minimum of high school diploma, GED certificate, or be at least sixteen years of age and enrolled in a GED program or enrolled in school

Work Experience Requirements

  • 100 hours of paid or volunteer work experience or activities related to mental health, community or public service within the last three years

Employment Requirements

  • Employment in Mississippi's "state mental health system" is a mandatory requirement to apply for and hold a DMH professional credential
  • Provide verification of current employment with a program funded and/or certified or licensed by the Mississippi Department of Mental Health

Training Requirements

  • 5-day training program provided through DMH
  • Training designations: Adult and Recovery, Parent/Caregiver, Young Adult, Forensic Peer Recovery, Recovery only
  • Grounds participants in recovery concepts and builds skills in creating helpful peer relationships

Examination

  • Complete test with passing score of at least 75%

Application Process

  1. Secure Employment: Obtain employment with DMH-funded or certified/licensed program
  2. Complete Application: Submit training application before deadline
  3. Complete Training: Attend 5-day CPSSP training
  4. Pass Examination: Score at least 75% on written exam
  5. Receive Credentials: Obtain credentials from DMH Division of Professional Licensure and Certification (PLACE)

Training Designations

  • Adult and Recovery
  • Parent/Caregiver
  • Young Adult
  • Forensic Peer Recovery
  • Recovery only (for those previously trained in Adult designation prior to May 2021)
