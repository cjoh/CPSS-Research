Certification Overview

New Jersey offers Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) certification through the Addiction Professionals Certification Board. The credential requires 46 hours of intensive instruction and is designed for individuals with lived recovery experience.

Addiction Professionals Certification Board, Inc.

Website: certbd.org

NJ Peer Network: njpn.org/peer-recovery

Requirements

Education

  • High school diploma or GED
  • As of January 15, 2021: Alternative qualification available with supervisor verification

Training

  • 46 hours of CCAR or CARES approved training
  • Covers four domains: Advocacy, Mentoring/Education, Recovery/Wellness Support, Ethical Responsibility

Certification Options

  • CPRS from Certification Board of NJ (no exam required)
  • NCPRSS from NAADAC (requires passing exam)

Fees

Fee Type Cost
Initial Application $175
Recertification $175
Training (varies) ~$300

Renewal

  • Period: Every two years
  • CE Required: 20 hours

Training Providers

  • Mental Health Association in NJ: mhanj.org
  • Prevention is Key: preventioniskey.org
  • Brookdale Community College: brookdalecc.edu
