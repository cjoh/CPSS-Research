# Alaska Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Peer Support Professional (PSP)**

## Certifying Body
**Alaska Commission for Behavioral Health Certification (ACBHC)**
- Website: https://akcertification.org/
- Specific Page: https://akcertification.org/peer-support-professionals/

**Alaska Division of Behavioral Health (DBH)**
- Oversees peer support certification system
- Website: https://health.alaska.gov/en/services/alaska-peer-support-certification/

## Certification Overview
Peer Support Professionals in Alaska are individuals who have experienced recovery from mental health challenges or substance use disorders, or who have cared for someone in recovery. Working under supervision, they provide behavioral health support, help others stay engaged in their recovery, and reduce relapse risks.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
**Must have ONE of the following:**
- 1 year of recovery from a mental health condition, OR
- 1 year of recovery from a substance use disorder, OR
- 1 year of caring for a family member in recovery from mental health or substance use disorder

### Educational Requirements
- No specific educational requirement listed
- Ability to complete required training hours
- Strong communication and interpersonal skills

### Background Check
- Background check may be required
- Contact ACBHC for specific requirements

## Training Requirements

### Initial Certification Requirements
**Total: 50 Continuing Education (CE) Credit Hours**

Required components:
- **37.5 hours**: DBH-approved peer support course
- **3 hours**: Ethics training
- **3 hours**: Confidentiality training
- **6 hours**: Infectious Disease/HIV/AIDS education
- **0.5 hours**: Remaining balance in approved topics

### Core Training Topics
- Peer support principles and practices
- Recovery-oriented care
- Trauma-informed approaches
- Professional boundaries and ethics
- Confidentiality and HIPAA compliance
- Cultural competency
- Self-care and wellness
- Documentation and recordkeeping

## Application Process

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Meet Eligibility Requirements**
   - Verify 1 year of lived experience (recovery or caregiving)
   - Prepare to demonstrate lived experience

2. **Complete Required Training**
   - Register for Alaska Behavioral Health training
   - Complete 37.5-hour DBH-approved course
   - Complete 3 hours ethics training
   - Complete 3 hours confidentiality training
   - Complete 6 hours Infectious Disease/HIV/AIDS training

3. **Accumulate Total 50 CE Hours**
   - Track all training hours
   - Maintain certificates of completion

4. **Apply for Peer Support Professional Associate Credential**
   - Submit application to ACBHC
   - Provide documentation of all training hours
   - Include proof of lived experience (format to be confirmed with ACBHC)

5. **Background Check**
   - Complete background check as required by ACBHC
   - May include criminal background and child abuse registry checks

6. **Receive Credential**
   - ACBHC reviews application
   - Issues Peer Support Professional credential upon approval

## Recertification Requirements

### Recertification Period
**Every 2 years**

### Required Continuing Education
- **3 hours**: Ethics training
- **3 hours**: Confidentiality training
- Total of 6 hours per 2-year recertification cycle

### Recertification Process
1. Complete required continuing education hours
2. Submit recertification application to ACBHC
3. Pay recertification fee (contact ACBHC for amount)
4. Maintain documentation of CE hours

## Supervision Requirements
- Peer Support Professionals work under supervision
- Supervisor must be a qualified behavioral health professional or experienced certified peer
- Specific supervision hours and requirements to be confirmed with employer and ACBHC

## Employment and Reimbursement
- Peer support services are Medicaid billable in Alaska
- Employment opportunities in:
  - Behavioral health clinics
  - Community mental health centers
  - Substance use disorder treatment facilities
  - Tribal health organizations
  - Hospital-based programs
  - Private practice settings (under supervision)

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become an approved training provider in Alaska:

1. **Contact Alaska Division of Behavioral Health**
   - Oversees training approval process
   - Website: https://health.alaska.gov/en/services/alaska-peer-support-certification/

2. **Submit Training Curriculum for Approval**
   - Must meet DBH standards
   - Include required core competencies
   - Address ethics, confidentiality, and infectious disease topics

3. **Meet DBH Quality Standards**
   - Qualified instructors with peer support expertise
   - Evidence-based curriculum
   - Evaluation and assessment protocols

4. **Coordinate with ACBHC**
   - Ensure training meets certification requirements
   - Establish process for reporting completions

5. **Approval Process**
   - Submit formal application to DBH
   - Undergo curriculum review
   - Receive approval to deliver training
   - Training hours count toward ACBHC certification

## Additional Notes

### Unique Features of Alaska's Program
- Training provided at no cost through state funding
- Virtual training delivery accommodates Alaska's geographic challenges
- Strong emphasis on cultural competency for Alaska Native communities
- Integration of trauma-informed care throughout training

### Career Pathways
After certification, peer support professionals can:
- Work in various behavioral health settings
- Pursue additional certifications (e.g., substance abuse counselor)
- Advance to peer supervisor roles with experience
- Specialize in specific populations (youth, veterans, families)

### Professional Support
- Alaska Behavioral Health provides ongoing support
- Information sessions held regularly throughout the year
- Networking opportunities with other peer professionals
- Access to continuing education opportunities

## Important Considerations
- Classes fill quickly - apply early for summer training
- Virtual training requires reliable internet access
- Commit to full week of training (37.5 hours over 5 days)
- Maintain certification through continuing education every 2 years
- Work under supervision until experienced
