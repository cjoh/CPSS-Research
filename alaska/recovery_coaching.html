Alaska Recovery Coaching Legal Information

Research Date: January 5, 2025

Legal Status Overview

Status: UNREGULATED - No prohibition found

Independent Practice: YES - Can operate independently

Certification Required: No state requirement

Legal Definition

Status: No legal definition found

Alaska does not appear to have a statutory or regulatory definition of "recovery coaching" or "recovery coach" as a distinct profession.

Related Certification

Alaska Commission for Behavioral Health Certification (akcertification.org) offers certifications for behavioral health professionals, but specific recovery coach certification details were not found in available sources.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

No Alaska statute specifically addresses the boundary between recovery coaching and therapy.

General Principle

Alaska, like most states, regulates the practice of:

  • Psychology (AS 08.86)
  • Professional Counseling
  • Social Work
  • Marriage and Family Therapy

Prohibited Activities (Without License)

Anyone practicing without a license cannot:

  • Diagnose mental health or substance use disorders
  • Provide psychotherapy
  • Present themselves as a licensed mental health professional
  • Engage in activities within the scope of practice of licensed professions

Independent Practice Requirements

Can Recovery Coaches Operate Independently?

YES - UNREGULATED

Key Findings:

  • No licensing requirement for recovery coaches
  • No certification requirement to practice as recovery coach
  • No employment restrictions found
  • No specific regulations governing independent practice

What This Means

  • No state certification required to call yourself a recovery coach
  • Can operate independent practice/LLC
  • Must avoid activities requiring professional licensure
  • Should carry appropriate liability insurance

Possible Business Structures

  • Sole proprietor
  • LLC
  • Corporation
  • Independent contractor

Risk Considerations

Low Risk Activities

  • Sharing personal recovery story
  • Providing peer support and mentoring
  • Assisting with goal-setting
  • Connecting to community resources
  • Recovery accountability

High Risk Activities (Avoid)

  • Clinical assessment or diagnosis
  • Treatment planning
  • Therapy or counseling
  • Using protected titles (therapist, counselor, etc.)

Legal Citations

Primary Authority

Alaska Commission for Behavioral Health Certification (voluntary certifications)

  • Website: akcertification.org
  • Not a state licensing board

Statutes Reviewed

  • Alaska Statutes Title 8 - Business and Professions
  • No specific recovery coach statute found

Regulatory Bodies

  • Alaska Commission for Behavioral Health Certification (private, voluntary)
  • No state recovery coach licensing board exists

Practical Guidance

No Barriers to Entry

Alaska does not require licensing or certification to practice as a recovery coach

Professional Boundaries Critical

Must carefully avoid:

  • Diagnosing disorders
  • Providing therapy or clinical treatment
  • Marketing as licensed professional
  • Using titles that imply licensure

Voluntary Certification

May choose to pursue certification through Alaska Commission for Behavioral Health Certification or national organizations (CCAR, IC&RC)

Insurance Recommended

Professional liability insurance strongly recommended even though not required
