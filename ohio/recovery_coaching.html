Legal Status Overview

Independent Practice Not Permitted

State-Defined Term: Certified Peer Recovery Supporter (CPRS)

Regulatory Status: Defined in administrative code - certification required

Independent Practice: NO - Must work within certified behavioral health organizations under supervision

Legal Definition

Ohio Administrative Code Rule 5122-29-15.1 defines "Certified peer recovery supporter" (CPRS) as:

"An individual, with a direct lived experience, who has self-identified as being in recovery from a mental health or substance use disorder and has been certified pursuant to Ohio Administrative Code Rule 5122-29-15.1."

Age Requirement: Must be at least 18 years of age at time of certification.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

CPRS Scope of Practice

  • Provide peer recovery support services based on lived experience
  • Limited to FOUR HOURS PER DAY per recipient
  • Work within certified behavioral health organizations
  • Distinct from Licensed Independent Behavioral Health Practitioners

Service Settings

Peer recovery support can be provided:

  • Through specialized recovery services program
  • As component of assertive community treatment
  • As component of substance use disorder residential treatment
  • As substance use disorder outpatient treatment service
  • As component of intensive home-based treatment service
  • As component of mobile response and stabilization service

Licensed Independent Behavioral Health Practitioners (I-BHPs)

  • Licensed by professional boards in Ohio
  • Authorized to practice independently WITHOUT professional supervision
  • CPRS do NOT have independent practice authority comparable to I-BHPs

Independent Practice Requirements

CRITICAL FINDING: NO INDEPENDENT PRACTICE PERMITTED

Recovery coaches in Ohio generally do NOT practice independently. They must work within certified behavioral health organizations under supervision structures.

Certification Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years of age
  • Self-identify as being in recovery from mental health or substance use disorder
  • Complete required training and certification pursuant to Rule 5122-29-15.1
  • Pass IC&RC Peer Recovery Examination

Supervision Requirements

  • When providing Medicaid-covered services, CPRS typically require supervision
  • Must work through certified behavioral health organizations
  • This differs fundamentally from Licensed Independent Behavioral Health Practitioners who ARE authorized to practice independently without professional supervision

Service Limitations

  • Peer recovery support limited to FOUR HOURS PER DAY per recipient
  • Must work within certified behavioral health organizations

2024 Update

Peer Support Services became available for BOTH mental health (MH) and substance use disorder (SUD) beginning September 1, 2024. This expanded the scope but did not change independent practice restrictions.

Scope of Practice

What CPRS CANNOT Do

  • Cannot establish independent private practice as CPRS
  • Cannot practice independently without organizational affiliation
  • Cannot provide services beyond 4 hours per day per client
  • Cannot work outside certified behavioral health organizational structures

What CPRS MUST Do

  • Must be employed by or contracted through certified behavioral health organization
  • Must work under supervision structure
  • Must operate within specific service settings listed in administrative code
  • Must adhere to 4-hour daily limit per recipient

Important Considerations

Practical Implications

Recovery coaches in Ohio seeking to provide services should:

  • Obtain CPRS certification through proper channels
  • Affiliate with certified behavioral health organization
  • Understand supervision requirements
  • Recognize scope limitations (4-hour daily limit)
  • Distinguish their role from independently licensed practitioners
