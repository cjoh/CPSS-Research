# Missouri Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Defined in regulations (9 CSR 30-3.310)

**Recovery support programs** offer services including recovery coaching, defined as support to develop proactive recovery-oriented problem solving skills.

**Three credential types:**
1. Certified Peer Specialist (CPS) - 5-day training
2. Certified Reciprocal Peer Recovery (CRPR) - IC&RC, 500 hours + exam
3. Missouri Recovery Support Specialist (MRSS) - 3-day training, 1000 hours

**Administered by:** Missouri Credentialing Board (sole source recognized by DBH)

**Citation:** 9 CSR 30-3.310; sections 630.050, 630.055 RSMo

## Coaching vs. Therapy
Recovery coaching is non-clinical support, not substitute for treatment. Provided before, after, or concurrently with treatment programs.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Must work through recovery support programs

**Service Delivery Requirements:**
> "Recovery coaching shall be offered before, after, or concurrently with any department-funded certified substance use disorder treatment program."

> "Recovery coaching shall be provided by a Missouri Recovery Support Specialist or Missouri Recovery Support Specialist - Peer."

**Requirements vary by credential:**
- **CPS:** $75 application, 5-day training, exam
- **CRPR:** $200 application, 500 hours (25 supervised), 46 training hours, IC&RC exam
- **MRSS:** $75 application, 1000 hours (last 10 years), 3-day training

**Renewal:** Every 2 years, 20 CE hours (6 hours ethics), $70 renewal fee

**Supervision:** Family Support Providers supervised by LMHP, QAP, or trained supervisor.

**Citation:** Missouri Credentialing Board standards; 9 CSR 30-3.310

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** YES - in regulations
