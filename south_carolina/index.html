Certification Overview

South Carolina offers Certified Peer Support Specialist (CPSS) certification through the Addiction Professionals of South Carolina and SC SHARE. The credential is designed for individuals with lived recovery experience who support others in their recovery journey.

Addiction Professionals of South Carolina

Website: addictionprofessionalsofsouthcarolina.org

SC SHARE: scshare.com/certification

Requirements

Education Requirements

  • High school diploma or GED (or college degrees may be substituted)
  • As of January 15, 2021: Those without minimum HSD or GED may qualify with supervisor verification of two years in recovery field

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • One year of continuous, sustained lived recovery
  • Practical experience verified by employers or volunteer organizations

Training Requirements

  • 46 hours of CCAR or CARES approved training
  • 40 hours of intensive classroom training online plus homework
  • Topics: Recovery Principles, Peer Support Practices, Ethics, Cultural Humility, Communication, Recovery & Personal Wellness Goals, All Pathways Recovery, Managing Crisis, Group Facilitation, Advocacy, Boundaries, Harm Reduction, and Workplace Skills

Work Experience Requirements

  • 100 volunteer and/or paid hours as a Peer Support Specialist in four domains:
    • Advocacy
    • Mentoring/Education
    • Recovery Support
    • Ethical Responsibility

Application Process

  1. Complete Training: Complete 46-hour approved CPSS training
  2. Submit Application Materials: Copy of high school diploma/GED, signed acknowledgements, three professional reference forms in sealed envelopes
  3. Gain Work Experience: Complete 100 volunteer or paid hours in four domains
  4. Receive Certification: CPSS certification from Addiction Professionals of South Carolina (no exam required)

Fees and Costs

Fee Type Cost
CPSS Certification Fee (NAADAC/APSC members) $100
CPSS Certification Fee (non-members) $150
Training Cost (varies by provider) ~$300 (scholarships available)

Renewal Requirements

  • Renewal Period: Every two years
  • Continuing Education: 20 hours every two years
