Related Professions: Licensed Addiction Counselors are regulated under SC Code § 40-75-225, but recovery coaches operate separately from this framework.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

Licensed Professional Framework

South Carolina extensively regulates licensed professionals including:

  • Addiction Counselors (SC Code Title 40, Chapter 75) - require master's degree, licensure
  • Professional Counselors
  • Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Social Workers

Recovery Coach Status

  • NO SPECIFIC STATUTORY SCOPE OF PRACTICE FOUND
  • Appear to operate separately from statutory licensing framework
  • NO explicit boundary definition found

Key Risk

Without statutory definition, recovery coaches must be extremely careful not to engage in activities that constitute practice of licensed addiction counseling or professional counseling.

Independent Practice Requirements

Regulatory Status: UNREGULATED

  • NO STATE-SPECIFIC LICENSING, CERTIFICATION, OR REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS FOUND
  • Recovery coaching appears unregulated at state statutory level
  • Voluntary certification programs may exist but are not state-mandated
  • No prohibition on independent practice identified

Scope of Practice

What Recovery Coaches Should DO

  • Provide peer support, mentoring, and recovery navigation
  • Share lived experience
  • Connect clients to appropriate resources
  • Refer to licensed professionals for clinical needs

What Recovery Coaches Must NOT Do

  • Diagnose substance use disorders or mental health conditions
  • Provide clinical treatment or counseling
  • Develop clinical treatment plans
  • Present yourself as a licensed addiction counselor
  • Engage in activities requiring licensure under SC Code § 40-75-225

Important Considerations

Recommendations

Even though unregulated, recovery coaches should:

  • Obtain voluntary national certification (CARC, NCPRSS, etc.) for credibility
  • Complete comprehensive training in trauma-informed care, ethics, boundaries
  • Obtain professional liability insurance
  • Develop clear service agreements defining non-clinical nature of services
  • Establish referral relationships with licensed professionals
  • Form LLC or other business entity for liability protection
  • Monitor legal developments - SC may develop regulations in future
