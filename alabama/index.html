Official Certification: Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS)

Certification Overview

A Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS) is a person living in recovery with a substance use disorder who has been trained and certified by the Alabama Department of Mental Health to use their personal recovery story to assist others in developing their personal plan for recovery.

Requirements

Personal Recovery Requirements

  • Minimum Recovery Time: 2 years of continuous demonstrated recovery time from a substance use disorder
  • Must be willing to self-identify as a person in recovery for the benefit of others
  • Must be willing to use lived recovery experience to support others

Educational Requirements

  • High school diploma/GED (recommended)
  • Strong reading, comprehension, and written communication skills

Application Requirements

  • Completed application form
  • Two written references that attest to your appropriateness for certification and verify length of recovery

Training Requirements

Total Hours: 40 hours

Format: Five-day online training program

Topics Include: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy techniques, Motivational Interviewing, various pathways to recovery, peer recovery support best practices, professional ethics and boundaries, Trauma-Informed Care, and recovery principles

Application Process

1

Contact COSA-NCADD

Reach out to Pat Brown at 334-262-7477 or pbrown@cosancadd.org to request information about upcoming training dates

2

Complete Application

Download and submit application from mh.alabama.gov with two written references verifying recovery

3

Complete Training

Attend all five days of 40-hour online training with instruction, discussion, and role-play

4

Pass Examination

Complete written exam at end of training and receive certification upon passing

Download Application Visit COSA-NCADD

Renewal Requirements

Contact ADMH Office of Peer Programs for renewal requirements and continuing education specifications. Attend the annual Alabama CRSS Peer Conference for continuing education opportunities.

Employment & Reimbursement

CRSS services may be Medicaid billable in Alabama. Employment opportunities available in treatment centers, recovery support programs, community mental health centers, hospital-based programs, and community-based organizations.
