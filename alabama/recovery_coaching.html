Legal Status Overview

Status: UNCLEAR - Not explicitly addressed

State-Defined Term: Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS)

Independent Practice: Not explicitly prohibited, but CRSS certification tied to agency employment

Legal Definition

Alabama uses the term "Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS)" rather than "recovery coach." A CRSS is defined as:

"A person living in recovery with a substance use disorder working within an agency or organization who has been trained and certified by the Alabama Department of Mental Health (ADMH) to use their personal recovery story to assist others in developing their personal plan for recovery."

Important Note

This is administratively defined, not in state statute. Recovery coaching as an independent activity is not legally defined in Alabama.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

No specific Alabama statute distinguishes between coaching and therapy for unlicensed practitioners.

Prohibited Activities

Recovery coaches who engage in the following would likely be practicing without a license:

  • Psychological assessment or diagnosis
  • Treatment planning requiring clinical judgment
  • Therapy or counseling services
  • Presenting themselves as licensed professionals

Permitted Activities (CRSS Scope)

Certified Recovery Support Specialists provide:

  • Role modeling showing recovery is possible
  • Coping skills and positive attitude skills
  • Facilitation of peer support/self-help groups
  • Connecting individuals to resources
  • Sharing lived experience

Independent Practice Requirements

Regulatory Status

Can recovery coaches operate independently? UNCLEAR - Not explicitly addressed

The CRSS certification is designed for individuals working "within an agency or organization." However, there is no explicit prohibition on independent practice for uncertified recovery coaches.

Key Factors

  • CRSS Certification: Designed for employment in agencies, not independent practice
  • Funding Tied to Employment: ADMH-funded positions require CRSS certification
  • No Licensing Law: Alabama does not license recovery coaches, suggesting independent practice is not prohibited if not engaging in regulated activities

CRSS Certification Requirements (if working as CRSS)

  • Minimum 2 years of continuous demonstrated recovery
  • Willing to self-identify as person in recovery
  • High school diploma or GED
  • Valid driver's license
  • Two written references
  • 40 hours ADMH-approved CRSS training
  • Pass exam with minimum 70% score
  • 16 CEUs annually after first year

Practical Guidance

Working as CRSS

Must be employed by organization, complete 40-hour training, maintain certification

Working as Independent Recovery Coach

Appears permissible if not using CRSS title and not engaging in regulated professional activities

Risk Management

Should avoid any activities that could be construed as therapy, counseling, or clinical assessment
