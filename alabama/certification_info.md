## Official Designation
Council on Substance Abuse - NCADD (COSA-NCADD) is the official provider of certified recovery support specialist training under the Office of Peer Programs.

## Certification Overview
A Certified Recovery Support Specialist (CRSS) is a person living in recovery with a substance use disorder who has been trained and certified by the Alabama Department of Mental Health to use their personal recovery story to assist others in developing their personal plan for recovery.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements
- **Minimum Recovery Time**: 2 years of continuous demonstrated recovery time from a substance use disorder at the date of application
- Must be willing to self-identify as a person in recovery for the benefit of others
- Must be willing to use lived recovery experience to support others

### Application Requirements
- Completed application form
- Two written references that:
  - Attest to your appropriateness for certification
  - Comment on your recovery
  - Verify the length of recovery
  - Document any pertinent peer recovery support activities

### Educational Requirements
- High school diploma/GED (recommended)
- Strong reading, comprehension, and written communication skills

## Training Requirements

### Core Training
- **Total Hours**: 40 hours
- **Format**: Five-day online training program
- **Training Components**:
  - Formal instruction
  - Interactive discussions
  - Role-play exercises
  - Examination at completion

### Curriculum Topics
The training covers proven skills in:
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy techniques
- Motivational Interviewing
- Various pathways to recovery
- Peer recovery support best practices
- Professional ethics and boundaries
- Trauma-Informed Care
- Recovery principles and practices

### Examination
- Written examination administered after training completion
- Must pass examination to receive certification

## Application Process

1. **Contact COSA-NCADD**
   - Reach out to Pat Brown at 334-262-7477 or pbrown@cosancadd.org
   - Request information about upcoming training dates

2. **Complete Training Application**
   - Download from: https://mh.alabama.gov/al-certified-recovery-support-specialist-training-application/
   - Submit completed application to COSA-NCADD

3. **Gather Required Documentation**
   - Two written references verifying recovery and appropriateness
   - Proof of 2+ years continuous recovery

4. **Complete 40-Hour Training**
   - Attend all five days of online training
   - Participate in instruction, discussion, and role-play

5. **Pass Certification Examination**
   - Complete written exam at end of training
   - Receive certification upon passing

6. **Receive Certification**
   - Certification issued by Alabama Department of Mental Health
   - Begin working as a CRSS

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become an approved training provider for CRSS in Alabama:

1. **Contact Alabama Department of Mental Health**
   - Office of Peer Programs
   - Phone: 1-800-832-0952 or 334-242-3456
   - Website: https://mh.alabama.gov/peer-programs/

2. **Review Guidelines and Requirements**
   - Request "Guidelines and Requirements of the Alabama Certified Peer Specialists Training Program"
   - Available at: https://mh.alabama.gov/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/Guidelines_and_Requirements_of_the_AL_CertifiedPeerSpecialistsTrainingProgram.pdf

3. **Submit Proposal**
   - Currently, COSA-NCADD is the sole designated training provider
   - Organizations interested in providing training should contact ADMH directly to inquire about the designation process

## Related Certifications

Alabama also offers mental health peer specialist certifications:
- **Certified Mental Health Adult Peer Specialist (CMHAPS)** - for individuals in recovery from mental illness
- **Certified Mental Health Youth Peer Specialist (CMHYPS)** - for youth with mental health experience
- **Mental Health Parent Peer Support Specialist (MHPPS)** - for parents of children with mental health challenges

For information on these certifications, contact the ADMH Office of Peer Programs.

## Notes
- Alabama has a single, centralized training provider system through COSA-NCADD
- The state maintains strong oversight through ADMH
- CRSS certification is specific to substance use disorder recovery
- Co-occurring disorder specialists may need both CRSS and mental health peer specialist credentials
