Overview

The Kentucky Peer Specialist (KPS) credential is issued by the Kentucky Department for Behavioral Health, Developmental and Intellectual Disabilities (DBHDID). This certification enables individuals with lived experience to provide peer support services to those with mental health, substance use, or co-occurring disorders.

Basic Requirements

  • Age: Must be 18 years or older
  • Education: High school diploma or GED certificate required
  • Lived Experience: Current or past diagnosis of mental health, substance use, or co-occurring disorders
  • Recovery Status: Must be in recovery when applying
  • Duration: No specific duration of abstinence or sobriety required

Training Requirements

Duration: 30 hours of training

Format: Varies by provider (in-person, virtual, or hybrid)

Curriculum: DBHDID-approved curriculum covering peer support competencies

Testing: Both written and oral test required

Passing Score: Aggregate score of at least 70% required

Examination Requirements

Candidates must pass both:

  • Written Test: Minimum 70% aggregate score
  • Oral Test: Competency-based assessment

Upon passing both tests, candidates will be certified as Kentucky Adult Peer Specialists.

Certification Maintenance

Validity: Certification is valid for one (1) year only

Renewal: Must renew annually

Annual CE Requirement: Minimum of 6 hours of relevant continuing education every 12 months

Key Advantages

  • Short Training: 30 hours compared to many states requiring 40-75 hours
  • Affordable Options: Training available from $65-$350
  • No Sobriety Duration: Focus on current recovery status
  • Dual Assessment: Written and oral exams ensure competency
  • Multiple Providers: Flexibility in training options and schedules
  • Virtual Options: Accessible from anywhere in Kentucky

Important Considerations

  • Annual Renewal: Plan for yearly renewal requirements
  • 6 Hours CE: Relatively minimal but must be completed annually
  • Training Costs: Research all options - range from $65-$350
  • Dual Exams: Prepare for both written and oral assessments
