Certification Overview

Vermont's training and certification exam for peer support specialists are currently in final development, with the Department of Mental Health offering interim credentialing until the full certification process is implemented in fall 2025.

Important Deadline

Once the certification process is established, individuals will have until July 1, 2026 to obtain certification to remain an allowable Medicaid biller for peer support services.

Vermont Department of Mental Health

Email: AHS.DMHPeerCredentialing@vermont.gov

Current Status (2025)

  • Interim Credentialing: Currently available for peer support providers working in Designated Agencies or Specialized Service Agencies
  • Full Certification: Expected to be implemented in fall 2025
  • Compliance Deadline: July 1, 2026 to obtain certification for Medicaid billing

Interim Credentialing

Interim credentialing permits individuals working as peer support providers in Designated Agencies or Specialized Service Agencies to bill Medicaid for peer support services until the full certification process is implemented.

How to Apply for Interim Credentialing

Contact the Vermont Department of Mental Health at AHS.DMHPeerCredentialing@vermont.gov for interim credentialing application and requirements.

Program Development

The Vermont Department of Mental Health has partnered with the Peer Workforce Development Initiative (PWDI) to develop an implementation plan for mental health peer support worker credentialing. The program is designed to create a comprehensive certification system that meets national standards while serving Vermont's unique needs.

How to Prepare

  1. Apply for Interim Credentialing: If currently working as a peer support provider
  2. Register for Training: Enroll in upcoming 2025 training sessions
  3. Stay Informed: Monitor Vermont Department of Mental Health website for certification process updates
  4. Plan Ahead: Prepare to complete full certification by July 1, 2026 deadline
