Two-Step Certification Process

Step 1: IC&RC Certified Recovery Coach

Obtain certification from International Certification & Reciprocity Consortium (IC&RC) as a Certified Recovery Coach.

IC&RC Requirements:

  • Personal recovery experience
  • Complete IC&RC-approved training program
  • Pass IC&RC certification examination
  • Meet continuing education requirements

Step 2: Vermont PRSS Registration

Register with Vermont Office of Professional Regulation (OPR) as Peer Recovery Support Specialist.

Vermont OPR Requirements:

  • Hold valid IC&RC Certified Recovery Coach credential
  • Complete Vermont-specific application
  • Demonstrate employment with certified RSO
  • Maintain registration through OPR

Recovery Services Organization (RSO) Requirement

MUST Work for Certified RSO

Critical Restriction: Vermont law requires peer recovery support specialists to work for organizations certified by the Vermont Department of Health as Recovery Services Organizations.

What This Means:

  • You CANNOT operate an independent recovery coaching practice
  • You MUST be employed by or contracted with a certified RSO
  • The RSO must hold current certification from VT Department of Health
  • RSO certification is required for state funding beginning State Fiscal Year 2027

What is a Recovery Services Organization (RSO)?

An RSO is an organization certified by the Vermont Department of Health to provide recovery support services. Certification requirements include:

  • Meeting VT Department of Health standards for recovery services
  • Employing appropriately credentialed staff (PRSS)
  • Adhering to quality and ethical standards
  • Maintaining compliance with state regulations

Important: Beginning State Fiscal Year 2027, RSO certification is required for organizations to receive state funding for recovery support services.

Scope of Practice

Permitted Activities (Within RSO Employment)

Peer Recovery Support Specialists working for certified RSOs may provide:

  • Peer support based on lived experience of recovery
  • Recovery coaching and mentoring
  • Advocacy for individuals in recovery
  • Connection to recovery resources and community supports
  • Facilitation of peer support groups
  • Recovery education and skill development
  • Support for recovery goal-setting and planning
  • Assistance navigating systems and services

Prohibited Activities

  • Independent Practice: Cannot operate private recovery coaching practice
  • Non-RSO Employment: Cannot provide PRSS services outside of certified RSO
  • Clinical Services: Cannot provide clinical diagnosis or treatment
  • Therapy: Cannot engage in psychotherapy or clinical counseling
  • Medical Advice: Cannot provide medical or psychiatric advice
  • Treatment Planning: Cannot create clinical treatment plans

Employment Requirements

To Practice as PRSS in Vermont

  1. Obtain IC&RC Certification: Complete IC&RC Certified Recovery Coach certification
  2. Secure RSO Employment: Obtain employment or contract position with VT Department of Health certified RSO
  3. Apply for Vermont PRSS: Submit application to Vermont Office of Professional Regulation
  4. Maintain Certifications: Keep both IC&RC and Vermont OPR credentials current
  5. Ensure RSO Compliance: Verify employer maintains current RSO certification

RSO Certification Timeline

State Fiscal Year 2027 Requirement: Organizations must obtain RSO certification to receive state funding for recovery support services.

If you work for an organization receiving state funding, ensure they are pursuing or have obtained RSO certification.

Practical Guidance

Steps to Become PRSS in Vermont

  1. Research RSOs: Identify certified RSOs in Vermont that hire PRSSs
  2. IC&RC Training: Complete IC&RC Certified Recovery Coach training program
  3. Pass IC&RC Exam: Successfully complete IC&RC certification examination
  4. Apply to RSOs: Seek employment with certified Recovery Services Organization
  5. Vermont Registration: Once employed, apply for PRSS registration through OPR
  6. Maintain Credentials: Keep both IC&RC and OPR credentials current
  7. Verify RSO Status: Ensure employer maintains RSO certification

Important Reminders

  • Independent recovery coaching practice is NOT legal in Vermont
  • You must work for a certified RSO to practice as PRSS
  • Both IC&RC and Vermont OPR credentials are required
  • RSO certification is becoming mandatory for state-funded services (SFY27)
  • Working outside RSO employment may constitute unauthorized practice
