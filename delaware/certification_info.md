# Delaware Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS)**

## Certifying Body
**Delaware Certification Board**
- Website: https://www.decertboard.org/
- CPRS Page: https://www.decertboard.org/certified-peer-recovery-specialist-cprs

**Delaware Division of Substance Abuse and Mental Health (DSAMH)**
- Oversees peer services statewide
- Website: https://dhss.delaware.gov/dhss/dsamh/peerservices.html

## Certification Overview
The Certified Peer Recovery Specialist (CPRS) credential is for individuals with personal, lived experience in their own substance use disorder and/or mental health disorder recovery who wish to help others on their recovery journey.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Experience Requirements
- Direct personal experience living with mental illness and/or addiction and recovery
- Must be willing to self-disclose lived experience
- Purpose: Education, role modeling, and providing hope about the reality of recovery

### Educational Requirements
**Training:** 46 hours of education/training specific to peer recovery specialist domains

**Domain Hour Requirements:**
- **Advocacy**: 10 hours
- **Mentoring/Education**: 10 hours
- **Recovery/Wellness Support**: 10 hours
- **Ethical Responsibility**: 16 hours

### Work Experience Requirements
- **Total Hours**: 500 hours
- **Type**: Work or volunteer experience as a peer recovery specialist
- **Documentation**: Must be properly documented and verified

### Written Requirements
- **Statement of Lived Experience**: 1,000-word essay describing:
  - Experience with recovery
  - History of sustained recovery
  - How experience informs peer support work

## Training Requirements

### Total Required Training: 46 Hours

**Breakdown by Domain:**
1. **Advocacy** (10 hours)
   - Rights and responsibilities
   - Self-advocacy and systems advocacy
   - Empowerment principles

2. **Mentoring/Education** (10 hours)
   - Peer mentoring techniques
   - Educational approaches
   - Sharing lived experience effectively

3. **Recovery/Wellness Support** (10 hours)
   - Recovery-oriented principles
   - Wellness planning
   - Relapse prevention support

4. **Ethical Responsibility** (16 hours)
   - Professional boundaries
   - Confidentiality
   - Code of ethics
   - Professional conduct

## Application and Certification Process

### Step 1: Meet Eligibility Requirements
- Verify lived experience with recovery
- Ensure 18+ years of age
- Have High School Diploma or GED
- Prepare 1,000-word statement of lived experience

### Step 2: Apply for CPRS Training
- Complete training application
- Submit to Mental Health Association in Delaware
- Await acceptance notification

### Step 3: Complete Nine-Day Training (46 hours)
- Attend all training sessions
- Complete all domain requirements:
  - Advocacy (10 hours)
  - Mentoring/Education (10 hours)
  - Recovery/Wellness Support (10 hours)
  - Ethical Responsibility (16 hours)
- Participate fully in all activities
- Pass training assessments

### Step 4: Accumulate 500 Work/Volunteer Hours
- Gain peer recovery specialist experience
- Document all hours appropriately
- Work under appropriate supervision
- Apply learned skills in real-world settings

### Step 5: Prepare Written Materials
- Write 1,000-word statement of lived experience
- Describe recovery journey
- Explain sustained recovery history
- Connect experience to peer support role

### Step 6: Submit Certification Application
- Apply to Delaware Certification Board
- Submit training completion certificate
- Provide documentation of 500 hours
- Include 1,000-word statement
- Pay application fee

### Step 7: Receive CPRS Certification
- Delaware Certification Board reviews application
- Issues CPRS credential upon approval
- Begin working as Certified Peer Recovery Specialist
