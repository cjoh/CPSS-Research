# Recovery Coaching Legal Status - State Summary
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Quick Reference Guide

### States Allowing Independent Practice

**UNREGULATED:**
- **Alaska** - No legal definition, unregulated
- **Arizona** - Unregulated  
- **California** - Independent practice with restrictions (practice protection state)

**CERTIFICATION AVAILABLE - Independent Possible:**
- Arkansas, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Georgia
- Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Kansas, Louisiana
- Maine, Maryland (exempt from licensing), Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri

### States Requiring Supervision

**CANNOT PRACTICE INDEPENDENTLY:**
- **Alabama** - CRSS tied to agency employment (unclear)
- **Florida** - MUST work at licensed behavioral health organization
- **Hawaii** - MUST work under mental health professional supervision
- **Iowa** - MUST work within licensed agency with IDPH contract
- **Kentucky** - MUST work under approved behavioral health practitioner
- **Mississippi** - Employment in state mental health system MANDATORY
- **Montana** - LICENSURE REQUIRED, cannot practice without license

**NEW LICENSURE:**
- **Massachusetts** - First state licensure, effective March 23, 2025

---

## State-by-State Summary

### Most Restrictive (Supervision Required)

1. **Mississippi** - MUST be employed in state mental health system
2. **Montana** - Licensure required by law
3. **Iowa** - Must work within licensed agency with IDPH contract  
4. **Florida** - Must work at licensed behavioral health organization
5. **Kentucky** - Must work under approved behavioral health practitioner
6. **Hawaii** - Must work under mental health professional supervision

### Least Restrictive

1. **Alaska** - Completely unregulated
2. **Arizona** - Unregulated
3. **Maryland** - Exempt from licensing requirements (COMAR 10.63)

### Special Cases

**California** - Practice protection (not title protection) - activity determines regulation

**Colorado** - Medicaid credentialing required by July 1, 2026

**Massachusetts** - First state with recovery coach licensure (March 2025)

**Maryland** - Explicitly exempt from licensing requirements

**Georgia** - Certain credentials grant legal scope of practice
