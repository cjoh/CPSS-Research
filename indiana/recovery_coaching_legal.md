# Indiana Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** Defined through certification programs

**Two credentials:**
1. Certified Peer Recovery Coach (CPRC) - ICAADA
2. Certified Peer Support Professional (CPSP) - State credential

**CPRC:** Experienced-level credential for those with personal lived experience in recovery.

**Administered by:** ICAADA (CPRC); Indiana Division of Mental Health and Addiction (CPSP)

## Coaching vs. Therapy
Peer support based on lived experience, distinct from clinical treatment.

**Medicaid Recognition:** CPSP peer support services reimbursed through Medicaid in Indiana.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** Certification required for certain employment

**CPRC Requirements (ICAADA):**
- High school diploma/equivalency
- Personal lived experience in recovery
- Live or work in Indiana 51% of time
- MHAI SWD or CCAR or DMHA CHW/CRS training (before July 1, 2024)
- 16 hours recovery support ethics education
- Renewal every 2 years (40 CE hours, including 6 ethics)

**Recognition:** Promoted Industry Certification by IN Division of Mental Health and Addiction and IN Dept of Workforce Development.

**Citation:** ICAADA standards; Indiana DMHA recognition

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** Through certification programs
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Peer support, not clinical
- **Independent practice?** State recognition; Medicaid reimbursement available
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Key Points
- Two credential pathways (CPRC, CPSP)
- State and workforce development recognition
- Medicaid reimbursement for CPSP
- Must live/work in Indiana 51% of time

**Contact:** ICAADA: icaada.org; Indiana DMHA: in.gov/fssa/dmha
