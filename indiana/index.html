Official Certification: CPSP & CPRC (Free Training & Certification)

Training & Certification at NO COST

Certification Overview

Indiana is covering training and certification costs for peer support professionals in an effort to build the peer support workforce. Two main certification pathways available.

Certifying Bodies

DMHA (Division of Mental Health and Addiction)
Oversees Certified Peer Support Professional (CPSP)
Website: Indiana Peer Support

ICAADA (Indiana Counselor's Association on Alcohol and Drug Abuse)
Administers Certified Peer Recovery Coach (CPRC)
Website: ICAADA Credentials

Certification Options

1. Certified Peer Support Professional (CPSP)

For individuals living in recovery with a mental health and/or substance use condition

Requirements:

  • 18 years or older
  • High school diploma or equivalency
  • Lived experience receiving mental health or substance use treatment
  • At least one year in recovery

NO COST DMHA is absorbing fees for training and initial exam

2. Certified Peer Recovery Coach (CPRC)

For individuals with personal, lived experience of recovery from substance use, mental health, or co-occurring disorders

Reimbursement: CPRC-A and CPRC are reimbursable through Indiana Medicaid and Indiana Problem Gambler Awareness Program

Recognition: Recognized by Indiana Division of Mental Health and Addiction

Recent Developments (2025)

New Curriculum: Indiana introduced a new curriculum earlier this year, aligned with national standards

Cost Coverage: State is covering training and certification costs to build peer support workforce

Training Providers

  • Mental Health America of Indiana: Training Institute
  • ICAADA: Credential Programs
  • Indiana Association of Peer Recovery Support Services: Peer Recovery Credentialing

Application Process

  1. Meet eligibility requirements (18+, HS diploma, lived experience, 1 year recovery)
  2. Complete approved training program
  3. Apply for CPSP (through DMHA) or CPRC (through ICAADA)
  4. Pass certification exam at no cost
  5. Receive certification and begin practice
CPSP Certification CPRC Info

Employment & Reimbursement

Medicaid Billing: CPRC-A and CPRC credentials are reimbursable through Indiana Medicaid

Employment Settings: Community mental health centers, substance use treatment facilities, recovery community organizations, hospital-based programs, and integrated behavioral health services

Key Benefits

  • Training and initial exam at NO COST through state funding
  • New curriculum aligned with national standards
  • Medicaid reimbursement available for CPRC credentials
  • State recognition and support for peer workforce
  • Multiple certification pathways available
