# Iowa Recovery Coaching Legal Requirements
**Research Date:** January 5, 2025

## Legal Definition
**Status:** LEGALLY DEFINED in administrative rules

**Definition:** "Recovery coach" means an individual who has a mental health disorder, substance use disorder, or co-occurring disorder, or has experience with addiction or mental illness in their family or close friendships, who provides nonclinical, peer-based activities that engage, educate, and support individuals with co-occurring disorders, using the coach's own personal, lived experiences of recovery.

**Citation:** Iowa Department of Public Health administrative rules

## Coaching vs. Therapy
**Clear distinction:** Recovery coaches are "not a sponsor or a therapist but rather a role model, mentor, advocate, and motivator."

Provide nonclinical, peer-based activities. Not clinical treatment.

## Independent Practice Requirements
**Status:** CANNOT PRACTICE INDEPENDENTLY

**CRITICAL RESTRICTION:**
> "This credential is for those individuals who are themselves in recovery and who provide mental health or substance abuse peer support services within a licensed mental health or substance abuse agency/organization."

**Requirements for Practice:**
1. Must work within licensed agency/organization
2. Supervisory organization must have contract with Iowa Department of Public Health (IDPH)
3. Must be covered under supervisory organization's liability insurance
4. Cannot practice independently or operate own LLC

**Certification Requirements:**
- Age 18+
- High school diploma/GED
- Lived experience with substance use disorder
- Minimum 12 months recovery
- Willing to share experiences
- 500+ hours documented coaching under approved supervision
- 46 hours training (10 Advocacy, 10 Mentoring/Education, 10 Recovery/Wellness, 16 Ethical Responsibility)
- Pass IC&RC examination

**Administered by:** Iowa Board of Certification (IBC)

**Note:** No statutory or regulatory standards for certification - developed by third-party nonprofit (IBC). However, practice setting IS restricted.

**Citation:** Iowa Dept of Public Health guidelines; Iowa Board of Certification standards

## Summary
- **Legally defined?** YES - in administrative rules
- **Coaching vs. therapy?** Clearly distinguished as nonclinical peer support
- **Independent practice?** NO - Must work within licensed agency with IDPH contract
- **Interventionists different?** No information found

## Practical Implications
Iowa has one of the MOST RESTRICTIVE frameworks for recovery coaches:

**Cannot:**
- Practice independently
- Operate own business/LLC
- Work outside licensed agency
- Practice without agency supervision and liability coverage

**Must:**
- Be employed by or volunteer with licensed agency
- Work under agency with IDPH contract
- Be covered by agency liability insurance
- Obtain Peer Recovery Specialist credential

**Employment Settings:**
- Substance use treatment facilities
- Social service agencies
- Peer-run recovery centers
- Hospitals
- Community-based organizations
