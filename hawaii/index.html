Official Certification: Limited SUD Peer Certification Available

Important Notice

Limited SUD-Specific Certification

Current Status: Hawaii does not have a specific standalone certification for substance use disorder peer support specialists.

Working Group: A peer support specialist working group has been established under legislative guidance to address these gaps.

Available Certification

Hawaii Certified Peer Specialist (HCPS)

Provider: Adult Mental Health Division
Focus: Primarily for mental health (not standalone SUD)
Requirement: Primary diagnosis of mental illness or co-occurring disorder
Note: A single, primary diagnosis of a substance use disorder does not meet certification
Contact: AMHD Office of Consumer Affairs at 586-4688
Website: HCPS Information

Alternative Professional Credential

Certified Substance Abuse Counselor (CSAC)

Provider: Department of Health - Alcohol and Drug Abuse Division

Note: This is a professional counselor certification, not a peer support specialist credential

Website: Substance Use Professionals

Current Developments

Hawaii is actively working to address gaps in peer support certification for substance use disorders as of 2024-2025. The establishment of a working group indicates potential future changes to the certification system.

Co-Occurring Disorders

If you have a co-occurring disorder (both mental health and substance use), you may be eligible for HCPS certification. Contact the AMHD Office of Consumer Affairs for specific eligibility determination.
