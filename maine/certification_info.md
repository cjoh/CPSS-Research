## Personal Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Lived Experience:** Must be someone who has experienced challenges maintaining their mental health and/or are in recovery from substance use disorder
- **Personal Recovery:** Required to have recovery experience (specific duration not publicly specified)
- **Background:** Behavioral health peer with personal experience of mental health challenges and/or substance use disorder recovery

## Training Requirements

### Intentional Peer Support Training
- **Duration:** Eight (8) day training
- **Curriculum:** "Intentional Peer Support: An Alternative Approach" - trauma-informed curriculum developed by Shery Mead and the (former) Office of Consumer Affairs
- **Format:** In-person intensive training
- **Approach:** Uses trauma-informed, relationship-based peer support model
- **Focus:** Emphasizes mutuality, connection, and moving toward rather than away from difficult experiences

### Training Components
- Foundational peer support principles
- Intentional Peer Support methodology
- Trauma-informed care practices
- Relationship-based support skills
- Ethics and boundaries
- Cultural considerations

## Certification Process

1. Meet personal eligibility requirements (lived experience)
2. Register for upcoming CIPSS training
3. Complete 8-day Intentional Peer Support training
4. Successfully complete all training requirements
5. Pass certification requirements
6. Receive CIPSS certification
7. Maintain certification through continuing education

## Distinction from Other Credentials

### CIPSS vs. Substance Abuse Counselor Licensing
Maine has a separate licensing system for alcohol and drug counselors through the Maine Board of Alcohol & Drug Counselors:
- ADCA (Alcohol and Drug Counselor Assistant)
- CADC (Certified Alcohol and Drug Counselor)
- LADC (Licensed Alcohol and Drug Counselor)

**CIPSS is distinct from these credentials:**
- CIPSS focuses on peer support using lived experience
- Counselor credentials require different education and supervised hours
- CIPSS uses Intentional Peer Support methodology
- Different regulatory pathways and requirements

## Additional Notes

### Key Features of Maine's Program
- **Intentional Peer Support Model:** Uses specific, evidence-based IPS curriculum developed by Shery Mead
- **Trauma-Informed:** Strong emphasis on trauma-informed care and practice
- **Relationship-Based:** Focus on mutuality and authentic connection
- **Dual Population:** Serves both mental health and substance use peer support
- **University Partnership:** USM Muskie School provides administrative infrastructure
- **Statewide Recognition:** Accepted across Maine's behavioral health system
- **Healthcare Integration:** Major health systems like MaineHealth require this certification

### Important Considerations
- **8-Day Intensive:** Requires significant time commitment for training
- **Specific Methodology:** Focus on Intentional Peer Support approach (not generic peer support)
- **Registration System Change:** New portal effective March 1, 2025 - update bookmarks
- **Lived Experience Required:** Must have personal experience with behavioral health challenges
- **Employer Requirement:** Many Maine behavioral health employers specifically require CIPSS
- **Distinct from Counseling:** Different pathway from traditional counselor licensing

### Unique Aspects
- Maine's use of Intentional Peer Support as the standard model
