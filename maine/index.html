Certification Overview

A Certified Intentional Peer Support Specialist (CIPSS) in Maine is a person with lived experience of behavioral health challenges (mental health and/or substance use disorder) who has been trained in the Intentional Peer Support methodology. This evidence-based, trauma-informed approach emphasizes mutuality, connection, and moving toward rather than away from difficult experiences.

Requirements

Personal Requirements

  • Lived Experience: Must have experienced challenges maintaining mental health and/or be in recovery from substance use disorder
  • Personal Recovery: Recovery experience required (specific duration not publicly specified)
  • Willingness to Share: Must be willing to use lived experience to support others

Training Requirements

Duration: Eight (8) day intensive training

Curriculum: "Intentional Peer Support: An Alternative Approach" - trauma-informed curriculum developed by Shery Mead

Format: In-person intensive training

Topics Include: Foundational peer support principles, Intentional Peer Support methodology, trauma-informed care practices, relationship-based support skills, ethics and boundaries, and cultural considerations

Employment Settings

CIPSS-certified specialists work in various settings across Maine:

  • MaineHealth Behavioral Health: Requires CIPSS certification for peer support positions
  • Sweetser: Peer Services program employing CIPSS-certified specialists
  • Penquis: Employs Certified Intentional Peer Support Specialists
  • Community mental health centers
  • Substance use treatment programs
  • Recovery community organizations
  • Hospitals and healthcare systems

Continuing Education

Continuing education requirements are the same as provisional certification fieldwork requirements. Specific hours should be confirmed with Maine OBH. Ethics and skills training are emphasized in ongoing professional development.

Distinction from Counselor Licensing

CIPSS is distinct from Maine's alcohol and drug counselor licensing system (ADCA, CADC, LADC). CIPSS focuses on peer support using lived experience and the Intentional Peer Support methodology, while counselor credentials require different education and supervised hours with a different regulatory pathway.
