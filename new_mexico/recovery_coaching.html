Legal Status Overview

INDEPENDENT PRACTICE PROHIBITED

State-Defined Term: Certified Peer Support Worker (CPSW) / Peer Support Worker (PSW)

Regulatory Framework: Administrative Code with state certification requirement

Independent Practice: Explicitly prohibited - must work under supervision

Legal Definition

Certified Peer Support Worker (CPSW): Peer support workers who have successfully completed training with the behavioral health service division's office of peer recovery and engagement (OPRE) and have obtained certification from the New Mexico credentialing board of behavioral health professionals.

Peer Support Workers (PSW): Individuals who have been successful in their own recovery from substance use disorder who help other individuals in their recovery process through shared understanding, respect, and mutual empowerment, reducing the likelihood of relapse.

Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

New Mexico maintains clear distinctions between peer support and licensed clinical practice:

Prohibited Activities

  • Independent practice without supervision
  • Clinical treatment or therapy
  • Diagnostic assessments (clinical)
  • Clinical treatment planning
  • Practicing outside comprehensive, individualized plan of care

Permitted Activities (Under Supervision)

  • Peer-based support services
  • Sharing lived experience and recovery journey
  • Supporting recovery process
  • Reducing relapse likelihood through peer support
  • Mutual empowerment activities

Independent Practice Requirements

Critical Limitation

Recovery coaches/peer support workers in New Mexico do NOT have independent practice authority. They MUST work under supervision.

This contrasts with licensed clinical mental health counselors (LPCC) or licensed professional art therapists (LPAT) who may practice independently without supervision once licensed.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Must be self-identified consumers in recovery from mental illness or substance use disorder
  • Must be supervised by independent practitioner OR someone trained and certified to supervise peers
  • Services must be coordinated within context of comprehensive, individualized plan of care with specific goals

Supervision Requirements

Peer support workers MUST be supervised by:

  • An independent practitioner, OR
  • Someone trained and certified to supervise peers

Important: Services are only reimbursable when provided under proper supervision.

Scope of Practice

Service Context

  • Peer support workers are NOT independent practitioners
  • Provide peer-based support services, not clinical treatment
  • Must operate within comprehensive, individualized plan of care
  • Work under supervision structure

Important Considerations

Key Restrictions

  • Cannot practice independently - supervision always required
  • Cannot operate private practice without affiliated supervision
  • Must work within comprehensive treatment plan context
  • Different from licensed professionals who CAN practice independently after full licensure
