Official Certification: Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS)

Certification Overview

Arkansas Department of Human Services, Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services (OSAMH) directs the Peer Specialist certification and placement process. PRSS are trained specifically in substance use recovery support.

Certifying Body

Arkansas DHS - OSAMH
Website: Arkansas Peer Recovery

NAADAC (Application Processing)
Website: naadac.org/arkansas-peer-specialist-program

Three-Tier Certification System

Tier 1: Core Peer Recovery Specialist

Core Training: 30 hours | Work as Peer in Training (PIT)

Tier 2: Advanced Certification

Additional Education: 16 hours | Experience: 500 documented hours

Tier 3: Peer Recovery Supervisor

Enables advancement and supervision of other peers

Requirements

  • Minimum Recovery Time: 2 years of sustainable recovery (non-substance use)
  • Must self-identify as being in recovery
  • High school diploma or GED recommended
  • Background check required

Core Training Curriculum (30 hours)

  • Science of substance use disorder
  • Various pathways to recovery
  • Five Stages of the Recovery Process
  • Trauma-Informed Care
  • Motivational Interviewing techniques
  • Professional ethics and boundaries
  • Cultural competency and self-care

Application Portal

Website: www.ar.gov/recovery

Applications processed on rolling basis through NAADAC Arkansas Peer Specialist Program

Training Coordination

UAMS MIDSOUTH MHTTC
Website: midsouth.ualr.edu/daabhs
Coordinates peer recovery training for Arkansas

Application Process

  1. Apply online at www.ar.gov/recovery
  2. Become Peer in Training (PIT) upon acceptance
  3. Complete Core training (30 hours)
  4. Receive Core Peer Recovery Specialist certification
  5. Complete additional 16 hours + 500 supervised experience hours for Tier 2
  6. Optional: Pursue Peer Recovery Supervisor (Tier 3)

Employment & Reimbursement

Peer recovery support services may be Medicaid billable. Employment opportunities in substance use disorder treatment facilities, community mental health centers, recovery community organizations, hospital-based programs, correctional facilities and reentry programs, and community-based organizations.

Career Pathway

  • Entry Level: Core Peer Recovery Specialist (30-hour training)
  • Mid-Level: Advanced certification (16 additional hours + 500 experience hours)
  • Advanced: Peer Recovery Supervisor (supervise other peer specialists)
