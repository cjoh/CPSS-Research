# Arkansas Peer Support Specialist Certification for Addiction/Substance Use Disorder

## Official Certification Name
**Peer Recovery Support Specialist (PRSS)** - for substance use recovery
(Separate from Peer Support Specialist which is for mental health recovery)

## Certifying Body
**Arkansas Department of Human Services (DHS)**
- Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services (OSAMH)
- Website: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/
- Arkansas Peer Recovery: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/divisions-shared-services/shared-services/office-of-substance-abuse-and-mental-health/arkansas-peer-recovery/

**NAADAC (The Association for Addiction Professionals)**
- Administers Arkansas Peer Specialist Program
- Handles applications and certification process
- Website: https://www.naadac.org/arkansas-peer-specialist-program

## Certification Overview
The Arkansas Department of Human Services, Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services directs the Peer Specialist certification and placement process. Peer Recovery Support Specialists are trained specifically in substance use recovery support, while Peer Support Specialists are trained for mental health recovery support.

## Detailed Requirements

### Personal Recovery Requirements
- **Minimum Recovery Time**: At least 2 years of sustainable recovery (non-substance use)
- Must self-identify as being in recovery
- Willing to use lived experience to support others
- Willing to share personal recovery story for the benefit of others

### Educational Requirements
- High school diploma or GED recommended
- Ability to complete required training hours
- Strong reading, writing, and communication skills

### Background Check
- Background check is required
- Must pass criminal background screening
- Details available through application process

## Training Requirements

### Three-Tier Certification System

Arkansas has three levels of Peer Recovery Support certification:

#### Tier 1: Core Peer Recovery Specialist
- **Core Training**: 30 hours
- Initial certification level
- Work as Peer in Training (PIT) during this phase

#### Tier 2: Advanced Certification
After Core certification, must complete:
- **Additional Education Hours**: 16 hours
- **Experience Hours**: 500 documented hours

#### Tier 3: Peer Recovery Supervisor
- Enables advancement and supervision of other peers
- Additional training and experience required
- Contact OSAMH for specific requirements

### Core Training Curriculum (30 hours)

**Topics Include:**
- Science of substance use disorder
- Various pathways to recovery
- The Five Stages of the Recovery Process
- Trauma-Informed Care
- Motivational Interviewing techniques
- Peer support best practices
- Professional ethics and boundaries
- Documentation and recordkeeping
- Cultural competency
- Self-care and wellness

## Application and Certification Process

### Step-by-Step Process

**1. Meet Eligibility Requirements**
   - Verify 2+ years continuous recovery
   - Prepare personal recovery statement
   - Obtain any necessary documentation

**2. Apply for Core Training**
   - Visit www.ar.gov/recovery
   - Complete online application
   - Application reviewed by NAADAC Arkansas
   - May require interview or additional information

**3. Become Peer in Training (PIT)**
   - Upon acceptance, designated as PIT
   - Complete Core training (30 hours)
   - Learn foundational peer support skills

**4. Complete Core Training and Certification**
   - Attend all training sessions
   - Pass required assessments
   - Receive Core Peer Recovery Specialist certification

**5. Advance to Tier 2**
   - Complete additional 16 education hours
   - Work 500 supervised experience hours
   - Document all hours appropriately
   - Apply for advanced certification

**6. Optional: Pursue Peer Recovery Supervisor (Tier 3)**
   - Gain significant peer support experience
   - Complete supervisor-specific training
   - Apply for supervisor certification

## How to Become a Certified Training Provider

To become an approved training provider in Arkansas:

### Step 1: Contact Arkansas DHS-OSAMH
- **Office**: Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services
- **Website**: https://humanservices.arkansas.gov/divisions-shared-services/shared-services/office-of-substance-abuse-and-mental-health/arkansas-peer-recovery/
- Inquire about training provider approval process

### Step 2: Review Requirements
- DHS-OSAMH, in collaboration with the DHS-approved accrediting entity, administers and monitors Arkansas Peer Recovery Program (APRP)
- Must use approved curriculum (Appalachian Model)
- Meet state quality standards

### Step 3: Submit Application
- Provide organization information
- Submit training curriculum
- Include instructor qualifications
- Describe quality assurance measures

### Step 4: Coordinate with NAADAC
- NAADAC handles application processing
- Training providers must coordinate with NAADAC system
- Ensure training hours are properly documented

### Step 5: Receive Approval
- DHS-OSAMH approves training program
- Begin offering certified training
- Report completions to state system
